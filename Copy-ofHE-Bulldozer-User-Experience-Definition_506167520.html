VARLab : Copy ofHE-Bulldozer User Experience Definition

Created by Stacey Dineen (Unlicensed) on Aug 04, 2022

Initial View and Welcome

On initial load, the camera will approach the vehicle in a cinematic fashion as though the student is walking towards it from a distance of approx 20-40m. This is from the student's point of view, at approx eye level, lasting no more than a few seconds to help orient the learner to the simulation environment.

The voiceover will welcome the student and advise them to follow the prompts to practice a walk-around inspection of the bulldozer. Students will be prompted to observe each inspectable item in order.

Camera Behaviour and Navigation

The HE-Bulldozer simulation will primarily be a Guided Mode style of learner interaction, where the camera will move automatically for each inspection, there will be no need for the learner to navigate manually using either keyboard or mouse. The Guided Mode inspection walks the student through the inspection process so that the student does not decide what to inspect or in what order. For certain views, we will consider the optional use of camera pan and zoom, but the objective will always be to ensure that the learner can see whatever they need to inspect without fiddling around with the camera.

Interaction Model

TBD - With the inclusion of multi-media visual experiences for the learner, it is unclear if our traditional user interaction for inspections will be appropriate.

Traditionally, we have shown the user a visual representation (a 3D model) of the inspectable component, and asked them to determine if the component was good or bad (and to specify the type of “bad” state shown). This method requires intricately detailed models, sometimes involving several different models of the exact same component to represent the good and bad states. Damages had to sometimes be exaggerated, even to an unrealistic extent, in order to be reasonably noticeable to the learner.

In this new mixed-media simulation, we might consider a totally different approach to teaching the pre-op inspection process, with a more comprehensive view. For example, we could consider the popularity of the Results Panel design that was used for Trucking Pre-Trip Inspection. That panel shows the GOOD state of the selected component as well as ALL OF THE BAD STATES. This helps the learner to identify the key differences between the good and bad states for the selected inspectable component, and can use photos, still shots captured from video footage, or even illustrations or diagrams to get the point across.

Another area for consideration is the learner feedback model for this inspection. Our e-learning experts, Lisa and our other resources in eLC, can help direct us toward the most effective model for providing feedback and redirection for learners.

Progress and Feedback

We may consider reuse of the Progress Checklist and Results Panel that were used in the Trucking Pre-Trip Inspection simulation:

Progress Checklist - The primary function of the Progress Checklist is to help the student identify what they have already completed in their inspection workflow, and what is left to be done. The Progress Checklist does not indicate if answers are correct or incorrect, and does not need to provide any details about what damages have been reported. The student is free to revisit any part of the inspection at any time, so they can review/change their answers by simply revisiting a POI.

Results Panel - Sometimes the inspection workflow does not provide any immediate feedback to the learner about their selected answer for any of the inspection tasks, so the Results Panel is the primary way for the learner to see their results. Other times, feedback is provided in real time and the Results Panel is a summary view of all feedback up to this point. On demand (usually a menu button), the student will be shown a complete list of every inspection task (these may be organized in a familiar way that is consistent with the Progress Checklist, to help the student find what they are looking for) where each task will be given one of THREE results:

  • Green checkmark - This task was completed successfully. The selected answer is the correct answer.

  • Red X - This task was completed, but the answer selected was incorrect. The correct answer will be provided at this time.

  • Incomplete - This result will be assigned to any inspection POI that was NOT VISITED by the student. This is an important distinction because we want the student to get a different result when they missed/forgot a step in the inspection, even if there was no damage present at that POI in this case.

Position #1 - Drivers Side Front 1 (DSF1)

The camera will stop at the front driver's side corner of the vehicle, angled so that the camera is looking at most of the front and driver's side of the truck.  This will be called Position DSF. From here, the student will be prompted by the voiceover to inspect items that are in this immediate area.

  1. Fluid Leaks (note if present) - visible from this vantage point

  2. Exterior Condition (dents, abrasions) - front bumper, grille, hood, drivers side cab and cab door.

  3. Windows (damaged, dirty) - windshield or drivers side cab door

After completing all of the tasks at the DSF1 position, the learner will click on Continue to move to the next inspection point.  In Guided Mode, if the learner tries to move on without identifying a damaged component, they will be unable to leave and will be prompted to complete what has been left undone.  If all inspection tasks have been completed, they will move to the next inspection point.

Position #2 - PS Engine Compartment - Cold Check (ENG1)

The camera will stop at the driver's side door, the door will swing open and the student will be prompted to insert the key into the ignition. They do NOT turn the key or enter the cab of the truck - the key is inserted so that the hood can be opened.  After the key is inserted into the ignition, the driver’s side hood latch will be pulled and the camera will follow around the front of the truck to the passenger side hood latch, which will also be pulled. The hood will swing open and the camera will move to the passenger side of the engine compartment, looking at the engine compartment at standing height. This will be called Position ENG1. From here, the student will be prompted by the voiceover to inspect items that are in this immediate area.

  1. General overview - check for evidence of leaking fluids anywhere, debris (tools/flashlights left behind, garbage such as plastic caps or empty containers from refilling fluids, broken hose clamps, gloves) or any otherwise unlisted damage (cracked hood joints or bushings, airbox damage, anything loose or excessively rusted.)

  2. Hoses - hoses may be loose or disconnected when hose clamps fail, they may split or crack, especially where they are bent or flexed.

  3. Wiring - wires may become disconnected, damaged, unbundled/loose.

  4. Washer fluid level - Only the neck of the washer fluid reservoir is visible at position ENG1 - fluid level is checked by observing the level of sky-blue coloured fluid in the translucent white plastic reservoir filler neck.

  5. Passenger side steering linkage - intact, not loose, not bent/cracked or excessively rusted

  6. Passenger side steer tire brakes and suspension - inspect brake chamber, springs, bushings.

After completing all of the tasks at the ENG1 position, the learner will click on Continue to move to the next inspection point.  In Guided Mode, if the learner tries to move on without identifying a damaged component, they will be unable to leave and will be prompted to complete what has been left undone.  If all inspection tasks have been completed, they will move to the next inspection point.

Position #4 - Cab Interior 1 (CAB1)

The voiceover will prompt the student to enter the truck cab. The camera will follow as the student climbs the steps outside the driver’s side door, the door will swing automatically open and the camera will pan briefly and smoothly across the forward cab (there is no need to explore the sleeper cabin, it is just in the background). This cinematic view of the interior should take no longer than 2-3 seconds, and is intended to give a quick overview of the cab interior before focusing in on the first inspection task. The camera will stop with a driver’s POV as if sitting in the driver’s seat, looking out the front windshield. The voiceover will prompt the student to complete tasks in the immediate area of this position.

  1. Parking brakes (always works) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION. Note that this is not an inspection task, but is required to proceed with the inspection. The parking brake controls are two large plastic buttons on the dashboard. The colour and shape of these buttons are standardized for all trucks, so it is important that model exactly match the reference photos (see Inspection 15 for a good front view). The student must verify that the brakes are applied by checking to ensure that BOTH buttons are pulled OUT fully. The student must not release the brakes and reapply - they must verify that the brakes are applied. If the either of the brakes are not applied (button is pushed in) they must pull the button to ensure the parking brake is applied before they proceed with the inspection.

  2. Steering mechanism (excessive play) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION (student is prompted to click the steering wheel icon) Animation shows the steering wheel turning easily a SHORT distance (approx 10cm or less) in each direction, indicating proper functioning of the steering mechanism. Alternatively, the animation shows the steering wheel moving a LARGE distance (approx 20+cm or 1/4 the total circumference) easily with no resistance, indicating excessive “play” or “lash” that points to a serious maintenance issue with the steering mechanism. This will be difficult to show clearly, so a toast/voiceover hint will help the student recognize when the steering is too loose, “The steering wheel feels very loose, it can easily be turned more than 1/4 of the way in either direction without any resistance” or “The steering wheel can be turned only a little before encountering resistance”

  3. Dashboard gauges (malfunctioning) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION (student is prompted to turn the key halfway without starting the engine) Turning the key halfway will initialize the dashboard instrument panel and cause all of the gauges to “sweep”. Any gauge that does not move at all, has a missing needle, or is fluctuating wildly can be selected at this time and marked as malfunctioning. Dashboard warning lights will all briefly light up (simultaneously, for about 1-2 seconds) and then go out. The warning lights are not part of this stage of the inspection, but we will demonstrate the normal function of the dashboard so that students become familiar with this normal behaviour.

  4. Activate Test Lights and Trailer Brake aka The Spike (always works) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION - Note that this is not an inspection task, but is required to proceed with the exterior inspection. Voiceover will prompt the student to click on the Test Lights button and the Trailer Brake lever to activate the exterior lights. Note that the dashboard indicators will blink in time with the exterior lights, so this will be visible on the dashboard instrument panel.

  5. Brake pedal (loss of brake pedal pressure) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION - The voiceover will prompt the student to put their foot on the brake pedal. If the pedal offers no resistance and sinks easily right to the floor with a thump, there is a loss of brake pedal pressure. When functioning correctly, the brake pedal should offer firm counterpressure when pressed, it should only go about 75% of the way to the floor. Voiceover and toast messages may be used to help demonstrate the condition of the brake pedal, such as “Whoa, this pedal feels really soft, there is hardly any resistance and it goes all the way to the floor”. The student must keep their foot on the brake pedal in order to inspect the gear shift and start the engine, regardless if it is operating properly or not.

  6. Gear shift (left in gear) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION - The student must verify that the gear shift position and the dashboard display show that the truck is in Neutral gear (N is lit up on the transmission indicator). If the truck is not in Neutral, the student must move the gear shift to put the truck in Neutral gear before they can start the engine.

  7. Start the engine (always works) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION - Note that this is not an inspection task, but is required to proceed with the instrumentation check later in the inspection process.

  8. Activate the windshield defroster - PHYSICAL INSPECTION - Note that this is not an inspection task at this time, but we need to start the defroster now so that we can check it later in this inspection.

After completing all of the tasks at the CAB1 position, the learner will click on Continue to move to the next inspection point.  In Guided Mode, if the learner tries to move on without identifying a damaged component, they will be unable to leave and will be prompted to complete what has been left undone.  If all inspection tasks have been completed, they will move to the next inspection point.

Position #5 - Drivers Side Front 2 (DSF2)

The camera will follow as the student exits the cab of the truck, door will swing automatically open and the student will descend to the ground, returning to the original camera position from DSF1. From this position, the voiceover will prompt the student to continue the exterior 360 inspection of the truck and trailer.

  1. Exterior Lights (damaged, burnt out) - Note that this is now a VISUAL inspection, because the student activated the Test Lights and Trailer Brake in Position CAB1.

    1. Headlights and fog lights

    2. Indicator lights

    3. Front license plate light

    4. Marker lights

  2. Mirrors (damaged, loose, missing) - drivers side hood and door mirrors

  3. Wheels/Tires - drivers side steer tire - VISUAL and PHYSICAL INSPECTIONS (specified below)

    1. Sidewall/Rim/Lug Nuts- VISUAL - Camera automatically moves to rim and sidewall view: Rim (cracked, dented), lug nuts (loose), tire sidewall (cracked)

    2. Tread - VISUAL - Camera automatically moves to tire tread view near the ground at the front of the DS steer tire: Steer tire tread (excessive or uneven wear, embedded object)

    3. Mallet icon - PHYSICAL INSPECTION - The camera automatically moves to an oblique angle showing both tread and sidewall of the DS steer tire. Voiceover prompts the student to click the tire pressure icon. When clicked, the mallet animation plays to demonstrate a properly inflated or underinflated tire.

After completing all of the tasks at the DSF2 position, the learner will click on Continue to move to the next inspection point.  In Guided Mode, if the learner tries to move on without identifying a damaged component, they will be unable to leave and will be prompted to complete what has been left undone.  If all inspection tasks have been completed, they will move to the next inspection point.

Position #6 - Driver’s Side Cab (DSC)

The camera will stop at the driver's side of the vehicle, around the fifth wheel, angled so that the learner is looking directly at the side of the vehicle. This will be called Position DSC. From here, the student will be prompted via voiceover to inspect items that are in this immediate area: 

  1. Fluid Leaks (note if present) - visible from this vantage point

  2. Exterior Condition (dents, abrasions) - driver’s side cab or front/side of trailer

  3. Exterior Lights (damaged, burnt out)

    1. Marker lights

    2. Indicator light-DS - located high up on the side of the cab body

  4. Fuel tank (dents, leaking, straps) - drivers side tank - ?? May require physical inspection?

  5. DEF tank (leaking) and battery (connections, damage) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION (click Battery icon to open access panel to inspect)

  6. Wheels/Tires - drivers side rear tractor tires aka drive tires - 2 sets of double tires - VISUAL and PHYSICAL INSPECTIONS (specified below)

    1. Sidewall/Rim/Lug Nuts- VISUAL - Camera automatically moves to rim and sidewall view of outer tire: Rim (cracked, dented), lug nuts (loose), tire sidewall (cracked)

    2. Tread - VISUAL - Camera automatically moves to tire tread view so that treads of both tires are visible in the same view: Rear tractor tire tread (excessive or uneven wear, embedded object)

    3. Mallet icon - PHYSICAL INSPECTION - The camera automatically moves to an oblique angle showing both tread and sidewall of each double set of rear tractor tires. Voiceover prompts the student to click the tire pressure icon. When clicked, the mallet animation plays to demonstrate a properly inflated or underinflated tire. Note that only one of any double set of tires will be underinflated, to promote comparison between good and bad tires when learning to recognize the signs of underinflation.

  7. Behind Tire - driver's side rear tractor tires aka drive tires. Camera moves automatically behind the tire to look at the inside aspect of the wheel and brake components. This view will be under the trailer, so supplemental lighting will be needed. Note that the voiceover will prompt the student to perform a visual inspection of the brake chambers, lines, and drums, there is no inspection task for the brake drums and they will never show any damage in this inspection. Note that the student will need to inspect brake components for both the forward and rear axles in the same view (looking from behind the rear set of drive tires toward the front of the truck). Both brake chambers and lines must be visible and inspectable in this view for the driver’s side rear tractor tires.

    1. Brake chambers (cracked, loose)

    2. Brake hoses (leaking, damaged)

    3. Axle seals (leaking)

  8. Fifth wheel coupling (pin and handle not correct position, coupling “jaw” not secure)

  9. Exterior Lights - Rear Tractor (damaged, burnt out) - rear tractor lights can only be seen while underneath the trailer, so we inspect these in the same view where we are inspecting the fifth wheel

    1. Brake lights

    2. Reverse lights

    3. Rear tractor license plate light

  10. DS Dolly leg (leg not raised, handle not secured)

  11. Air line and power cable connections (loose power cable, damaged power cable, leaking air line, damaged air line)

After completing all of the tasks at the DSC position, the learner will click on Continue to move to the next inspection point.  In Guided Mode, if the learner tries to move on without identifying a damaged component, they will be unable to leave and will be prompted to complete what has been left undone.  If all inspection tasks have been completed, they will move to the next inspection point.

Position #12 - PS Engine Compartment - Warm Check (ENG1B)

The camera will stop at the passenger side of the engine compartment, looking at the engine compartment at standing height. This is the same camera view that was used in Position ENG1, but this time the engine is running. From here, the student will be prompted by the voiceover to inspect items that are in this immediate area.

  1. General overview - check for evidence of leaking fluids dripping. Some fluids may only drip when the engine is running because the fluid is under pressure.

  2. Hoses - Air leaks may only be detectable when the engine is running, causing a loud hissing sound.

  3. Wiring - wires may arc (causing sparks) when the engine is running

  4. Engine sound - a good engine will sound smooth and constant. Student must be prompted to listen for sounds of grinding, rattling, and squealing, which indicate mechanical issues.

After completing all of the tasks at the ENG1B position, the learner will click on Continue to move to the next inspection point.  In Guided Mode, if the learner tries to move on without identifying a damaged component, they will be unable to leave and will be prompted to complete what has been left undone.  If all inspection tasks have been completed, they will move to the next inspection point.

From here, the student will be prompted by the voiceover to inspect items that are in this immediate area.

  1. General overview - check for evidence of leaking fluids dripping. Some fluids may only drip when the engine is running because the fluid is under pressure.

  2. Hoses - Air leaks may only be detectable when the engine is running, causing a loud hissing sound.

  3. Wiring - wires may arc (causing sparks) when the engine is running

  4. Engine sound - a good engine will sound smooth and constant. Student must be prompted to listen for sounds of grinding, rattling, and squealing, which indicate mechanical issues.

After completing all of the tasks at the ENG2B position, the learner will click on Continue to close the hood of the truck and move on to the next inspection point.  In Guided Mode, if the learner tries to move on without identifying a damaged component, they will be unable to leave and will be prompted to complete what has been left undone.  If all inspection tasks have been completed, they will move to the next inspection point.

Position #14 - Cab Entry Door (CED)

The camera will move around the front of the truck and follow up the stairs at the driver’s side cab door, but will stop at the top of the steps, looking into the cab at the driver’s seat from the doorway. From here, the student will be prompted via voiceover to inspect items that are in this immediate area: 

  1. Fire extinguisher (missing, non-functional) - located at the base of the driver’s seat and easily accessible with the driver’s door open. If it is missing, an empty bracket must be shown. If non-functional, the pressure gauge on the extinguisher must show in the red.

  2. Safety triangles (missing 1 or more of the set of 3) - located on the floor of the truck near or just behind the base of the driver’s seat.

  3. Seatbelt (damaged, malfunctioning) - this will be inspected with the student standing beside the empty driver’s seat, so that they can view the seatbelt, clasp, and release. If the belt is damaged it should be torn or the clasp visibly damaged. If the seatbelt is malfunctioning, the belt cannot be fully extended to reach the clasp, or the clasp cannot be clicked shut, or the seatbelt is not released when the release button is pressed.

  4. First Aid kit (missing) - located on a storage shelf in the sleep cab interior, easily visible from the CED camera vantage point. NOTE: “missing” is a fake bad state. The first aid kit will always be present and easy to find, it will always be in the same location. We are not trying to make the student search the truck for the first aid kit, this is just a quick verification that it is present. There is no need to open it or verify contents.

After completing all of the tasks at the CED position, the learner will click on Continue to move to the next inspection point.  In Guided Mode, if the learner tries to move on without identifying a damaged component, they will be unable to leave and will be prompted to complete what has been left undone.  If all inspection tasks have been completed, they will move to the next inspection point.

Position #15 - Cab Interior 2 (CAB2)

The camera will continue to enter the cab of the truck and move into the same driver’s seat viewpoint that was used in position CAB1. The voiceover will prompt the student to inspect items that are in this immediate area:

  1. Dashboard gauges - needles should be yellow or orange for better visibility on the black dashboard.

    1. Oil pressure (low) - if gauge shows 0-10 PSI the needle will be in the red zone, indicating the oil pressure is too low. Anything over 10 PSI is fine, even if needle is at the maximum end of the gauge. There is no such thing as oil pressure being too high.

    2. Fuel gauge (low, critically low) - gauge reading between 1/4 and 1/2 tank is low, less than 1/4 tank is critically low.

    3. DEF gauge (low, critically low) - same as fuel gauge for low and critically low.

    4. Air pressure gauge (low, high) - there are TWO air pressure gauges because there are two separate tanks. The tanks fill in sequence, so it is normal for Air Pressure 1 to read higher than (while filling) or the same as (after filling) Air Pressure 2. If either tank reads 50 PSI or below, that is “air pressure low”. If either tank reads 125 PSI or above, that is “air pressure high”. Both the high and low zones of the gauge should be red.

    5. Volt meter (low) - this can be analog or digital. Reading of less than 12 is “low”

    6. Warning lights (stay on) - all warning lights come on briefly when the instrument panel is initialized. They will take anywhere from 1-30 seconds to go out after being initialized. Warning lights that remain on by the time the student has completed the exterior inspection should be noted on the inspection results checklist.

  2. Windshield wipers and washer (comes on when activated, cleans windshield) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION (click the Wipers icon)

  3. Heater and defroster (activated previously) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION (click the Heater icon) This one is tricky because it isn’t enough for the defroster vents to blow, the student needs to verify that the air coming from the vent is warm. We can animate a hand that reaches over the dashboard to feel the air that is blowing from the defroster vents, and use a toast/voiceover hint to indicate “The vents are blowing cold air, the defroster isn’t working” or “The vents are blowing nice warm air, this seems to be working well”.

  4. Air Horn (pull cable) and City Horn (press centre of steering wheel) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION (click the Horn icons)

After completing all of the tasks at the CAB2 position, the learner will click on Continue to complete the inspection. The voiceover will congratulate the learner on completing the inspection successfully, and the inspection checklist will be shown. Note that the inspection checklist is available for viewing at any point during the inspection simulation, just by clicking on the checklist icon in the bottom toolbar.
