VARLab : Requirements Review

Created by Jacob Nelson (Unlicensed), last modified by Balazs Karner on Sept 21, 2021

Purpose

To verify if the simulation created meets the requirements set out by the Content Expert. This document lists various requirements and if they were met.

Shop Safety Inspection

7

Students should be placed in a 3D environment that resembles an automotive workshop. This allows the student to be immersed in the learning experience and get an understanding of how a typical workshop would be laid out, with emergency and fire safety equipment, shop equipment, and materials.

As a student I want the environment to resemble an automotive workshop so that I can be immersed in the learning experience.

The workshop has been modelled to exactly reflect that at the Guelph Campus. This includes all the fire equipment which has been placed to reflect reality.

8

Students must identify the locations of emergency and fire safety equipment in the automotive shop. Emergency and fire safety equipment includes:

  • fire extinguishers

  • fire exits

  • fire alarm pulls

  • emergency phone

  • eye-wash station

As a student I want to locate emergency equipment in the automotive workshop so I can become familiar with common locations of this equipment.

As a student I want to understand the benefits of having an emergency phone in the auto shop.

Students are tested on locating all modeled emergency equipment. They also have to learn how to operate the emergency phone and why this is important.

9

Students should understand what types of fire extinguishers are found in the automotive shop.

As an instructor I want students to know which types of extinguishers are used in the automotive shop.

The notepad explains to the user the type of extinguisher present.

10

Students must inspect the condition of fire extinguishers in the automotive shop.

As a student I want to inspect the condition of fire extinguishers in the automotive shop so that I can determine if they are safe for use.

Students are able to inspect both working and non-working models of the fire extinguishers for defects. If defects exist the notepad appears explaining to the user what those defects are.

11

Students must identify potential hazards in the automotive shop.

As a student I want to identify spills and tripping hazards in the automotive shop environment.

Students can identify chemical spills, misplaced objects, and wire tripping hazards. They can also click on these elements to fix the hazard.

12

Students must inspect the condition of bench grinders in the automotive shop.

As a student I want to inspect the condition of bench grinders in the auto shop so that I can determine if they are safe for use.

Students inspect the bench grinders for lens clarity, wheel cracking, improper spin, loose tool rests, and any other physical damage.

13

Students must inspect the parts washer in the automotive shop to determine the chemical used, WHMIS/GHS labels, and ensure the lid is functional.

As a student I want to be able to read the GHS workplace label on the parts washer and parts washer chemicals to be aware of safety considerations for their use.

As a student I want to be able to open and close the lid of the parts washer to determine that the lid is functional.

As a student I want to understand that leaving the parts washer lid open is a fire hazard

Students can click on the lid of the parts washer to see if it works. They can also click on the WHIMIS label to view the chemical information.

Hoist Mechanics

15

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, user must be able to press and hold on the “up” button on the control panel.

As a student I want to be able to press and hold the “up” button on the control panel

Pressing and holding the UP button causes the hoist to raise.

16

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, the hoist arms must move upward when the “up” button is pressed and held.

As a student I want the vehicle hoist arms to move upward when the “up” button on the control panel is pressed and held

Pressing and holding the UP button causes the hoist to raise.

17

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, user must be able to move the vertical lock knob up and down.

As a student I want to be able to move the vertical lock knob up and down

Vertical lock lever moves up and down.

18

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, the vertical locks must be engaged when the vertical lock knob is in the “up” position

As a student I want the vertical lock mechanism to be engaged when the vertical lock knob is in the “up” position

When the vertical lock lever is at 90 degrees the vertical locks are engaged.

19

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, the vertical locks must be disengaged when the vertical lock knob is in the “down” position

As a student I want the vertical lock mechanism to be disengaged when the vertical lock knob is in the “down” position

When the vertical lock lever is at 45 degrees the vertical locks are disengaged.

20

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, the user must be able to click and drag up on the horizontal lock mechanism to disengage the lock

As a student I want to be able to pull up on the horizontal lock mechanism to disengage the lock

To disengage horizontal locks the student must click the lock, no dragging required.

21

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, when the user releases their click on the horizontal lock, the lock must engage

As a student I want to be able to release the horizontal lock mechanism to engage the lock

The horizontal locks can be engaged by clicking on the lock.

22

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, when the horizontal lock is disengaged, the hoist arm must be able to move freely

As a student I want the vehicle hoist arm to move freely when the corresponding horizontal lock is disengaged

When the horizontal locks are disengaged the hoist arms can be moved.

23

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, when the horizontal lock is engaged, the hoist arm must not move horizontally

As a student I want the vehicle hoist arm to stay in place when the horizontal lock is engaged

When the horizontal locks are engaged the hoist arms cannot be moved.

24

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, the user must be able to click and drag on the hoist arms to extend or retract their length

As a student I want to be able to extend or retract the lengths of the hoist arms

Students can extend or retract the hoist arms by clicking on them and dragging.

25

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, the user must be able to click on the height cut-off switch to engage the switch

As a student I want to be able to interact with the height cut-off switch

When the height cut off bar is depressed (via click) the hoist arms stop moving upwards.

26

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, when the height cut-off switch is engaged the vehicle hoist arms must not move

As a student I want the hydraulic host to not move when the height cut-off switch is engaged

When the height cut off bar is depressed (via click) the hoist arms stop moving upwards.

27

User must be able to engage the hydraulic vehicle hoist and the height cut-off switch at the same time to test working condition of the cut-off switch

As a student I want to be able to engage the hoist and the height cut-off switch at the same time to test the working condition of the cut-off switch

Students can use the keyboard to raise the hoist, then their cursor to click the Height Cut-off Bar and stop the upwards movement.

28

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, user must be able to press and hold on the release handle

As a student I want to be able to interact with the hydraulic release knob

Students can click on the hydraulic dump valve.

29

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, when the release handle is pressed and the vertical locks are disengaged the hoist arms must lower until they reach the ground

As a student I want the vehicle hoist arms to lower to the ground when the hydraulic release handle is engaged and the vertical locks are disengaged

When pressing and holding the hydraulic dump valve the arms lower.

30

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, when the release handle is pressed and the vertical locks are engaged the hoist arms must lower until they reach a vertical lock

As a student I want the vehicle hoist arms to lower but stop at a vertical lock when the hydraulic release handle is engaged and the vertical locks are engaged

The hoist arms stop at the next engaged vertical lock.

31

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, both hoist arm assemblies must lower uniformly when the release handle is engaged

As a student I want both hoist arm assemblies to lower at the same time when the release handle is engaged

When the hydraulic dump valve is depressed the arms lower uniformly.

Hoist Inspection

33

System must be able to display leakage of hydraulic fluid

As a student I want to see a visual indication of any hydraulic fluid leaking from the vehicle hoist

If the fluid reservoir is in the “Leaking” bad state, then a dripping animation is played and a hydraulic fluid puddle is visible below

34

User must be able to identify any leakage of hydraulic fluid from the hydraulic controls

As a student I want to be able to identify and make note of any fluid leaking from the hydraulic controls

The fluid reservoir is now inspectable on the hydraulic hoist with 2 states, “Intact”, and “Leaking”

35

User must be able to identify any damages to equalizing cables

As a student I want to be able to identify and make note of any damages to the equalizing cables

Students can inspect and report damaged cables.

36

User must be able to identify any cracks or damages in the hoist arms

As a student I want to be able to identify and make note of cracks or damages in the hoist arms

The hoist arms have a “Cracked” bad state

37

User must be able to identify incorrect horizontal swing function of the hoist arms

As a student I want to be able to identify and make note of incorrect horizontal swing function of the hoist arms

If the horizontal lock is in the, “Stuck” state then incorrect horizontal swing function is demonstrated. Additionally, there is a disaster scenario with broken horizontal locks that demonstrate to students what will happen to a vehicle on the hoist with stuck horizontal locks

38

User must be able to identify incorrect telescoping behaviour of the hoist arms

As a student I want to be able to identify and make note of incorrect telescoping behaviour of the hoist arms

One bad state for the hoist arms is, “Stuck Extension”. This demonstrated incorrect telescoping behaviour

39

User must be able to identify malfunction of the height cut-off switch

As a student I want to be able to identify and make note of malfunction of the height cut-off switch

Students can identify and report a non functional Height Cut-off Bar.

40

User must be able to identify non-uniform movement of the hoist arms

As a student I want to be able to identify and make note of non-uniform movement of the hoist arms

If one of the vertical locks are in the “Stuck” state, then the hoist arms will move in a non-uniform way. Additionally, there is a disaster scenario with a vertical lock that is broken. This demonstrates to the student what will happen to a vehicle on the hoist

41

User must be able to identify malfunctions in the vertical locking mechanism

As a student I want to be able to identify and make note of malfunctions in the vertical locking mechanism

Students can identify and report a broken vertical lock. This is done by lowering hoist arms and seeing if they both stop uniformly at the next vertical lock.

42

For standard operation of the vehicle hoist, hoist pads must be seated in the slots on hoist arms

As a student I want to be able to inspect the hoist pads to ensure they are seated in the slots on the hoist arms

Students can inspect and report hoist pads for deficiencies such as improper seating or missing pads.

43

User must be able to move and rotate a hoist pad to properly seat it in the slot on the hoist arm

As a student I want to be able to adjust the position of the hoist pads to properly seat them in the slots on the hoist arms

INCOMPLETE students can inspect and report pads for deficiencies but they can not seat hoist pads.

Post-Inspection Demonstrations

45

To test proper functionality of the hoist system, user must have the opportunity to lift a vehicle (or prototype object of vehicle size), where the vehicle has gravity acting upon it

As a student I want the opportunity to lift a vehicle with the vehicle hoist and observe the behaviour

In the post inspection demos students can raise a car and gravity acts upon it.

46

For the post-inspection demo scenarios, the student will be presented with a hoist with a vehicle correctly positioned on the hoist pads. The user will engage the hydraulic controls to lift and lower the vehicle.

As a student I want a vehicle to be positioned at the hoist ready for lifting without my input, after selecting a post-inspection demo scenario

Students are presented with a hoist with a vehicle included. They can use this simulation to see how a hoist should properly work or how it can fail.

47

The student is required to participate in all of the post-inspection demo scenarios.

As an instructor I want students to complete all of the post-inspection demo scenarios presented to them.

Students must complete all post inspection simulations before they can quit. Completed simulations are displayed with the button to select greyed out.

48

As one of the post-inspection demo scenarios, the student must be presented with a fully functional hydraulic vehicle hoist.

As a student I want to be presented with a demo of a hoist in good condition lifting and lowering a vehicle.

Students are presented with a fully functional hoist with a vehicle included. They can use this simulation to see how a hoist should properly work.

49

As one of the post-inspection demo scenarios, the student must be presented with a hoist where the vertical locks fail to engage. As a result of this failure, the vehicle will fall.

As a student I want to be presented with a demo of a hoist where both vertical locks fail to engage when a vehicle is lifted.

In the “Two Broken Vertical Locks” simulation the student can see a car drop due to multiple broken vertical locks.

50

As one of the post-inspection demo scenarios, the student must be presented with a hoist where the one of the vertical locks fail to engage. As a result of this failure, the vehicle will drop unevenly when lowered.

As a student I want to be presented with a demo of a hoist where one vertical lock fails to engage when a vehicle is lifted.

In the “Single Broken Vertical Lock” simulation the student can see a car drop due to one broken vertical lock.

51

As one of the post-inspection demo scenarios, the student must be presented with a hoist where one of the horizontal locks fail to engage. As a result of this failure, the arm with defective lock will kick out when the vehicle has been raised, causing the vehicle to teeter and potentially fall.

As a student I want to be presented with a demo of a hoist where one horizontal arm swing lock fails to engage when a vehicle is lifted.

Such a simulation has been created.

52

As one of the post-inspection demo scenarios, the student must be presented with a hoist with a height cut-off bar that fails to cut the hydraulic power when engaged. As a result of this failure, the roof of the vehicle will be damaged by the top of the hoist frame when raised.

As a student I want to be presented with a demo of a hoist where the height cut-off bar fails to cut hydraulic power.

Students can crush a car by raising it too much in the “Broken Height Cut-off Bar” simulation.
