This DLX will provide students the opportunity to hone their skills and practice labs in which they diagnose/repair electrical circuitry issues in motive power/ truck & coach vehicles. Students will apply their knowledge to:

  • Lab 1: Basic circuit - power source, conductors, load devices

  • Lab 2: Add-on Basics - Circuit protection and control devices

  • Lab 3: Build a series circuit

  • Lab 4: Build a parallel circuit

  • Lab 5: Build a series-parallel circuit

  • Lab 6: Measuring current flow through a series circuit. Circuit faults - this could be a diagnostic type of lab where the student has to identify the cause and repair a fault in a specific type of circuit

  • Lab 7: Measuring current flow through a parallel circuit Electrical test equipment - meters, test light, power probe(Trademark) if possible, AVR (battery tester)

  • Lab 8: Voltage drop testing for a series circuit to identify & repair a fault in the circuit

  • Lab 9: Voltage drop testing for a parallel circuit to identify & repair a fault in the circuit

  • Lab 10: Battery test foundation - this uses a battery of some kind, not the trainer board.

22 Jan 2024

  • Meeting with David, Steph and team to discuss the requirements for Labs 8 and 9, which are focused on voltage drop testing to identify faulty components in a pre-made series and parallel circuit

  • The required circuit will already be present on the board when the Lab 8 or 9 module is launched. As with all labs 6-9, there will be no user prompts or feedback in-game, so the student will need to rely on the accompanying Lab Worksheet or similar document.

  • By pre-building the circuit, we will have total control over the presence and placement of faulty components, thereby eliminating the risk of not knowing where (or if) the faulty component(s) were used.

    • In Lab 8, there is one specific lead that is damaged in the pre-made series circuit so that it has much higher resistance than expected, causing the circuit to not function as expected. The student will need to identify the faulty component using the DMM to measure voltage drops throughout the circuit. After replacing the faulty lead, the circuit will behave as expected. The student will record measurements and results in the separate worksheet.

    • In Lab 9, there are two faulty components in a parallel circuit that the student must identify using voltage drop testing with the DMM. The light bulb in the pre-made circuit will be faulty (open filament) and the student must identify the problem and replace the bulb. Another lead in the circuit will have high resistance, and the student must identify the problem and replace the lead so that the circuit functions as expected. The student will record measurements and results in the the separate worksheet.

  • Depending on timelines, it is possible that some ports or components on the board that are not expected to be used in any the labs might not have SpiceSharp digital twin functionality implemented in time for General Release. We have agreed that any missing functionality must be documented as a Bug in Jira, so that it can be included in the Release Notes. These tickets will have low priority because they are not expected to interfere with any of the required workflows in any way, but we will watch student feedback carefully to ensure that none of these missing ports or components are causing problems. If necessary, we can ‘disable’ or otherwise block non-functional ports or components from being used, to prevent any chance that they would cause confusion or problems for students. We will wait for further input from ID, CE, and student feedback to make a final decision.

Objectives for Motive Power - Trucking & Coach - Electrical Circuits

The objective of this module is to allow MP Trucking & Coach students to practice their theoretical knowledge of automotive/commercial vehicle electrical circuits and power systems in a workbench-style virtual lab environment.

The project charter describes 10 main tasks that the DLS should cover:

  1. Basic circuit - power source, conductors, load devices (pictorial/visual and schematic/technical form)

  2. Add-on Basics - Circuit protection and control devices

  3. Build a series circuit

  4. Build a parallel circuit

  5. Build a series-parallel circuit

  6. Circuit faults - this could be a diagnostic type of lab where the student has to identify the cause and repair a fault in a specific type of circuit

  7. Electrical test equipment - meters, test light, power probe(Trademark) if possible, AVR(battery tester)

  8. Voltage drop testing step by step - need clarification on how this is different from #6, possibly combine

  9. Testing circuit faults - need clarification on how this is different from #6, possibly combine.

  10. Battery test foundation

We will want a very sleek and user-navigable tutorial design as opposed to a narrative walkthrough design, so that users can go directly to the part of the tutorial that they need with minimal disruption to their DLS progress.

Assumptions / Known Constraints

  • Simulations developed under this plan are not intended to be played on tablets or cell phones.

  • Simulations developed on this charter are not guaranteed to have full functionality on devices that do not meet standard program device requirements.

  • Simulations developed under this charter are not intended to replace the teaching of the learning content, but rather augment it. They are intended to be part of formative learning experiences, not formal assessments. The simulation will not report any grade to the LMS, or provide any lasting record of the student’s performance outside of the current user session.

  • These simulations will not be designed to play with VR headsets or controllers.

  • These will not be multiplayer simulations (i.e. students playing in pairs or the professor having the opportunity to be in the simulation with the student at the same time).

  • Completion status will be recorded ONLY for the currently logged-in student in the LMS. If students work in pairs at a shared computer, only one of them will show as having completed the simulation.
