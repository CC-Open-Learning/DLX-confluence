Our Persona

Who

A 3rd year student in the Bachelor of Environmental Public Health Program

Our learner has the theoretical knowledge from classroom on the guidelines of Public health inspections.
However, they need to be able to implement that in the real-world-environment to get the practical aspect.

Goals

To apply the knowledge/skills in the real-world environment- salons, restaurants, etc

Technology Pre-requisites

--

This learner is not a gamer and is not always aware what keyboard keys should be used for navigation.

HMW (How Might We…)

Enable the student in the Public Health Inspection program, with the theoretical knowledge apply the skills through a decision-based procedural learning in conducting a health inspection similar to how one would conduct in the real-world following the Ontario Public Health Protocols and Guidelines.

Challenges

Restaurants and other facilities are generally reluctant to host students to conduct an inspection and instructors are often unavailable to accompany the students during any inspection. Hence students would not ideally get the practical experience.

Task Breakdown by Inspection Area:

Introduction POI and Handwashing Station POI

Public Health Inspector (PHI) enters the restaurant to conduct a routine public health inspection.

Before any tasks are available, the student must understand how to move around in the scene by completing a short tutorial.

After completing the tutorial, the learner has two mandatory tasks to perform before they can begin the inspection

  • Introduce yourself and declare the purpose of your visit to establish a right to enter the food service areas of the restaurant

  • Wash hands

Rationale

Click ‘Start’

DLX begins when Start is clicked. Game clock starts counting minutes and seconds (upward from zero)

Students need to tell us when they are ready to begin

Inital view

The initial view at the start of the game is a restaurant foyer, just inside the main entrance doors. From this position, the student should be able to see several areas of the restaurant, including the reception area or customer service counter, dining room, entrance to food service area, handwashing station, etc.

Restaurant environment should be immersive and realistic, so that the student feels like they can walk around naturally.

Follow tutorial

Brief tutorial shows the user how to move their mouse around the scene to see “move targets” in locations they can move to. User must click to select a target in the foyer to move to.

When completed successfully, it must be very clear that the tutorial is complete and the simulation is starting (click to confirm)

Students need to understand how to move around the scene by completing a brief tutorial. NOTE all POI targets are disabled during the tutorial, the student is only moving around within a few feet of where they spawned in. These non-POI targets are only active during the tutorial.

Click to select first POI target

Student will see POI targets as they move their mouse around the scene. They must click on a POI target to go to that location.

If the student selects any POI other than the reception area, they will get feedback to let them know that their simulation is ended, because they failed to properly identify themselves and the purpose of their visit before starting the inspection.

If the student selects the reception/host area, they will trigger successful completion of the Introduction POI task. This can be represented with a short audio or video example of a proper introduction and statement of purpose.


This is such a critical activity that the CE’s want the student to be inconvenienced if they forget about it. The simulation will end and the student must start again.

The student can continue with the inspection procedure if they remember to introduce themselves properly before getting started.

Click second POI target

Student will see POI targets as they move their mouse around the scene. They must click on a POI target to go to that location.

If the student selects any POI other than the handwashing station, they will get feedback to let them know that their simulation is ended, because they failed to wash their hands before starting the inspection.

If the student selects the handwashing station, they will trigger successful completion of the Handwashing POI task. This can be represented with a short audio clip signifying handwashing (scrub scrub rinse)


This is such a critical activity that the CE’s want the student to be inconvenienced if they forget about it. The simulation will end and the student must start again.

The student can continue with the inspection procedure if they remember to wash their hands before getting started.

Progress

Checking progress during the Inspection

As a learner, I need to be informed about the progress on the inspection, to keep track on how I am doing.

User tasks

  • Learner can click on the “Review” Inspection button on the toolbar to view their progress on the inspection conducted so far

  • Learner can view the

    • Number of locations visited (eg: 6/10)

    • Number of non-compliances reported ( eg: 8 )

    • Incorrect number of non-compliant issues

    • Total time taken so far

UI Behaviors

Review button on the toolbar

Onclick - opens a dialog box

The progress bar is shown on the left side

Learner can close the dialog by clicking the 'X'

Each value is White

Each value will be indicated by a color and/or with an exclamation mark that refers to the message “ Please verify your inspection.”

For incomplete /invalid numbers - font color is red ( #FF363F) with the exclamation mark else it will be white (#FFFFFF) with NO exclamation mark.

eg:

Locations visited – 6/10  (yellow exclamation)

Non-compliances reported- 7/7  (no exclamation)

Invalid non-compliances- 0   (no exclamation)

 

Message for exclamation still exists for at least one exclamation mentioned above else it disappears.

Decision
REMOVE the invalid non-compliances
REMOVE the indicating total number of non-compliances
REMOVE warning for verifying inspection
CHANGE font color from red to white
Rationale
Since the purpose of the DLX is a testing tool, even indicating the number of invalid non-compliances or total number is a form of hint to the player.

Non-compliance log

Log any non-compliance issues

As a learner, I need to log all the non-compliance issues and be able to access them during the inspection at any point of time.

On selecting the row, the background color changes- to view selection

A confirmation dialog appears for deletion.

On deletion, a success message appears on the top

Deleting Non-compliance Log entries

As a learner, I want to be allowed to delete an inspection entry, so that I have the freedom to change my decision and manage the compliance log

User Tasks

  • Learner can delete a non-compliant inspection from the log

  • Learner can confirm on the single deletion of an inspection

  • Learner can confirm the deletion of a photo inspection and stay informed that a photo is attached to it will also be deleted.

UI Behaviors

  • There is a trash icon across each entry

  • Clicking on the trash icon will trigger a confirm deletion box

  • if the inspection is anything but a photo inspection , then the confirmation delete will be only for that entry.

  • If the inspection is a photo inspection, then the confirmation delete will inform that the photo associated will also be deleted from the gallery

  • On deletion, a success message appears on the top

Non-compliance log - delete functionality

Decision
Single delete of entries- trash can against the entry. removal of edit, delete, cancel button
Rationale
To avoid complexity with a batch deletion of entries - confirmation messages for a batch that has diverse types of inspections
