Notes below indicate observations by dev team and Paul, as well as changes or improvements requested by Paul during the demo.

Notes and Feedback

  • Engine oil dipstick is the little yellow stick. Our current dipstick is actually the engine oil OR the transmission fluid fill area

    • Transmission oil may be incorrect as well. Paul will provide photos

  • Hydraulic fluid fill area

 

  • Each cap/engine part has symbols that Paul will provide. This will help students identify what engine part is which and transfer their knowledge from the sim to other machines, etc.

    • Idea is to have a symbol pop up on engine part on hover/select

  • Ensure that the dip sticks are pulled out, cleaned, re-dipped, and THEN checked during the graph scenario

 

  • Paul would prefer for the teeth to keep the falling animation. That means the retaining pin is missing when the teeth falls away. The teeth naturally have give, so students should know that a jiggle does not mean a loose tooth

 

  • Pins would be best to be inspected in groups instead of individually

    • Swing linkage

    • Stick linkage

    • Stabilizer Legs (Left/Right)

    • Bucket Linkage

    • Loader Bucket Linkage

 

  • Look at pictures for the top of the boom arm (Stick linkage)

    • The stick linkage should be 2 parts instead of 3

 

  • Boom Lock Latch control is in the incorrect location

    • It is supposed to be on the other side of the interior

 

  • Control Lever is named the Forward Neutral Reverse lever (FNR Lever)

 

  • Pedal beside brakes controls the tilt for the steering wheel. The telescoping is controlled by our current method.

    • Add this pedal

 

  • Backhoe controls

 

  • Make sure to add the gear shifter. Some models don’t have them, but Paul says it is important

 

  • Put the pin back in after the front loader bucket is lowered onto the lift arm guards before checking the engine belt

  • Just do a basic visual inspection to check that no obvious wires are damaged in the engine compartment. Don’t take anything apart, don’t dig down to every single wire

  • If bowl present, visually inspect the fuel water separator to know when to drain. If no bowl is on the fuel water separator, then watch the instrument panel (Hours). It will tell you when to drain it.

 

  • Fluid colours:

    • Coolant is pink or yellow/green

    • Oil is beige when fresh to black

    • Hydraulic/transmission fluid is light beige or clear
