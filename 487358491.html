VARLab : Drag & Drop - Sort and Match

Created by Stacey Dineen (Unlicensed), last modified on May 12, 2022

The concept here is simple:

The learner clicks and drags objects (photos, simple art assets, dialog box with text) to a defined Target Zone on the screen, and gets real-time feedback about their choices.

<insert sketch to illustrate concept>

Feedback

Real-time feedback helps guide the learner to discovery of the correct path. Feedback should be immediate, clear, and should not rely exclusively on one of the learner’s 5 senses to be understood. For example, if using a sound effect (ding for correct, sad trombone for incorrect), that feedback should be accompanied by a visual effect such as a thumbs-up/thumbs-down or checkmark and X. Never rely exclusively on colour (green for correct/ red for incorrect) because differently-abled learners may not perceive colours as we do.

One idea: If the student tries to drag an item to the correct target zone, it can snap into place or even disappear with a visual and audio effect to signal success. If the item is dropped at any other position on the screen, the item can float back to the spot where it was picked up, along with a visual and audio effect to signal “try again”

Example Use Cases

Personal Protective Equipment: The learner is presented with a large collection of personal apparel and PPE. They must choose which items are appropriate for the specified setting by dragging and dropping items onto a silhouette or photo of a worker.

Associate Rules with Rationale: Understanding the reasons for workplace rules or routines is an essential ingredient in recall and compliance. The learner could be given a collection of workplace rules to sort into categories such as Worker Safety, Food Safety, Safe Workplace.

Orientation Seek and Find: Learner could be given a collection of tools or items that need to be put away in their correct locations around the 360 photo environment.
