Pre-Lab

The Pre-Lab needs to be done before every Lab. The UI elements like instructions and controls are going to live inside glass panels.

Current Step (Highlighted):

The current step shall be highlighted with a rectangular fill with the following specifications:

  • Color: #FFFFFF (white)

  • Opacity: 15%

  • Left & Right Margin: 24px

  • Padding (all sides): 8px

  • border-radius: 16px

The complete instructions for a lab are displayed in the top half of the glass panel on the left. As a user completes each step, check-marks show up next to that step.

Instructions & Automatic Check-marks

The complete instructions for a lab are displayed in the top half of the glass panel on the left. As a user completes each step, check-marks show up next to that step.

Steps that need manual confirmation

The steps that can be completed only by confirming them manually, shall have a button to do so.

Instructions Panel:
This layout puts the instructions on the top-left as it will help students associate the instructions as the most important part and may help build the association to read the full instructions first. This area may be dynamic and change based on the instructions or feedback that the students need.

Circuit Diagram:
The bottom-left area of the screen shows a schematic circuit diagram that may change based on the connections made on the Ohmâ€™s Law and DC Circuit Trainer.

Knobs & Dials

The selected option shall be highlighted with white at 15% opacity.

Controls

Controls such as Full Screen, Exit and Settings show a label on hover to make their purpose clear to the users.

Feedback & Hints

Whenever the user makes a mistake, we shall interrupt their work and bring attention to the feedback modal. Feedback and hints show up as floating glass panels which can be minimized or expanded as needed.

Knowledge Check / Quiz

A knowledge check will show up after or before certain steps. The students can select the correct option and get visual feedback on whether their answer was correct.
