Created by Sana Javeed, last modified on Feb 04, 2025

Date: June 19,2024

This report contains details for testing the Ambulance and Paramedicine DLX with 23 students in-person.

A survey was also shared providing valuable insights into their experiences and perceptions of the simulation. This report aims to summarize and analyze the feedback, highlighting key areas of improvement and positive aspects.

Positive

The Ambulance DLX got a 64 as the Net Promotor score, where we asked them if they would recommend this DLX to other students in the program.

  • Students appreciated the simulation's accuracy, intuitive layout, and ease of finding items. They enjoyed the experience, noting that it closely resembled their real-life equipment from the lab. Overall, they found it an interesting and a valuable learning opportunity by visualizing them.

  • User 8 timed their seek & find II results with a friend and noted how the list allows optimizing the route

  • Excited about using this in 3rd semester

  • Majority of students found the difficulty level to be very easy, easy, or neutral.

  • Average time to complete the scenarios in seek & find II:

    • The ‘time taken to complete' data captured by 6 users.

Instructional Design Considerations

Overall, students understand the purpose of DLX and they appreciate the value of this resource.

Purpose

Overall, students had a strong understanding of the purpose of the experience. They identified the following purposes:

  • Locating equipment (18)

  • Application in scenarios (7)

  • Prepare for the future/placement (3)

  • No access to amb/opportunity to practice (3)

  • Low stakes practice (2)

  • Practice being efficient (2)

  • Gain confidence (2)

What would be most useful to first year students?

  • Learning what tools are used (5)

  • Ability to practice outside of a real ambulance (3)

  • Timer (challenged to located things quickly) (2)

  • Trauma and oxygen bag (2)

  • Prepare for placement (2)

  • Ability to become more efficient (1)

  • Gain comfort and confidence (1)

  • In-cabinet items (1)

Suggestions/ Improvements

Navigation and Control

  • Implement mouse sensitivity control for better user experience.

  • Allow panning the camera with WASD or directional keys to facilitate easier navigation. (1/22)

  • Enhance navigation to be easier, smoother, and faster.

  • Improve the responsiveness of the bags (Oxygen and trauma bags)

Item Positioning and Labelling

  • Have the items being positioned better like the straps and oxygen

  • Label bins (e.g., A1, A2) for faster navigation in the learning mode, and remove labels for timed sections.

  • Just highlighting the proper pocket and D tank in particular

Compatibility and Accessibility

  • Ensure the DLX works on all types of computers for wider accessibility.

  • Enable users to walk through the main door into the back of the ambulance for a more realistic experience.

User Engagement

  • Add a leaderboard feature to compare times with classmates and encourage friendly competition. Challege specific classmates

  • Reduce time to 5 mins in Seek & Find

  • Hear background distracting noise

  • Students wanted to turn on the lights and sirens

  • Would love to know how many mis-clicks in seek & Find II scenarios

  • Easter egg ideas:

    • Hawiian Shirt with “Embrace the Gray” 

    • Snacks in the glove box 

    • Used items back in the bag (eww!) 

  • Advanced level:

    • Shorter timer as they try the same scenario again

    • Find a subset of the list “ Go get this now”

    • No visible list, collect your own list

    • Wrong item penalties - reduce the timer by 15 seconds

Concerns

  • Difficulty and Frustration with Navigation & lag

    • 15/ 23 Users who found the navigation difficult to very difficult (7) OR neutral OR easy also found the speed slow and frustrating.

    • Specific instances of difficulty included:

      • 8/10 mac users experienced significant issues, including glitching and slow navigation (users 2, 3, 4, 5, 11, 13, 14, 15). while 2 users ( User 1 and 12 ) did not have any issues as they had a newer laptop

      • 7 Users ( user no. 7,8,10,16, 17, 18,20 ) - DID NOT own a mac also experienced similar lagging issues while exploring the bags/ cabinets

    • One reported slow navigation due to popups for each item and attributed the experience to be slow and frustrating without a mouse.

  • Lagging simulation : in full screen mode ( user 18)

  • Navigation Points: User 8 experienced glitches that threw their view around outside the ambulance.

  • Item Selection Difficulty:

    • Some items were tough to select or get to (more about it in the table below)

  • Battery drain

    • Drained the battery faster. Students did not have their charger with them.

Lessons Learnt

Research Goals and Objectives

Research Goals

Research Questions

Answers

Ease of use & Navigation (User Experience)

Find out how do students navigate using the DLX in order to complete tasks. How successful are they and how do they go about completing tasks?

Can the students navigate independently? Is it intuitive to move around and how do you find the speed of movement?

With what ease or difficulty are the students able to complete the levels in the DLX? Where do they get stuck?

Promotes learning (Instructional Design)

 Discover the aspects of design that support their learning achieved from the DLX

Are the students able to understand what is needed to be done to achieve the learning outcomes?

How would they use this resource? How would this help them with their learning?

What would be the frequency at which they would use this DLX? which parts of it are helpful?

Engagement Factor (Game Design)

 Understand how users stay engaged to complete an inspection while also determining the pain points encountered.

Do they understand the controls that are being used in the DLX? Do they have any pain points?

How engaged are they in the Explore ALL Level before they move to the next level?

What would keep them motivated to play the levels?

NOTE TO OBSERVERS:

WHAT TO DO

WHAT NOT TO DO

Being friendly and smiling when talking to them

Interrupting them way too often while they are doing the task

OR chatting with them

Observe the reactions and make notes at what point do they feel - frustrated, happy or confused

Helping them click on what they are supposed to do

Asking them follow-up questions when you see a reaction or a struggle OR ask them if everything is what they were expecting

When they are stuck, ask them questions like:

  • Could you explain more on how you got here?

  • What do you think should be the next step?

When they are stuck,

  • oh very simple. here it is.

  • Do you think it is a good idea?

If they are still stuck and are not able to proceed, then

  • Do you see anything on the screen that would help with you what you are maybe looking for?

  • Do you think there was a better way to approach or an intuitive way to represent that?

  • I observed that you seemed to be looking for something on the screen, could you tell me more?

    • What do you think about the back button being there? is that the place you expected it to be? if not, then where?

If they are still stuck and are not able to proceed, then

  • this is the button to click

  • is it a bad design to have that look?

  • is that confusing for you?

  • Did you notice that back button on the top left? was that intuitive enough?

When they provide feedback good or bad,

  • That is great feedback. that is very helpful

When they provide feedback good or bad,

  • Well,… we designed it that way because….

(justification for a design)

Having a poker face on any task they do

Expressions like being surprised or happy or congratulating on being able to successfully complete or saying

  • oh wow, nobody else got it but you

  • that is pretty obvious, you should have figured that out by now
