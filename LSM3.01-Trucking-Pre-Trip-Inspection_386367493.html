Incremental Release Plan

Because this program is just getting started, with the first cohort of students expected to begin classes in January May 2022, we have decided to deliver this project in several stages. At each stage, students will have a valuable learning experience with a portion of the total learning content, and we will have the benefit of feedback and input from students and instructors.

Release I - January 2022 - Exterior truck and trailer inspection - This release did not occur because the program start date was moved to May 2022

Release II - May 2022 - Exterior and interior (including instrumentation) Guided inspection

Release III - Sept 2022 - Exterior, interior, and engine compartment Guided inspection

Release IV - January February March 2023 - Alternate trailers inspection + Advanced inspection

Objective

The objective of this module is to allow Trucking students to inspect a truck and trailer combination in a virtual setting, as a supplement for practicing with the real equipment. It’s expected that the students would have an understanding of the necessary steps of the pre-trip inspection before beginning the simulation, so no new material needs to be presented by the simulation.

As a learning tool, the simulation can be used in three modes to enhance and support learning outcomes:

The Tutorial mode will teach the learner how to interact with the DLS, what to expect, and how to succeed. The Tutorial will automatically be launched the first time the learner accesses the simulation, but they are not forced to complete it (they can exit). The Tutorial remains available anytime the learner wants to revisit it.

In January 2023, we received feedback that the in-game tutorial for Guided inspection was not well accepted by students. The feedback indicated that students were not accessing the tutorial prior to attempting the virtual inspection, and that most students were exiting the tutorial after less than a minute (prior to being shown how to complete the inspection tasks). This was pretty harsh feedback, but very useful. On Jan 17/2023, I had a meeting the Ujjwal and Anzhelika to discuss options for making the in-game tutorial a bit more user friendly. We identified two main areas for improvement:
1. Script overhaul - we need to massively trim down the voiceover script, especially the ‘introductory’ information that takes up the first several minutes of the tutorial. Voiceover must be much more focused and concise. The complete tutorial must be substantially reduced in length.

2. Modular topics - we need to let the student navigate directly to what they want to know, instead of making them complete the entire tutorial from the beginning when they just have one quick question. This will involve logically chunking the information up into independent segments that can be accessed in any order, so that the student can go straight to the thing they need information about.

It is anticipated that the redesign and new implementation will take at least 2-4 extra weeks of development time prior to GR, which shifts our delivery target to end of Feb 2023.

After the learner is confident that they know how to use the DLS, we will offer two different play modes. Both of these modes will follow the same basic progression through the simulation, but one of them is more about EXPLAINING & SHOWING and the other is more about DOING.

EXPLAIN

Tell me what needs to be done and why.

Show me what to do and how to do it

Let me do it myself to practice what I’ve learned

In Guided mode, the learner will be guided through the pre-trip inspection, receiving prompts, rationale, and immediate feedback at each step of the inspection. This is intended to solidify the student’s understanding of the inspection process and support their ability to visually recognize good and bad conditions of the equipment. Many people learn best by watching and listening, they prefer to be shown how to do something before they try it themselves.

In Advanced mode, the learner would conduct the inspection without prompts, and feedback would be provided at the end of the inspection, instead of after each step. This will allow students to assess their own readiness for an in-person summative evaluation.

As in all DLS outcomes, we need to strictly avoid any feedback or results that could be mistaken by the learner as a “grade”, as these learning experiences are not being graded.

Assumptions / Known Constraints

  • It is expected that the students be familiar with the vehicle in question.

  • In the real world, students will have access to a variety of different equipment and inspection conditions that will not be covered in this simulation. This experience is intended to focus on the inspection procedure and best practices, rather than emphasize the specifics of the equipment used in this simulation.
