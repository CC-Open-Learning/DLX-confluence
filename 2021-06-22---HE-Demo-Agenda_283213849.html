Demo Outcomes

Notes below indicate observations by dev team and Paul, as well as changes or improvements requested by Paul during the demo.

Notes

  • Loose nuts could have alternate colours to improve visibility. Possibly yellow or red.

  • There should be only one windshield wiper on the front and back window, right in the centre. Can be attached at top or bottom.

  • Inaccuracies for the backhoe: Parts are missing at the rear of the backhoe. Paul will send photos of the rear connections.

  • Backhoe piston/cylinder rods should be a bright, shining chrome colour. Paul will send more photos.

  • "Click to continue" prompt could be changed to "Click when inspection complete" or similar

  • Suggestion for guiding learner through the inspection - We need to guide the learner to do some steps before others, such as you have to check the oil before you inspect the cab interior.

  • We want to enforce the flow & logical order of the inspection. Ideally the inspection should take no more than about 10 minutes

  • Oil, coolant, hydraulic fluid - if these are not addressed, the learner should not be allowed to progress with the simulation

  • Every cap and dipstick has a symbol on it to indicate the fluid involved - this is really important for learners to recognize.

  • Suggestion was a popup thermometer to convey that the engine is hot. If the engine is hot, only check the fluid level on the side marking of the tank, do not remove cap

    • Alternate approach could be refraction effect above engine, or UI message indicating engine is hot

  • The inspection has to stop if the fluids are not at correct/usable levels, the learner should not be allowed to start the engine. To do so would cause serious damage to the machine

  • Immediate feedback to the learner if they try to start the machine without addressing the fluids

  • Once fluids have been checked the engine can be started and allowed to warm-up. At this point the rest of the backhoe can be inspected. This teaches the student to maximize time by inspecting the backhoe while it is warming-up.

  • Some inspection points are mission-critical (could cause damage to the machine or pose a serious safety risk) while other aspects are less crucial (broken window or back-up beeper)

  • Brakes will not function unless the backhoe is on

  • RPM gauge would be on, this indicates the engine is turned on. All lights must be turned on manually, they will not turn on just by starting the backhoe

Next steps from Paul

  • Paul will get additional photos of:

    • universal symbols of engine fluids (oil, coolant, hydraulic)

    • underside of carriage

    • inside of the cab

    • Rear bucket and arm, how the components are connected

Ideas for the Future

  • When clicking on a part, real-world images of that part would pop-up to show the student.

  • Each attempt at a scenario would result in less and less "hand-holding" of the next run. Ex. 1st attempt would be 100% guided. 2nd attempt would be 80% guided. And so on.
