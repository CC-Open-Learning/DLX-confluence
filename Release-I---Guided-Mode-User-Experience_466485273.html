VARLab : Release I - Guided Mode User Experience

Created by Stacey Dineen (Unlicensed), last modified on Mar 17, 2022

Order of Inspection steps for Guided Mode:

Initial View and Welcome

On initial load, the camera will approach the vehicle as though the student is walking towards it from a distance of approx 20-40m. This is from the student's point of view, at approx eye level, lasting no more than a few seconds to help orient the learner to the simulation environment.

The voiceover will welcome the student and advise them to follow the prompts to practice a walk-around inspection of the truck and trailer exterior. Students will be prompted to observe each inspectable item in order.

Camera Behaviour

In Guided Mode, the camera will move automatically for each inspection, there will be no need for the learner to navigate manually using either keyboard or mouse. The Guided Mode inspection walks the student through the inspection process so that the student does not decide what to inspect or in what order. It is important to note that the student will have no control over the order of the inspection steps - they will be required to go through the inspection in a set order, with no opportunity to skip ahead or return to a previous step.

Interaction Model

There are two types of inspections in this simulation: Visual and Physical.

Visual inspections are passive - they require no extra action to identify the condition of the component being inspected - the student will simply observe the model and decide whether or not to report damage for the component that is shown. Examples of visual inspections would include Windows, Mirrors, and Exterior Condition.

Physical inspections are active - these inspections require a simulated action beyond just looking. Examples of physical inspections would include Tire Pressure, where the student needs to thump the tire with a mallet to evaluate the air pressure, or Van Doors, where the student needs to unlatch and open the doors. To trigger a physical inspection, the learner will be prompted to click on an icon in the Physical Inspection tray.

Note: At each position of the inspection process, only the applicable icons will be available in the physical inspection tray. These icons will change as the learner progresses to each stage of the inspection, so that the icon tray doesn’t get too crowded or overwhelming. Each icon will be enabled when the student is prompted to trigger that inspection.

For all inspection interactions, the learner will observe the virtual truck model to evaluate the condition of the component, and will have two options for how to respond: Report Damage or .

Report Damage: If the learner sees something wrong (or questionable), they will click on the item. Clicking on the model will trigger the Inspection UI dialogue box, which will provide selectable options for what types of damage/wear could occur for this component. These options are customized for each component, so that only applicable options are displayed for the specific component (ie. tires will offer options such as excessive tread wear or embedded object, while lights will offer options such as cracked or burnt out). In Guided Mode, the learner will have immediate feedback when they select an option from the Inspection UI - the background colour of the option they select will change to green (if they have correctly identified the state of the component) or it will change to red (if they have incorrectly identified the state of the component). The learner will have the opportunity to change their answer as many times as they need to, until they get the “green” result. Note that the Inspection UI is ONLY used for reporting damage/wear. There is no option in the dialog to report that a component is in good condition.

Next Inspection (Nothing to Report): If the learner does not see anything wrong with the component under inspection, they will click the “Next Inspection” button in the toolbar to move on to the next component. This will prevent the learner from having to report components that are in good condition, which will make the inspection process true to real-life practice where only damage/wear is reported. In Guided Mode, the student will not be allowed to move on while there is unreported damage/wear. If they fail to report damage that is present, the voiceover will prompt them to take a closer look, and will remind them about what to look for. If the learner has successfully identified all damage/wear for a particular inspectable component, the Next Inspection button will move them to the next inspectable component at this position. When all components at a particular position have been inspected successfully, the Next Inspection button will move the learner to the next position.

Position #1 - Drivers Side Front (DSF)

The camera will stop at the front driver's side corner of the vehicle, angled so that the camera is looking at most of the front and driver's side of the truck.  This will be called Position DSF. From here, the student will be prompted by the voiceover to inspect items that are in this immediate area.

  1. Fluid Leaks (note if present) - visible from this vantage point

  2. Exterior Condition (dents, abrasions) - front or drivers side cab

  3. Exterior Lights (damaged, burnt out) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION (student is prompted to click the Test Lights and Trailer Brake icon to inspect)

    1. Headlights and fog lights

    2. Indicator lights

    3. Front license plate light

    4. Marker lights

  4. Windows (damaged, dirty) - windshield or drivers side cab door

  5. Mirrors (damaged, loose, missing) - hood mirror, drivers side door mirror

  6. Wheels/Tires - drivers side steer tire - VISUAL and PHYSICAL INSPECTIONS (specified below)

    1. Sidewall/Rim/Lug Nuts- VISUAL - Camera automatically moves to rim and sidewall view: Rim (cracked, dented), lug nuts (loose), tire sidewall (cracked)

    2. Tread - VISUAL - Camera automatically moves to tire tread view near the ground at the front of the DS steer tire: Steer tire tread (excessive or uneven wear, embedded object)

    3. Mallet icon - PHYSICAL INSPECTION - The camera automatically moves to an oblique angle showing both tread and sidewall of the DS steer tire. Voiceover prompts the student to click the tire pressure icon. When clicked, the mallet animation plays to demonstrate a properly inflated or underinflated tire.

  7. Behind Tire - drivers side steer tire - PHYSICAL INSPECTION (click Behind Tire icon to inspect). Camera moves behind the tire to look at the inside aspect of the wheel.

    1. Brake chamber (cracked, loose)

    2. Brake hoses (leaking, damaged)

    3. Brake drum (cracked)

    4. Axle seal (leaking)

Note that we cannot complete the Behind Tire inspection of the brakes and axle seals for steer tires in Release I. This is because it is not possible to see behind steer tires without opening the hood. Therefore, the Behind Tire POI for steer tires in Release I, this will be done in Release III, when the hood is open).

After completing all of the tasks at the DSF position, the learner will click on Next Inspection to move to the next inspection point.  In Guided Mode, if the learner tries to move on without identifying a damaged component, they will be unable to leave and will be prompted to complete what has been left undone.  If all inspection tasks have been completed, they will move to the next inspection point.

Position #2 - Driver’s Side Cab (DSC)

The camera will stop at the driver's side of the vehicle, around the fifth wheel, angled so that the learner is looking directly at the side of the vehicle. This will be called Position DSC. From here, the student will be prompted via voiceover to inspect items that are in this immediate area: 

  1. Fluid Leaks (note if present) - visible from this vantage point

  2. Exterior Condition (dents, abrasions) - driver’s side cab or front/side of trailer

  3. Conspicuity Tape (peeling, missing) - driver’s side of trailer

  4. Exterior Lights (damaged, burnt out) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION (click icon to inspect)

    1. Marker lights

  5. Fuel tank (dents, leaking, straps) - drivers side tank

  6. DEF tank (leaking) and battery (connections, damage) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION (click Battery icon to open access panel to inspect)

  7. Wheels/Tires - drivers side rear tractor tires aka drive tires - 2 sets of double tires - VISUAL and PHYSICAL INSPECTIONS (specified below)

    1. Sidewall/Rim/Lug Nuts- VISUAL - Camera automatically moves to rim and sidewall view of outer tire: Rim (cracked, dented), lug nuts (loose), tire sidewall (cracked)

    2. Tread - VISUAL - Camera automatically moves to tire tread view so that treads of both tires are visible in the same view: Rear tractor tire tread (excessive or uneven wear, embedded object)

    3. Mallet icon - PHYSICAL INSPECTION - The camera automatically moves to an oblique angle showing both tread and sidewall of each double set of rear tractor tires. Voiceover prompts the student to click the tire pressure icon. When clicked, the mallet animation plays to demonstrate a properly inflated or underinflated tire. Note that only one of any double set of tires will be underinflated, to promote comparison between good and bad tires when learning to recognize the signs of underinflation.

  8. Behind Tire - driver's side rear tractor tires aka drive tires. Camera moves automatically behind the tire to look at the inside aspect of the wheel and brake components. This view will be under the trailer, so supplemental lighting will be needed. Note that the voiceover will prompt the student to perform a visual inspection of the brake chambers, lines, and drums, there is no inspection task for the brake drums and they will never show any damage in this inspection. Note that the student will need to inspect brake components for both the forward and rear axles in the same view (looking from behind the rear set of drive tires toward the front of the truck). Both brake chambers and lines must be visible and inspectable in this view for the driver’s side rear tractor tires.

    1. Brake chambers (cracked, loose)

    2. Brake hoses (leaking, damaged)

    3. Axle seals (leaking)

  9. Fifth wheel coupling (pin and handle not correct position, coupling “jaw” not secure)

  10. Exterior Lights - Rear Tractor (damaged, burnt out) - rear tractor lights can only be seen while underneath the trailer, so we inspect these in the same view where we are inspecting the fifth wheel

    1. Brake lights

    2. Reverse lights

    3. Rear tractor license plate light

  11. Exterior Lights - Passenger side rear cab (damaged, burnt out) -

    1. Indicator lights - these are located on either side of the cab, up high above the level of the doors. There are no indicator lights on the rear of the tractor area that would be underneath the trailer.

    2. Marker lights

  12. DS Dolly leg (leg not raised, handle not secured)

  13. Air line and power cable connections (loose power cable, damaged power cable, leaking air line, damaged air line)

After completing all of the tasks at the DSC position, the learner will click on Next Inspection to move to the next inspection point.  In Guided Mode, if the learner tries to move on without identifying a damaged component, they will be unable to leave and will be prompted to complete what has been left undone.  If all inspection tasks have been completed, they will move to the next inspection point.

    2. Unlatch doors and open fully (hinge damage, door damage) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION

    3. Cargo area interior of trailer (damaged, debris present)

    4. Close doors and securely close (latch damage) - PHYSICAL INSPECTION
