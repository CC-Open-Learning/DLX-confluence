(PoV) (Photo representation of the plant equipment with some 3D assets)

This simulation was developed as part of a research grant project funded by OMAFRA. It attempts to augment traditional training on sanitation practices in a meat packing plant, with a special focus on the employee training experience for newcomers to Canada with limited English but still in need of critical safety and compliance training.

Objective

The primary objective of the OMAFRA research project is to prove that learning outcomes can be significantly improved through the use of interactive VR/AR learning simulations, when compared with more traditional paper or PowerPoint-style training.

Points of Contrast:

Traditional Paper or Slides

XR Immersive/Interactive Training

Instructor driven, pace decided by trainer

User driven, learn at your own pace

Heavy reliance on literacy or English fluency

Focused on demonstration, less reliance on text or spoken language

No learner interaction, very passive experience

High degree of learner interaction

Poor retention, too much memorization of facts with no chance to practice

All essential tasks and procedures are practiced in simulated scenarios

High risk to learner or workplace if mistakes are made in hazardous environments while in training

Zero risk to worker or workplace, mistakes are a healthy part of learning when they happen in a safe environment

5 Modules

VARLab will deliver 5 DLS modules in SCORM format (suitable for deployment using any LMS that supports SCORM format).

2 Custom-branded modules: GMP and RTE

Of the 3 planned content modules listed in the project definition, two of these will be copied and re-built using custom branded photos and/or slightly modified content that is specific to VG Meats food processing facility.

NOTE that there is no intention to cover facility-specific processes or equipment training in any detail. The learning objectives for the customized modules are not significantly different from the generic modules. Example: The generic module indicates that the trainee should follow SOP for documenting equipment sanitation. The customized module will instruct the trainee to follow SOP specified by VG Meats to document equipment sanitation. We can show photos of VG Meats documentation, but we will not add module content

Assumptions / Known Constraints

  • Project is timeboxed: It needs to be as ‚Äúgood as it can get‚Äù by Dec 2022

  • The deliverables are ‚Äúrelease candidate‚Äù builds, in SCORM package format, suitable for deployment to any standardized LMS. For the purposes of this project, a ‚Äúrelease candidate‚Äù is defined as being a playable, interactive learning experience that is user-friendly and satisfies some or all of the defined learning objectives for the target module.

  • Content expert Nicole Detlor has identified ‚ÄúGood Manufacturing Processes‚Äù and ‚ÄúReady to Eat Sanitation‚Äù as our two modules.

  • Two different versions of each module will be delivered:

    • Branded module - This will include the name, logo, and other branded apparel or assets from our industry partner, VG Meats.

    • Generic module - All media or text that makes specific reference to or includes the name, logo, or brand identifiers of VG Meats will be replaced with generic (non-branded) media or text, as appropriate.

  • It is understood that the release candidate modules do NOT have to cover 100% of the training content associated with that area. These modules are intended to enhance existing training, to make it more effective and improve learning outcomes. These modules are not intended to REPLACE existing employee training programs, and will be used in conjunction with comprehensive traditional training programs.

  • We have fixed development resources for this project.

  • These modules must be developed in CORE, for purposes of potential future maintenance or extension.

  • It is recognized that our development team will require ongoing access to the industry partner facility, VG Meats, as needed, to take photos or better understand training processes or content.

  • There is no requirement for User Acceptance Testing as part of this project. Our goal is not to produce complete DLS modules, but to demonstrate what those modules could be like (samples) and how our interactive and engaging DLS experiences lead to improved learning outcomes.
