Today’s Q&A meeting was focused on the student focus group that happened in the morning of 11APR2024, with groups of 4th year students that played the 29MAR2024 Playable Demo in the isolated demo course shell. Our Q&A meeting was not an in-depth analysis of the student feedback, because they team had not had any opportunity to review or consolidate the feedback prior to meeting with Ken, but we did take the opportunity to raise several questions that came up during the students' experience, to get Ken’s perspective. I’ll focus on capturing those responses here:

  • Results NOT Feedback: The most important decision that Ken made in our meeting was to agree that our original plan of evaluating the student’s inspection results (“marking” inspection results as Correct, Incorrect, Invalid) is not actually going to work. We decided that our Inspection Results will continue to include a complete record of all the inspections that the student recorded during their playthrough, but without any evaluation or feedback for those inspection results.
    Rationale: There are many contextual or situational variables that impact inspection results, which are not possible to include in our virtual experience without adding an enormous amount of scope to the project. For example, when the student inspector finds a rice bin sitting directly on the floor in the pantry, this could be a storage issue that needs to be recorded as a non-compliance, or it could just be temporary situation while someone is using the rice to prepare a recipe. If the temperature readings on the wall thermometer and the free-hanging thermometer in the walk-in cooler are showing very different temperatures, further investigation would be required to determine if one of the thermometers was faulty, or if they are both correct and the fault is with the refrigeration unit. Bottom line, there is just no way for us to judge the compliance or non-compliance of every inspectable, because there may be factors contributing to either outcome. The student will provide those notes and details in their Hedgehog report, but there is no opportunity to do so in the DLX, so we can’t judge. Decision as follows:

DLX Inspection Results WILL include

Results WILL NOT include

  • Complete list of inspections recorded in the Inspection Log (all Compliant and Non-Compliant inspections) including photos and temperature readings, if taken.

  • Any feedback about correct/incorrect/invalid inspection results.

  • List of Non-Compliances that were present in the scene, but were NOT reported by the student.

  • Any indication that the student’s assessment of those inspectables (if any) was “incorrect”. We are highlighting items that we intended to be perceived as non-compliant. If the student disagrees, that is not “incorrect” - their opinion is valid and they can explain themselves in Hedgehog. The list is for reference only, so they can reconsider their process and assessments.

  • Information about the total number of Inspection Areas (POI’s) that were inspected, compared to the number of POI’s that were available to be inspected, so that they can identify if they skipped any areas of the facility.

  • NOTE: We previously used to use the word “visited” but we will change that to “inspected”. Simply navigating to/through a POI will not count as “inspected”; the student must click on at least one inspectable for that POI to be considered “inspected”. To help prevent confusion, we will be more specific in our wording.

  • Specific list of individual inspectables for each POI that were inspected or not inspected - this gives away too much information for their next playthrough. We will list the areas, not the individual items.

  • Time elapsed to complete inspection

  • Indication if this time was “good” or “bad”. Students may wish to evaluate their own efficiency or try to improve their process through practice, but the DLX will not make judgements about how much time was spent

  • Some simple bugs/adjustments are noted below, with Jira tickets for reference.
