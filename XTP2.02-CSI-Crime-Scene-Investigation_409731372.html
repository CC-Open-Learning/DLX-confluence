Students investigate a randomized series of scenarios with criminal actions at a diner and decide which criminal charges are appropriate. They collect and ‘bag’ evidence, write up the report and identify the appropriate charges. (This is an early version, a more complex version has been developed for both flatscreen and VR Headset play)

Workaround

Camera control can be difficult, mouse sensitivity is high. This can be temporarily disorienting or may cause minor frustration when looking/moving around the simulation. This is not a blocking issue.

Learners will quickly adapt mouse movement to make very small, controlled movements with the mouse. If the mouse cursor is bounded at the edge of the screen, the learner can open the Tablet UI (press Tab key) to interrupt the camera controller and reset their mouse cursor to a more convenient position on the screen.

Very wide screen resolution (e.g. 2560x1080) can cut off the top and bottom of the play area in full screen mode. This is not a blocking issue.

Most popular monitor configurations would not see this problem, and it is easily avoided by exiting full-screen mode, if the view is annoying.

Browser security settings, especially in Safari, may interfere with launching the sim. This problem may affect other browsers, but we have only been able to reproduce the problem so far with Safari. This is a browser setting that the learner can manipulate, and therefore is not a blocking problem.

Disabling all cookies prevents the simulation from launching. For best experience, cross-site tracking and all cookies should be enabled in the Safari browser. We recommend Edge and Chrome.

Browser with less than 4GB RAM will not perform well. The sim may be very slow to launch, navigation may be jerky and slow, and the simulation may crash when RAM requirements are not met. This is not a problem with the simulation, it is a platform requirement.

We recommend minimum 4GB (8GB recommended) for playing this simulation

Browser with WebGL disabled will be unable to launch the simulation. This is not a problem with the simulation, it is a platform requirement.

We recommend recent version of Edge or Chrome for best experience.
