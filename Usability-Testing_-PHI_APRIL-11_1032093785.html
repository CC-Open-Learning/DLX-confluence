Instructor pre and de-briefing requirements User 17a3a

  • Pre-brief: Prior to the inspection, it was observed that 3 out of 6 individuals failed to introduce themselves, with 2 students among them. Two of these individuals mentioned that they were unaware of the requirement. It's crucial to reinforce to students the importance of completing the two mandatory tasks before commencing an inspection: introducing themselves to the manager and washing hands at the sink.

  • De-brief: Discussions on follow-up actions, notes based on the infractions reported, critical and non-critical infractions (expiry dates on the food items, questions to the operator) can be part of the de-briefing activity with the students.

Ease of use & Navigation (User Experience)

Find out how do students navigate using the DLX in order to complete tasks. How successful are they and how do they go about completing an investigation?

Can the students navigate the scenes independently (i.e. would a tutorial be needed to provide enough info)?

While some students felt the speed of navigation fast, they appreciated the target points to navigate to an area. However, a tutorial was suggested to ensure they know the purpose of the inspection review window and how to access it.

With what ease or difficulty are the students able to complete the tasks? What issues do they run into (if any) while completing tasks?

They found the closing of the inspection window to be an obstruction everytime they clicked on ‚Äúcompliant‚Äù or non-compliant‚Äù

Promotes learning (Instructional Design)

¬†Discover the aspects of design that support their learning achieved from the DLX

Are the students able to understand what is needed to be done to achieve the learning outcomes?( a practice tool)

Students see the benefit and found conducting inspections to be intuitive and easy to understand, to then view the inspections reported and to be informed by the progress being made in the simulation ( eg: how many locations have been inspected), viewing the gallery of photos and

Does the inspection log help in completing their hedgehog report?

They pointed out certain infractions like unlabeled food items that indicate raw/cooked and/or expiry dates that need to be a part of the pre-briefing and de-briefing activities with the instructor
