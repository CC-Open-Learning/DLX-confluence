What is a Lathe and what is it used for?

  • A lathe is a machine tool that rotates a workpiece about an axis of rotation to perform various cutting and forming operations, usually employing a single-point tool which moves in 2 axes to cut material, creating symmetry about the rotating axis​

Major parts

  • E-stop

  • Power

  • Brake

  • Spindle and Spindle on/off

  • Feed on/off

  • Main Power Switch

Things to be aware of

  • Tool holder

  • Chuck key

  • Position of the piece in the chuck

  • brake

How to Safely Start the Lathe

  1. Turn on the main power

  2. Place the workpiece in the chuck. Important!!! Never take your hand off the chuck key when it’s in the chuck!!!

  3. Set the RPM according to the workpiece size. (check video to see if it was mentioned potential hazard if the rpm is incorrect)

  4. Adjust the settings

  5. Check if E-Stop is presses and release it after confirming issue that caused e-stop has been mitigated.

  6. Turn on the machine (power switch in the front panel) and make sure the power light is on.

  7. Inspect tool, check if it’s burnt, chipped or broken.

  8. Adjust the tool in the tool holder

  9. Before turning on the spindle, move to the side of the machine, away from the workpiece

    1. metal chip can fly towards you

  10. Turn on the spindle

Turning off the machine

  • Anytime that something dangerous or unexpected occurs on the machine, you should immediately press the brake or the E-stop to stop whatever is happening on the machine. 

  • It is preferred that you use the brake, as it will cut power to the spindle and apply the brake, which will stop all moving parts. 

  • Using the E-stop only will cut power, but the spindle will continue to rotate and “coast” to a stop. 

  • Before restarting the machine, investigate and make the appropriate changes to prevent whatever happened from happening again.

  • To restart the machine, move the spindle lever to neutral, rotate the e-stop clockwise enough to allow it to pop out, and proceed as normal
