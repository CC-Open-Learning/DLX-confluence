Release Notes

What's New in Trucking Pre-Trip Inspection - Release IV

In this final milestone for Trucking, you will find the following brand new content:

  • Advanced Inspection - This is the "next level" of the inspection workflow, where the students are given the opportunity to practice a pre-trip inspection entirely on their own, with no step-by-step prompts or immediate feedback.  

    • Free roaming navigation means that students can start anywhere and inspect the truck in any order. They can revisit areas that they have already inspected and change their answers as many times as they want.

    • Checklist feature will help students keep track of what they have already inspected, and help remind them about what needs to be inspected in each area of the truck.  However, it is up to the student to look at the Checklist, because no prompts or reminders will be shown during the inspection.

    • When they are ready, students will "Finish" their inspection and see their Results.  They will be able to clearly see how many mistakes they made (if any), along with photos to clarify what went wrong.  Side-by-side comparison photos will help students identify equipment that is in good or bad condition.

    • Just like the Guided Inspection, students can leave and come back anytime.  They will be given the opportunity to choose to Continue their previous inspection or Start from the beginning.

  • Redesigned Tutorials - We have separated the tutorials for Guided and Advanced and segmented them into short, playable episodes that focus on specific aspects of the sim.  

    • Tutorial topics such as How to Report a Problem and How to Get a Better Look will allow students to quickly get help with a specific problem without having to sit through several minutes of other content.

    • Playthrough videos will show students how to succeed in every type of inspection in Guided mode. These videos do not give away any answers, but they do help to make sure that nobody gets stuck.

    • Simple pictorial menus help students with weaker reading comprehension skills focus on the truck, and not the text.

  • Alternate Trailers - We recognized that there are some essential inspection steps that we just couldn't cover in the main inspection, because different trailers have special inspection needs.  We can't cover every trailer that exists, but our content expert John Way helped us to identify two really common trailers that would be of high value to our students. Please note that none of the common inspections that were practiced on the cargo van trailer in the main inspections are repeated in the extended trailer segments - only the inspections that are specific to these new trailers are practiced in the extended segments.

    • Flatbed trailer - Focus is on load securement devices (straps, hooks, winches) and inspecting the cargo deck for damage or debris.

    • Refrigerated trailer - Focus is on inspecting the refrigeration unit, including the self-diagnostic routine on the control panel, and checking the fuel supply.

Of course, we also invested a lot of time fixing bugs and implementing student feedback into the Guided Inspection, so you will notice changes there as well.  Our focus has been mainly on improving the playability and flow, eliminating extra steps, avoiding confusion about what to do next, and making sure that the student can focus on the equipment.

TI-952

Med

Instructors cannot use Save and Load - they do not have a LearnerID from the LMS

Brightspace LMS does not provide a unique identifier for users in the Instructor or Group Leader roles. When a save file is generated for one of these users, the Learner ID is “preview” (for all of them, not a unique value). As such, if there are multiple instructors, everyone overwrites the save file in a last-one-out fashion, causing very unpredictable behaviour. Students have unique LearnerID’s, so this problem does not affect students.
