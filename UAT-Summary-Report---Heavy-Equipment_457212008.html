Synopsis of User Feedback - 8 Responses Total

  • All respondents (8/8) had no trouble accessing or launching the DLS.

  • Most respondents (8/8) felt the introduction video provided the right level of information.

  • Most respondents had some trouble moving around the backhoe.

    • Few respondents (2/8) indicated all movement was perfect.

    • Few respondents (5/8) indicated it was mostly okay, with some trouble moving around.

    • 1 respondent (1/8) had a lot of trouble moving around.

  • The problem areas noted by the testers can be summarised with the 3 following categories:

    • Order of operations: Several respondents, including two of our content experts, were not satisfied with direction the simulated inspection took in comparison to a real life pre-op inspection. They felt it did not correctly represent the inspection experience they would want learners to have.
      Examples of issues mentioned by respondents:

      • The user enters and exits the cab too many times

      • The inspection should be a single straightforward path around the backhoe, no going back and forth around

      • Rear boom lock needs to be an inspection point very early on for safety reasons

    • Minor errors in models or labels: A number of respondents also stated minor issues such as incorrect labels and small appearance flaws that need to be corrected to accurately represent a real-life backhoe.
      Examples of issues mentioned by respondents:

      • Engine components require universal symbols on them

      • 4-wheel driving instead of 4-wheel steering

      • Fuel cap colour

      • Door handles being label as locks

      • Coolant reservoir needs level notches

    • Damage too subtle to be seen: Most respondents made comments that they could not visually distinguish a fair amount of the “good” components from their damaged versions.
      Examples of issues mentioned by respondents:

      • Unable to tell cylinder/piston states

      • Unable to determine if there are leaks

      • Unable to tell if the ROPS is damaged

      • Unable to see engine belt damage

Post-UAT Plan of Action

Jira Issue

Issue Description

Proposed Solution

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-667

The camera angle set in place for this inspection does not actually give the user a close enough view of the latch to be able to accurately inspect it.

Move the camera angle closer.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-675

All damage to the pistons/cylinders is not visible at all, or is not noticeable enough to indicate realistic damage.

Create new decals that are more in line with what the damage should look like, and exaggerate them to make them more noticeable.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-680

The cleanliness of the coolant fluid in the coolant reservoir is irrelevant during the inspection. Only the level matters, which is difficult to assess at it stands.

Make a “High”/”Low” marking or notch on the coolant fluid’s reservoir.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-681

The object labelled as the "Air Filter" isn't correct. It should be labelled the "Air Flow Restriction Indicator".

Review every reference to “Air Filter” and change it to “Air Flow Restriction Indicator”.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-682

In the cab, the "torque converter temperature" indicator should be labelled as the "transmission fluid temperature" indicator.

Change the label to “Transmission Fluid Temperature”.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-683

Symbol being used is 4-wheel steering, which is rather uncommon. 4-wheel drive is more universal and has a similar symbol.

Replace the 4-wheel steering indicator with the one for 4-wheel drive.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-684

The door handles on the inside of the cab are listed as locks. There are no locks on the inside of the cab in reality.

Make the current red “lock” tab just a regular door handle/latch.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-685

The fuel tank cap is the wrong colour. Currently it looks blue, and in real life if fuel was put in a tank with a blue cap, it would be a major issue as blue is for a different fluid.

Change the cap colour to green or black.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-687

Top beacon light does not flash or spin like one would in real life so it does not necessarily signify that it is working correctly.

The projected orange light currently being used to indicate that it’s working can be made to flash/strobe.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-688

The section where the rear boom lock is checked and locked needs to be one of the first things done before the rest of the inspection. It's a lethal hazard to walk under the rear boom while it is unlocked.

Move the lock check section to the very first time the user enters the cab.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-689

All hydraulic line leaks are not visible or not noticeable enough to indicate a leak.

Create new decals to make leaks stand out, exaggerated even if necessary.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-690

The damage on the engine belt is difficult to see and needs adjusted to make it more obvious.

Make a more notable and exaggerated texture for the damaged belt instead of using decals.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-691

Fluid puddles under the backhoe are currently hard to notice.

Change the reflectiveness and colour of the puddles, while also increasing their size.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-692

Damage to the backhoe ROPS is difficult for the learner to see to correctly and assess properly for the inspection.

Create new decals and exaggerate existing decal usage to make damage easily notable.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-693

The names for the pistons/cylinders around the backhoe are specific/descriptive enough.

Name the pistons/cylinders more accurately to correspond to the technical name of what they are attached to.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-695

The two dipstick checks are not straightforward, and differ from the norm of other inspection points.

Click on the oil directly to get a standardised inspection UI/panel so that this inspection point is streamlined with the rest inspection.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-700

Removing debris from the swing linkage area can be difficult and also hard to notice as well.

Make debris spawn points in open locations that don’t require zoom or tons of moving around, and/or make debris larger.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-702

Toast messages were only used at a few points, users expected them throughout the inspection to aid them with inspection points once they got used to the initial ones.

Add toast messages to every inspection point to spoon feed the learner what the condition at that point is.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-708

Many components lack the associated symbols you would find on said components to help identify what they are. These symbols are also relatively universal, so they would help the learner work with other similar machinery as well.

Add the appropriate symbols to the coolant, engine oil, transmission oil, and hydraulic oil components. They can be found in the manual.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-715

If the windows were dirty and noted as such, they should be cleaned after that inspection point in order to aid the user with further tasks.

If the windows are dirty, set them back to the clean texture and mesh after the user has inspected them and noted them as dirty.

https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/HE-716

The user does not have 3 points of contact with entering/exiting the cab.

Add toast messages and/or contact points to the steps and railing of the backhoe for when the user enters/exits.
