Objective

The objective of this project is to identify requirements and a repeatable process for converting existing flat-screen CORE DLS into “room-scale” simulations for deployment in a VARLab VR space.

Keep in mind that our primary deployment strategy, using WebGL to SCORM on eConestoga for flat-screen play in a remote learning setting, remains unchanged. All simulations must be deployed using this strategy until further notice. Room-scale VR is considered an “uplift” and is NOT intended to replace flat-screen learning on eConestoga in the foreseeable future.

What do we mean by “Room-Scale”

Conestoga College roughly designates VR learning experiences into 3 categories or tiers, as follows:

First Tier

  • Requires headset and handheld remotes, desktop sensors. Roughly 1x1m (3x3ft) space.

  • Player is the centre of the VR sphere, and remains mostly stationary

  • Player can look around or turn their body, but does not move around the space

  • Items of interest are brought to the player, and virtual

  • Could be played remotely, if the player had appropriate hardware at home - does not strictly require a VR facility.

Second Tier

  • Requires wireless headset and handheld remotes or haptic apparel, room-mounted sensors and lighthouses. Roughly 3.5x3.5m (10x10ft) space.

  • Player safety considerations such as boundary enforcement and near object detection to help prevent injury

  • Object of interest is at the centre of the VR sphere, player walks around it and can interact with all sides of the object

  • May be multi-player

  • May include physical props (e.g. carved out of styrofoam and weighted) with haptic sensors.

Third Tier

  • Fully wireless free range headset with body-mounted remotes or haptic apparel. Numerous room-mounted sensors and lighthouses. Roughly 15x15m (50x50ft).

  • Free range environment with purpose built structures in the gaming space. Numerous physical props for learner to interact with.

  • Collaborative play

  • There is no “centre” of the VR sphere at this tier.

Discovery Objectives - Fall 2021

  • In partnership with XPertVR, we will convert the existing Traffic Forensic Investigation and CSI sims into Tier 2 VR experiences. A big part of this goal will be informing our design choices when creating CORE sims, to best enable them for uplift to room-scale following primary deployment to eConestoga.

  • Use the existing Hydraulics simulation as a candidate for uplift to Tier 1 VR. This is a good fit because Hydraulics labs are done at a stationary work bench, with no requirement for the player to walk around that work area.

  • Use the existing Motive Power - Hoist Inspection module as a candidate for uplift to Tier 2 Room-Scale VR. This is a good fit because it is a small work area, but requires the player to walk all around the hoist to inspect the various components.

  • Document the process to uplift an existing Unity-based or CORE DLS simulation to room-scale VR
