SUMMARY

Positive Findings

Observations = High Engagement

·         Students were working in pairs or groups, asking questions, discussing details, high engagement

·         Students were looking up code, discussing findings; one pair used Google to check code when they thought there were in-game errors

·         Few finished the DLX with no issues

Survey Findings

·         Liked the realism and practice of the game; many answered ‘nothing they didn’t like’

·         Good way to visualize, good graphics, well made

·         Liked low risk, online exploring/practice with real-world-like environment

·         95% agreed they’d use the simulation more than once as a learning tool to help them practice

·         They found most useful: selecting devices and getting supervisor feedback to be most useful, then fixing mistakes, viewing devices in scene and finally practicing looking up code.

·         Placement of boxes created more self-reported errors than code or devices (suggests our game mechanics are harder than their code and device knowledge)

·         Some would have liked: more free movement, more actions to perform (strip, drill, clean up), more details in the scene, a mouse, clearer feedback or list of done/not done

Negative Findings

Performance-Related Findings

·         Sim made computers work extremely hard, get hot, drained battery quickly, was glitchy, or in worst cases, lagged so badly or became unresponsive as to be unplayable (as was the case with the laptop cart versions)

·         Different devices performed at differing capacities, with some playing it fine, and others struggling to the point of frustration and stopping play

Design Findings

Several items in the game created points of confusion for learners. These include:

·         Try again ‘button’

·         Lack of ‘remove box’ option or instruction to that point

·         The word task list was confusing because 1. It wasn’t a list; and 2. It was an expectation of installation choices not tasks. Thought “Begin installation” or something like that would have been a better label.

·         Thought they could move around the scene; frustrated they couldn’t (instructions addressing this will help)

·         Confused what to do, where to start, where to go when they thought were done (didn’t realize needed to request feedback). Thought better (any) instructions would help.
