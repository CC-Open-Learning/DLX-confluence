This series has four DLX to help powerline students progress through their first and second year studies. The first two modules are introductory to gain familiarity with equipment and the second two modules help students work through complex and dangerous procedures.

The Powerline Technician Training Simulations series is an innovative educational journey designed to equip
powerline technician students with essential safety awareness and technical skills. Through a structured progression of thirteen modules, students begin with foundational safety orientations, such as Personal Protection Equipment (PPE) usage, and advance through more complex operational tasks, including vehicle and equipment inspections, live line tool applications, and practical procedural simulations such as the dead-end insulator change on a three-phase system. This scaffolded approach ensures a comprehensive understanding and application of critical safety protocols and procedures in a virtual, risk-free environment.

¬†Objective

  • Powerline training can present some profound occupational hazards to learners on the job.

  • Practicing through virtual reality will help students become familiar with the processes and occupational awareness to safely learn and perform their duties.

Mandatory Features

  • Accessible from Conestoga‚Äôs LMS, eConestoga (D2L ‚Äì Brightspace), and on student laptops that meet program device requirements¬†¬†

  • Simulations will be SCORM-wrapped for Conestoga LMS compatibility with the assistance of Conestoga.¬†

  • Built as (a) webapps or (b) in Unity but published to the web.¬†

  • Learning outcomes achieved through a series of tasks the students will navigate through.¬†

  • Photo capturing of equipment.¬†

  • Video capturing of the processes outlined in the simulation.¬†

  • Closed captioning of the videos.¬†

  • Development is intended for PC laptops only.¬†

  • Students will navigate by using a mouse or identified keys on a keyboard.¬†

  • Feedback will be provided to students on what they completed correctly and where they made errors and why their choices were correct or incorrect where appropriate.¬†

  • Instructor mode made available so faculty / instructors can pick the scene they would like to demonstrate in class.¬†
