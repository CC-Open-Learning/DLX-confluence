Over the course of three weeks, students collaborate on teams to help make the best decisions for one of the four industries they represent. They are given scenarios to work through, with each scenario having a cost of different resources and potential outcomes that would help mitigate the impact of climate change or worsen it. The game takes students through three decades, 2030, 2040 and 2050. Random economic, social, political and natural events may occur that may boost the success of their choices or create more challenging circumstances. This game is offered in a course that is an elective to both degree and diploma programs through Interdisciplinary Studies at the college.

Wicked problems stand out due to their complexity, lack of clear-cut solutions, and constant evolution. They demand interdisciplinary thinking, collaboration, and adaptability – skills ideally suited for VR's immersive and emotionally engaging environment. Conestoga's Centre for Virtual Reality has created an interactive, multiplayer game in which players collaborate to develop solutions to help four key industries meet climate sustainability goals over the progression of three decades. This immersive experience tackles the complex issue of climate change by gamifying the solutioning experience to challenges faced globally today. Students representing diverse industries participate in a multiplayer simulation, making impactful decisions affecting the virtual environment. This serious game empowers students to become effective changemakers and cultivate the critical and collaborative thinking needed to navigate climate change from multiple perspectives.

06 Dec 2024

The finalized build has been tested and successfully received. The implementation of a 'reset' button resolved the previous issues with player management data. We now have the source code, allowing us to work on this build in-house as needed in the future.

Out of 11 issues tested, 8 were successfully resolved, including readability problems, player dropout, view rotation issues, initial load errors, email access errors, login without a name, exiting full-screen mode, and visibility problems. There were just 3 minor issues: text spacing in the chat interface, an occasional audio glitch, and a camera glitch – that we plan to address internally in the future.
