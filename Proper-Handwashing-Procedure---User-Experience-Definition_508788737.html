VARLab : Proper Handwashing Procedure - User Experience Definition

Created by Stacey Dineen (Unlicensed), last modified on Aug 26, 2022

In this page, we will work toward a description of the learner experience when playing the DLS.

Please note that the User Experience will be identical for the branded and generic modules - the only differences will be that “VG Meats” branded media or text will be substituted for generic media or text in the generic module. This page describes the user experience for both branded and generic modules.

Introduction

The user needs an overview of what will be covered in this module. At bare minimum, this would just be a list of topics (probably with nested sub-topics) that are arranged in the order that the learner will encounter them. Ideally, the overview will help the learner identify exactly WHAT they are about to learn, WHY they need to know this information, and HOW they are expected to interact with the DLS to succeed at this training.

At high level, this module will satisfy the following learning objectives:

From email:

  • Introduction: Identify what will be covered and what is expected of the learner

  • Importance of Proper Handwashing: Describe the role of handwashing in protecting food safety and employee health and safety

  • Identify the Steps in Proper Handwashing Technique: Each step of the proper handwashing procedure will be identified, described, and (where appropriate) solid rationale will be given for each step.

  • Demonstrate the Steps in Proper Handwashing Technique: Using photo and/or video media, the learner will be shown exactly what is required for good handwashing. This segment will include very clear procedural order of the steps, and ensure that the rationale for each step and the correct execution of each step is clearly communicated.

  • Practice Proper Handwashing Technique: Using a series of multiple choice quiz questions and a drag-and-drop minigame, the student will have the opportunity to apply their understanding of the imporance of handwashing, as well as proper handwashing technique.

Module Content

This module will follow a pattern of EXPLAIN-SHOW-DO that will allow the learner to be introduced to new content, and then understand how to apply that knowledge through demonstration, followed by an opportunity to practice their new skill or knowledge right away in an interactive activity.

Most of the instructional content for this module can be found in the SharePoint workspace, in the slideshows in SharePoint “GMP FINAL.PPTX”

Segment 1 - Introduction

EXPLAIN and SHOW

Through the use of info panels and/or images or embedded video, the module will explain:

  1. Content scope: This module is intended to communicate the importance of handwashing, and how to perform this essential task correctly

  2. System requirements: Keyboard and mouse, minimum 8GB RAM, up to date web browser

  3. User participation: Learner will be required to answer questions and participate in a minigame to check their understanding of proper handwashing technique.

Segment 2 - Importance of Proper Handwashing

EXPLAIN and SHOW

Through the use of info panels and/or images or embedded video, the module will explain:

Panel #1: The Importance of Proper Handwashing Proper handwashing is the most important daily Good Manufacturing Practice that employees can perform to protect their own health, as well as food safety.

  • Everyone is familiar with washing their hands, but doing it CORRECTLY is essential

  • We think of handwashing as a single task, but it actually has many small steps that each need to be done properly for handwashing to be effective.

  • Failing to properly wash your hands, or skipping steps in this process, can result in illness, injury, product recalls, loss of customer loyalty, or facility closures. This is serious.

  • Handwashing protects workers and consumers from bacterial and chemical contamination

To progress from one panel to the next, learners must answer a skill-testing question.

Panel #1 Knowledge Check: Why is it important to wash your hands correctly?
Correct answer: Proper handwashing protects food safety and employee safety. If selected, a smaller feedback panel is shown, saying “Correct! Proper handwashing is essential for food safety and employee safety”

Incorrect answer: Handwashing should be done quickly and efficiently to save time. If selected, a smaller feedback panel is shown, saying “That’s not correct. Proper handwashing is essential for food safety and employee safety.” Action button: I understand.

Panel #2: Frequent Handwashing - Who, When, and Why?

  • Who needs to wash their hands? (Everyone who enters the food processing areas, regardless of their role)

  • Handwashing must be done frequently to be effective. Wash your hands EVERY time:

    • You enter a food processing area

    • You cough or sneeze

    • You touch anything other than the food/packaging that you are currently working with, including chemicals, cleaning agents, or allergens

    • You return to work after any type of break or interruption, even if it was very brief

    • You change your PPE

    • After eating, drinking, or using the toilet

    • When changing lines/products/packaging

    • In accordance with facility SOP

Panel #2 Knowledge Check: Should I wash my hands after my lunch break?
Correct answer: Yes, always wash your hands after every break. If selected, a smaller feedback panel is shown, saying “Correct! Even when your hands look clean, handwashing will protect against cross-contamination.” Action button: Got it!

Incorrect answer: If your hands look clean, you don’t need to wash them. If selected, a smaller feedback panel is shown, saying “That’s not correct. Even when your hands look clean, handwashing will protect against cross-contamination.” Action button: I understand.

Segment 3 - Instructional Content - Steps for Proper Handwashing

EXPLAIN and SHOW

Through the use of info panels and/or images or embedded video, the module will explain the steps required for proper handwashing:

  1. Panel #3 Prepare to wash hands:

    1. Remove jewelry, artificial nails, and non-metal-detectable bandaids (if any)

      Panel #3 Knowledge Check: Why is it important to remove jewelry?
      Correct answer: Jewelry could cause injury or contamination. If selected, a smaller feedback panel is shown, saying “Correct! All jewelry must be removed before starting work” Action button: Got it!

      Incorrect answer: Some jewelry is okay, such as wedding bands or earrings. If selected, a smaller feedback panel is shown, saying “That’s not correct. All jewelry must be removed, even if it will be covered with a hair net or gloves.” Action button: I understand.

  2. Scrub hands thoroughly for at least 20 seconds:

    1. Wet hands with warm running water

    2. Apply soap

    3. Scrub palms

    4. Interlace fingers

    5. Scrub backs of hands

    6. Scrub thumbs

    7. Clean fingernails

    8. Scrub wrists - Note that it should take at least 20 seconds to complete all scrubbing steps
      Panel #4 Knowledge Check: What should I do if the soap dispenser is empty?
      Correct answer: Use a different handwashing station or ask your supervisor. If selected, a smaller feedback panel is shown, saying “Correct! Soap is essential to remove bacteria and other soils.” Action button: Got it!

      Incorrect answer: It’s okay, use lots of water and wipe your hands clean with paper towels. If selected, a smaller feedback panel is shown, saying “That’s not correct. Soap is essential to remove bacteria and other soils.” Action button: I understand.

  3. After scrubbing:

    1. Rinse thoroughly with running water

    2. Dry with clean, single-use paper towel

    3. Apply metal-detectable bandaids (if needed) and gloves (in accordance with facility SOP)
      Panel #5 Knowledge Check: Why is it important to use single-use paper towels?
      Correct answer: Drying your hands with a towel or hot air dryer can spread bacteria. If selected, a smaller feedback panel is shown, saying “Correct! Single-use paper towels are the safest and most effective way to dry your hands.” Action button: Got it!

      Incorrect answer: A clean cloth will work just as well as a paper towel. If selected, a smaller feedback panel is shown, saying “That’s not correct. Single-use paper towels are the safest and most effective way to dry your hands.” Action button: I understand.

Note that the steps WITHIN a given phase of the procedure (Prepare, Wash, After washing) may be done in any order, as long as no steps are skipped within each phase.

DO - HANDWASHING TECHNIQUE

Drag and drop activity to put the correct steps of effective handwashing into order. These should be still photos of each step of the process, accompanied by descriptive text. Please note that for the mockup below, I didn’t have photos for these steps. It would be very preferable to have photos instead of relying on text alone, so the implemented design will need to be an upgrade from this mockup Each photo can include a short caption, but should be clear enough on its own that the text isn’t the main information.
