VARLab : Sanitation of RTE Areas - User Experience Definition

Created by Stacey Dineen (Unlicensed), last modified on Sept 20, 2022

In this page, we will work toward a description of the learner experience when playing the DLS.

Please note that the User Experience will be identical for the branded and generic modules - the only differences will be that “VG Meats” branded media or text will be substituted for generic media or text in the generic module. This page describes the user experience for both branded and generic modules.

Initial Load

On initial load, the scene will open on a 360 skybox view of the lobby or entrance of the meat processing facility. A welcome message will be displayed, as well as a voiceover greeting. At minimum, the welcome message must confirm the module name and briefly describe the purpose and scope of this module, so that the learner knows they have launched the right DLS module.

Introduction

The user needs an overview of what will be covered in this module. At bare minimum, this would just be a list of topics (probably with nested sub-topics) that are arranged in the order that the learner will encounter them. Ideally, the overview will help the learner identify exactly WHAT they are about to learn, WHY they need to know this information, and HOW they are expected to interact with the DLS to succeed at this training.

At high level, this module will satisfy the following learning objectives:

  1. What is Sanitation? - Cleaning (removing soils) + Sanitizing (removing biological hazards)

  2. Why is sanitation important? Reduce contamination hazards, product and employee safety

  3. Where to sanitize - Utensils, Food Contact Surfaces, Non-Food Contact surfaces - this will be covered under Why is sanitation important (surfaces that become contaminated must be sanitized)

  4. Wet vs Dry Sanitation - BRIEFLY cover the difference between these two processes, and provide at least one example of when each would be used. Use the source material in the SharePoint workspace, in the PPT titled “Sanitation of RTE Areas-IN PROGRESS.pptx”

  5. What are the steps of wet sanitation, and a minigame to practice this skill

Module Content

This module will follow a pattern of EXPLAIN-SHOW-DO that will allow the learner to be introduced to new content, and then understand how to apply that knowledge through demonstration, followed by an opportunity to practice their new skill or knowledge right away in an interactive activity.

EXPLAIN

Through the use of info panels and/or embedded video, the voiceover will explain what sanitation is and why it is essential to food safety. Each step of the wet sanitation process will be explained, in order, with rationale for WHY it must be done in this exact way.

SHOW

Through a series of video clips, we will demonstrate the complete process of sanitizing a piece of equipment in the RTE area.

Steps of Wet Sanitation:

The numbered steps are in the correct order, but it may be necessary to reorder the specific sub-steps within each larger step. For example, the content photos provided clearly show step 1c needs to be done before step 1b, otherwise the LOTO step would not be viewable (covered by protective plastic).

  1. Preparation

    1. Prepare YOURSELF by applying appropriate PPE - Goggles, Face shield, Chemical resistant gloves, Chemical resistant apron, Non-slip steel toe boots

    2. Prepare the AREA by putting away or covering anything that isn’t being cleaned, and removing waste or debris from the floor before getting started

    3. Lock Out Tag Out - safety first!

    4. Prepare the EQUIPMENT by disassembling as appropriate to make sure you can access all of the parts that need to be cleaned, nothing will block the water or cleaning liquids from reaching the areas to be cleaned.

  2. Pre-Rinse

    1. Top to bottom. Be methodical, don’t rush

    2. Hot water

    3. Avoid high pressure to minimize spreading bacteria and soils around the room

    4. Rinse away as much visible soil as possible, until it “looks clean”

    5. Avoid excessive water that will make the room difficult to dry. High moisture = higher risk of bacteria

  3. Cleaning

    1. Refer to company SOP to use the correct cleaning agent in the correct concentration

    2. Apply the detergent to all surfaces, working from the bottom up toward the top of the equipment. Cover every inch of the equipment surface.

    3. Scrub every surface, particularly any food contact surface, or any rough or uneven surface that could hold on to soils or bacteria.

    4. Let foam sit for 10-15 minutes, or as specified in facility SOP

  4. Post-Cleaning Rinse

    1. Removes detergent as well as all soils

    2. Top to bottom, be thorough

  5. Inspection - the learner does not perform this task, so it will not be included in the minigame. It is still very important for the overall process and will be part of the instructional segment of the module.

    1. Careful visual examination which may include swabbing or other QC testing protocols as appropriate

    2. If any residues are found, Steps 3, 4, and 5 must be repeated until the inspection passes

  6. Sanitizing

    1. Surface must be completely free of soils before sanitizing so that sanitizer solution can be in direct contact with the surface it needs to work on

    2. Refer to company SOP to use the correct sanitizing agent in the correct concentration. VERY important - incorrect concentration could cause chemical or biological contamination!

    3. Flood all surfaces with the sanitizer solution, working from bottom to top

    4. Wait for 1 minute, or as specified in facility SOP. Some sanitizer solutions may need to sit longer than others, and some need to be rinsed off after a specific time period. VG Meats uses a QUAT 400 solution that does not require rinsing, so we will not rinse the sanitizer in our minigame. We will specify that facility SOP should be followed to ensure sanitizer is used properly.

    5. Squeegee excess sanitizer and any other liquid to the floor drains - the room should be as dry as possible when completed. We will not use our conveyor belt photos for this step, because the floor and floor drain are not visible in those shots. The squeegee step will be covered in the instructional segment, but may not be included in the minigame.

  7. Documentation - refer to company SOP to correctly record all details of sanitation procedure completed. For the “branded” version of the module that will be delivered to VG Meats, we can show images of their own documentation (although we will not go into specifics about how to fill it out).

DO

The learner will participate in a “scratch off lotto ticket” minigame, where they must use the mouse cursor to transform a piece of equipment (e.g. a conveyor belt) from dirty to fully sanitized and ready for use.

Our minigame will involve stacked photos of the equipment being sanitized, and is not planned to include any images for steps that do not involve that equipment (e.g. apply PPE, squeegee the walls and floor, fill out documentation). It is understood that the those steps will be covered in the teaching portion of the sim (EXPLAIN-SHOW) but will not need to be practiced in the minigame (DO)

Full details of this minigame can be found in ONB-81 ONB-81 - As a learner, I need a scratch-off photo transformation interaction so that I can engage with learning content Done

We need photos of the selected piece of equipment in each phase of the process, so that we can layer them and create the desired effect.
