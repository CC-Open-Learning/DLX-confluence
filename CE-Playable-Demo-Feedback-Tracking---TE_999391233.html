Student Feedback about Supply Cable to Boxes - Knowledge Check questions and feedback:

  • In the student feedback from the focus group, the following was noted:

    Chris confirmed that the answers in the DLX are correct, and explained this feedback by saying that students often forget two things when they are cutting or unsheathing cable:
    1. They need to create a 12” service loop before they clamp the cable in the box, and they need at least 8-10” of cable past the clamp, so that’s why the correct answer is to cut the cable 16” past the box.
    2. They need 6-7” of free conductor from the box face, and the clamp is at the back of the box. The box is typically about 1.5 - 3” deep, so you have to add the depth of the box when you’re choosing how much sheathing to remove. 8-10” is the correct amount of sheathing to remove if the box is 2-3” deep.

  • Chris agreed to review the questions, options, and feedback to make sure that everything is presented accurately and with the right level of detail. No immediate changes were identified, but Chris will think it over and suggest changes if he sees fit.
