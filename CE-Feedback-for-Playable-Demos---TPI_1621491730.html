  • Inspection of the service areas does not need to include live demonstration of setting up a procedure tray - the important aspects of that inspection are

Review Proposed Environment

  • Separate rooms for piercing and tattoos are good

11 FEB 2025 - CE feedback from: Ken Diplock

This week, the meeting focused on a review the scope planning document PHI – Tattoo and Piercing Studio Inspection Planning.docx and a discussion about how to manage various inspection scenarios in a logical and meaningful way. No playable demos yet, project is just getting started.

Stakeholder Feedback

Jira Ticket Number(s)

No NPC’s

  • We decided as a group that it will not be necessary to have any NPC’s in this inspection DLX.

  • Interactions with the practictioner or manager will be handled the same way as the introduction task in the PHI Restaurant Inspection DLX - a cardboard cutout can be used to represent a person that the PHI needs to interact with, and then dialogue between the player and the interactable character will be displayed on screen in text while a voiceover is playing.

  • There is no need to actually see a tattoo or piercing happening during the inspection.

Building the Art Asset Requirements

  • Planning document has been updated with great references to the Guide, and some examples of how to inspect in-game

  • Need more concrete examples of inspectable objects and what types of non-compliances are important for students to see

  • Autoclave - Ken will arrange a visit to Doon campus for us to see/photograph a tabletop autoclave in the skills lab there. Technicians can demonstrate the different inspections associated with sterilizing instruments using the autoclave. There are many inspections associated with an autoclave, each item is a separate inspection

    • Log book

    • Test strips

    • Indicator tape

    • Printout from autoclave

    • Correct type of instruments/supplies being sterilized

  • Sharps Container - No specific type/style is required, but it must be intact, properly labeled, and not overfilled (fill line on side). There should be multiple containers around the work stations, student can inspect each one.

  • Used Equipment Container - Can be any container, but it must have a lid and be appropriate size for the items it needs to hold.

  • Porfolio book - This is a book of photos that show the types of tattoo and piercing options that customers can get from this artist/studio. The PHI inspects the book to ensure that the practitioner is not offering any unsafe or illegal services, such as surgical body modifications like tongue splitting or subcutaneous implants, or tattooing the eyeballs. All pictures in this Portfolio book will be rated PG13, even though the photos are often very NSFW in real life!

  • Autoclave Log Book - This is a physical book that may contain handwritten entries, test strips, and/or printed ‘receipts’ from the autoclave, intended to demonstrate the maintenance and calibration status of the autoclave. Failure to produce this log book or failure to follow routine documentation practices for autoclave use/maintenance is a non-compliance.

  • Procedure setup or walkthrough - NO live procedures during the inspection, but the PHI can request the practitioner to set up for a specific procedure (e.g. cartilage piercing, lobe/soft tissue piercing, tattoo) or walkthrough a specific procedure (e.g. reprocessing of sterile instruments). We will make sure that these inspections focus strictly on the objects/equipment/supplies, with almost zero focus on the person doing the setup or procedural walkthrough. We might just see hands, when necessary. Actions (walkthrough) can be shown as a cutscene, finishing with a POI that can be inspected one item at a time. Procedure setup can just focus on the end result vs the process of setting up - the PHI can judge if the setup was correct by inspecting the items that have been set up. We don’t need to watch the supplies being added to a tray, for example, but the PHI can inspect each item once the tray is set up.

No Jira tickets yet - we are building documentation and ideas. Everything needs to fit into our existing workflow and UI, so this is a great opportunity to discuss how that will happen.

Equipment Orientation - Possible Future Scope Expansion

  • Great discussion about the fact that (unlike the restaurant inspection) some of the equipment that must be inspected at a tattoo and piercing studio is quite specialised and would be unfamiliar to many PHI or anyone that is not in the tattoo industry. Examples of specialized equipment might include tattoo machines, inks, piercing implements, etc.

  • Ken really enjoyed the RT Equipment Orientation and AMB “bag explosion” interactions, and pointed out that students in the BEPH program (as well as professional PHI’s who may be unfamiliar with some of this equipment) could really benefit from an equipment orientation. The goal of this activity would be to familiarise inspectors with the proper names and intended purpose of various pieces of specialised equipment. Using the “explode” animation (both forward and in reverse) would demonstrate how various pieces of equipment are assembled and disassembled, so that an inspector could become familiar with all the pieces and fittings. Everyone agreed this would be a very nice add-on to the TPI project, but it was also acknowledged that this is outside of the scope of the project charter, so we can’t really consider it until after we have satisfied all of the charter requirements. Great idea! We are just going to put a pin in it for now and see how things go.
