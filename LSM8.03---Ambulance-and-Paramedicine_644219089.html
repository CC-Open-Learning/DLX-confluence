Dec 18, 2023

Meeting with Sana, Corey, Ian and Balazs. Design decisions are below.

Title: Seek and Find

Objective: Find and select the correct object from a list of X items, using hints to guide the learner.

Start Game:

  • An intro is provided to the learner on what to expect or what must the learner do

  • Display a message with a scenario of what the player must do

Display Object List:

  • Present a list of objects the player needs to find.

Hint:

  • Click on the hint icon for each object to be found

  • Hint content focused on location not purpose of use.

  • Hints enabled in the Object list for Items NOT found

  • Hints disabled in the Object list for items FOUND

Correct Object Selected:

  • Highlight green on the object.

  • Item checked green in the Object list

  • Display a success message.

  • SFX for success

Incorrect Object Selected:

  • Highlight red on the object.

  • Display an error message.

  • SFX for error

05 Dec 2023

Design meeting with Corey, concluded that we will stick with a seek and find of one item at a time. It is fine if we want to present “collections” of items that relate to a given situation or task, this can enhance learning, but the game mechanic of finding one item per task prompt will be maintained. This will enable to us to give meaningful feedback for each attempt a student makes to find a given item, instead of just having them click on everything until they win.

Great expansion ideas from the CE team, we have some great goals for future projects that could continue to build on the Ambulance Orientation experience! Suggestions for more advanced interactions such as drag & drop to sort equipment and supplies or pack a bag (perhaps with a time limit), and inventory-focused games such as What’s Missing from this Cabinet? (multichoice select) would be great options to continue to engage students as they move past the initial equipment orientation. Those games are outside the scope of this project, but can be kept for a future project!

Sept 19, 2023

After meeting with CE, we determined that the orientation experience will be divided into two separate phases:

Explore

Objective: For the student to gain familiarity with the ambulance/equipment through free exploration and helpful tips.

  • Learner begins in free-roaming exploration mode. Spawn in first person POV at the exterior of the parked ambulance. Students are able to walk freely around the ambulance.

  • Voiceover welcomes the students and explains how to move around (simple in-game tutorial). Voice prompts provide high-level directions without assigning specific tasks (start by exploring the exterior, etc)

  • While exploring, the student can click or mouseover equipment to receive a more detailed panel containing photo(s) and information about that equipment.

  • A progress bar/checklist will indicate when students have explored all items within an exploration area (exterior, patient bay, cab interior). Students will be able to see progress from 0-100% completion for each exploration area, so they understand when to continue exploring vs when they have seen everything and are ready to move on.

  • When progress bar is complete, a voice prompt will direct students to the next exploration area (patient bay, cab interior)

Seek and Find

  • After exploration is complete, voice prompt will ask the student if they are ready to begin the seek and find game- If yes, they move into game at beginner level (default). If no, they can continue exploring (will need option to join game when ready)

  • Voice prompt will confirm that this is a self-assessment game- not for grades.

  • Level I - Beginner: Students receive the name of an item that needs to be located and then they must navigate to the correct area and storage location for that item and click on the item to “find” it. Feedback is provided (Correct, that is the item or No, that is not the item). At this level, the student will be asked to locate larger, more frequently used, or easier to find items around the ambulance, such as the patient gurney, PPE items, paramedic bags, spinal boards, communications equipment, etc.

  • If they find all items, it can unlock intermediate/hard challenges.

  • Level II - Intermediate: Same as Level I, but now we will ask the students to locate/identify items that are harder to find. This will include prompts to find specific items (specific size/type/use) when similar items are present (similar name or similar appearance), or items that are harder to find because they are stored inside of or behind something else. For example, a specific suction tip, 4x4 gauze, pediatric NEBS, ECG paper, etc.

  • Level III - Advanced: At this level, we will require the student to collect all of the items that they need for a specific task - without telling the student which items are needed! This could include scenarios such as pediatric intubation, overdose, MVC, etc. These would need to be developed with the CE’s and must only include scenarios that have very clear equipment requirements. Instead of requiring the student to identify every individual item that they would need, we can look for higher-level knowledge of what to grab, such as Trauma Bag (not each item in the trauma bag). This is a great idea but it will need much more specific input from the CE’s and ID’s before we could implement it with confidence.

DLX Pre-Flight Pillars

Concept & Learning Objectives

At the concept level, this project appears to be a virtual ambulance learning environment where some scripted tasks will take place. Depending on the specific learning objectives chosen, the models and props would need to be more or less detailed and interactable. For a virtual vehicle circle inspection, we would need a very high degree of detail and articulation, while a virtual soft-skills practice scenario would not require much interaction with the 3D model.

Remaining questions:

  1. What are the learning objectives for this DLX?

  2. What kind of feedback or guidance should the learner have?

  3. How hands-on will the learner interactions be? What are the tasks required to complete the scenario, at the most basic level?

Assumptions / Known Constraints

  • Simulations developed under this plan are not intended to be played on tablets or cell phones.

  • Simulations developed on this charter are not guaranteed to have full functionality on devices that do not meet standard program device requirements.

  • Simulations developed under this charter are not intended to replace the teaching of the learning content, but rather augment it. They are intended to be part of formative learning experiences, not formal assessments. The simulation will not report any grade to the LMS, or provide any lasting record of the student’s performance outside of the current user session.

  • These simulations will not be designed to play with VR headsets or controllers.

  • These will not be multiplayer simulations (i.e. students playing in pairs or the professor having the opportunity to be in the simulation with the student at the same time).

  • Completion status will be recorded ONLY for the currently logged-in student in the LMS. If students work in groups at a shared computer, only one of them will show as having completed the simulation.

Asset Notes

At the time of delivery, the ambulance asset is polished and accurate, but very incomplete. For example, essential equipment such as the cab console, communcations equipment, overhead console. The specific equipment and interactable attributes would be driven by learning objectives and learner path.
