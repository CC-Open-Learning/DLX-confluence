What is a Surface Grinder and what is it used for?

Surface grinders are used on flat surfaces to create smooth finishes and accurate sizes by utilizing a rotating abrasive grinding wheel.

The part is stationary and is moved about by the table.

Major Parts

  • Coolant On/Off

  • Clamp Status

    • This MUST be illuminated before any grinding can occur.

  • Magnetic Vise Control

  • Wheel Spindle On/Off

  • E-Stop

  • Table Traverse Control

  • Main Power Switch

  • Magnetic Table

  • Grinder wheel

Main Hazards

  • Before using the machine, check to see if the grinder wheel is damaged by turning the wheel with your finger. If the grinder wheel is damaged, it is not safe to used.

  • You may need to open the door housing the grinder wheel to thoroughly check it, or it may be accidentally left open by a repair technician. Never use the Surface Grinder while the door is open.

  • When placing a work piece on the grinder’s magnetized table, there should be as many of the lines on the table touching the piece as possible.

    • Correct

    • Also correct

    • Incorrect

  • Like any other machine, make sure the Surface Grinder is free of debris and metal dust before starting your work.

  • The coolant faucet should be correctly placed so that it just touches the grinder wheel. Otherwise, it will get fluid all over the work table, or worse, jam up the grinder wheel.

    • Correct

    • Incorrect

  • Make sure the table is always moving while the grinder wheel is touching your work piece. The grinder wheel gets very hot, and the piece can expand and fly off the table.

  • The table’s magnet is very strong, so be sure to keep any metal or metal devices, such as a watch or smartphone, away from the table.
