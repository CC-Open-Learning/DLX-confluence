DOZ-89 - Ground Leak Check Done

There are two inspections for this epic. Both inspections require 2 pieces of media. One photo will show the dozer with no puddles while the second photo will show a puddle formed by oil.

The learner wants to see if there are any puddles or liquid underneath to make sure there isnt a leak that sat overnight and fluids drained out of the machine

Ground Leak Check

  • Look behind the blade on the ground

  • Go to the back of the dozer and look underneath for any wet spots or anything leaking

  • Continue looking at the dozer to make sure there are no visible problems

Begin the sim by panning the camera up to the dozer and go to the front where the blade is and have the camera look behind

Then pan to the other side and walk around to the back and look underneath

  • Front of the dozer

    • DOZ-129 - Puddle from Leaking fluid (front) - No Puddles Done

    • DOZ-128 - Manipulating Image to create: Puddle due to Leaks - Dozer Front Done

  • Rear of the dozer

    • DOZ-130 - Puddle from Leaking fluid (rear) - No Puddles Done

    • DOZ-132 - Manipulating Image to create: Puddle due to leaks - Dozer Rear Done

DOZ-92 - Engine Compartment Done

This epic will contain the first 2D inspection of the simulation. Workflow for 2D Inspection details the workflow and how to create a 2D inspection. There are several inspections located within the engine compartment. This will also be the first epic that will make use of a video for pre-inspection viewing. Adding Pre-Inspection Videos to Inspections is a good resource for learning how to implement it.

The learner wants to check the fluids and the engine condition.

When looking at the coolant, the above compartment may need to be opened and the learner will look inside it to check the levels.

Engine Compartment (DS)

  • Open it up

  • Check the access panel safety brace

  • Check the oil dipstick

  • Look at the rest of the engine areas

    • Wiring

    • Fuel/Water separator

    • Any wet spots that shouldn't be there

    • Should notice dirt and goo but thats normal

    • Fan Belt, Cables

    • Fan shroud

    • Air filter (squeeze to let out excess dirt)

    • Coolant tank

      • Hard to see if coolant is correct so we look up top and open the compartment and look down on it

For the dipstick, well show a video of checking the oil level

Everything else can be clicked

We can possibly show the video of needing to check the coolant from the top. We need to open the cap and check inside if we cant see it from the outside (ONLY DO IT IF ITS COLD)

DOZ-97 - Undercarriage (DS & PS) Done

Undercarriage (PS)

  • Check Sprocket, it should be blunt

  • Pins and bushings

  • Check Rollers

  • Check the bolts on the final drive (look for any leaks or fluids. If theyre coming loose youll see a shiny spot under the bolts)

Some labels for track tension checking will be on the side of the vehicle, others will be located in the owners manual

Pins and bushings: Wear takes place where the bushing engages with the root of the sprocket. That engagement is where the majority of the wear takes place. We need to make sure were watching and observing so they dont fail.

  • If there is oil leaking from the rollers then youll need to talk to a mechanic about it. If oil leaks then they could seize.

  • For track tension, the student will need to move the dozer according to the instructions

  • Maybe we can have a game where they select where they can find the label

DOZ-146 - Label Instructions Done

Once warmed up

  • Check track tension

    • Label will mention how to check track tension

  • If everything is checked an books have been signed off, last step is moving machine around, stopping it, doing a measurement of the tracks

    • This will involve following the track instructions on the label

    • Lay a board or something with a straight edge from the front of the track to the center and measure down and make sure its within the tolerance that the operators manual says.

The track check would take place after youve run the machine for a bit and got it going to loosen everything up so it hasn't been sitting.
