For each playable demo, we will record actionable feedback and provide a link to the Jira ticket(s) where the feedback is added to the project backlog. Not every feedback ticket will be resolved prior to GR, but we will easily see a record of the date the feedback was received and traceability in Jira.

This page was only created in later stages of the project, so earlier demos are not recorded in this format.

08 MAY 2024 - CE approval for GR from: Adam Jarosz

Builds GR SCORM Modules

This meeting was not a traditional playable demo, since Adam had already had the GR candidate modules since 04MAY2024 and had played through them all prior to the meeting with no suggested changes for us. Instead of reviewing the GR builds, we focused on reviewing the Release Notes, particularly the Known Issues section, and discussing post-release deployment plans. Key points summarized below:

  • Student feedback surveys for Labs 1-5 were reviewed - 22 responses in all and results were overwhelmingly positive. Other than feedback from 2 students who observed that the DLX is not playable on an iPad, and one student who believes that the layout of the trainer board is not optimal and should be rearranged, the students felt that the DLX modules were easy to use, helpful, had excellent learning value, and did not have any quality issues. Many students commented that they would appreciate more of a free-play opportunity and a greater challenge, which is a strong indicator that we were right on track with Labs 6-9 (which these survey participants had not had an opportunity to play).

  • Release Notes contained a terminology error in the Known Issues section, for MPC-755. The document should say “current flow” instead of “resistance” when describing the type of measurement in question. This problem was quickly fixed during the meeting and the review was completed at that time.

  • The model course shell was reviewed, and a typo was identified on one page where the manufacturer of the trainer board was incorrectly spelled as “Compulab” when it should be “Consulab”. This was quickly fixed during the meeting and I searched all other pages and the Release Notes to check for that error.

  • I explained that the General Release of the Circuilt Builder 1.0 modules had been approved by VLT (and Adam confirmed his approval again in the meeting) so I confirmed that the modules were now available in the DLX model course shell. I confirmed that I would send an email to Adam, Kevin Bell, and Bill Adams to formally announce the release and provide links and documentation to help with deployment. I specified that any other faculty or staff that require access to the model shell just need to be added to the classlist and they will immediately have copy access. I confirmed that I remain available by Teams, email, or by phone to discuss any questions or concerns that might come up post-release.

  • Confirmed that the Playable Demo course shell would be taken down (we reuse them for other projects).

  • Confirmed that our usual interval for checking back with a program/school for opportunities to refresh or expand on their DLX content is approx 12-18 months, or whenever needed.

  • The meeting concluded with lots of thanks and appreciation all around, I confirmed that there would be no more scheduled meetings after today, but that Adam or any member of his team could reach out anytime.

02 APR 2024 - CE feedback from: Adam Jarosz

Builds MPC-24W6.2-28MAR2024-Circuits Lab 6-Development.zip and MPC-24W6.2-28MAR2024-Circuits Lab 7-Development.zip and MPC-24W6.2-28MAR2024-Circuits Lab 8-Development.zip

Stakeholder Feedback

Jira Ticket Number(s)

Lab 6 (and a bit of Lab 7) - The mathematically calculated amperage values for one lightbulb in Lab 6 is 200mA in the digital twin, but in real life it would be slightly higher. This is because we assumed 50 Ohms of resistance for each bulb, but the actual resistance of an incandescent bulb will vary based on the brightness of the bulb.  This difference is not much of a concern for one light bulb because it is within the expected range. However, when there are 2 light bulbs connected in series, they are sharing the source voltage and are therefore dimmer, therefore reducing their resistance.  Each light is approx 33 Ohms of resistance when there are 2 connected in series.  This ends up with an inaccurate result when you do the lab on the digital twin vs the real life trainer board, which can confuse students.

The same problem affects Lab 7, but it is less noticeable because the bulbs are connected in parallel instead of in series. Solving the resistance for Lab 6 would also solve it for Lab 7.

MPC-708 - L1 and L2 are using a static resistance value of 50 ohms, but this is not realistic. Incorrect measurements in Lab 6 and 7. Done

Stacked jacks - In real life, students move stacked jacks at the same time, by grabbing the bottom jack and moving both jacks at once to a different location.  In the digital twin, it isn't possible to move stacked jacks together, so this requires a different procedure where leads have to be removed instead of being moved.

We don't need to address this in the digital twin, Adam may choose to update the procedure in the lab worksheet, just to make it clear that you can't move the stacked jacks at once in the DLX.

We can consider moving stacked jacks in a future release - Jira ticket added to the backlog in the Future Considerations epic.

MPC-710 - As a learner, I need to be able to move stacked jacks together, instead of moving them one at a time To Do

Lab 8 - Playing around with it, the digital twin results were mostly what you would expect.  However, in Lab 8 the lights at the beginning look totally dark, but they should be DIMLY LIT.

David: There is already a change coming in the next demo that will improve the visible brightness of the bulbs

MPC-615 - Light bulbs at L1 and L2 need to be brighter and have more realistic appearance Done

MPC-636 - As a learner, I want to see the filament of the bulb illuminated when voltage is applied so that I know that the bulb is on even at low voltages Done

L3 LED light bulb discussion - Adam reports that on the latest version of the physical trainer board, L3 is NOT POLARIZED, it works both ways.  The other diode at D1 IS POLARIZED as indicated by the jack colours, so these are not the same.

L3 has 4 bulbs in total, 2 on either side (2 lows and 2 highs), it will dim when you lower the voltage and brighten when you increase the voltage.

Visually, the difference between LO and HI should be represented on the digital twin.  Adam will send photos of 50% intensity and 100% intensity.  The bulb is Sylvania 3157 LED, the two metal pieces are deflectors.  The photos will show how they appear on the real life trainer board.

MPC-682 - As a learner, I want the dual circuit LED (L3) to function as a diode so that I can properly connect it to a larger circuit Done

Components not implemented for 1.0 Release:

Discussed the Transistors (T1 and T2) and PWM signal generator components on the trainer board, and confirmed that these are not planned to be functional for the 1.0 release. Rationale: These components are not required for any of Labs 1-9, and are too complex for us to take on in the last few weeks before release.

Adam agreed that these components should be disabled in the 1.0 release so that it is not possible for students to try to use them when building circuits on the digital twin.

Adam also pointed out that these components would be valuable for the Level 2 students, so it would definitely be great if they were included in a future release. All tickets for T1, T2, and PWM signal generator have been moved to the Future Considerations epic.

MPC-527 - As a learner, I want to use the NPN transistor (T1) in circuits on the trainer board so that I can understand how this type of transistor works To Do

MPC-528 - As a learner, I want to use the PNP transistor (T2) in circuits on the trainer board so that I can understand how this type of transistor works To Do

MPC-713 - As a learner I want to use the PWM signal generator so that I can understand how it works in a circuit To Do

MPC-712 - As a learner I want to only connect leads to ports on working components, so that I am not confused when I try to use a component that doesn't work in the simulation Done
