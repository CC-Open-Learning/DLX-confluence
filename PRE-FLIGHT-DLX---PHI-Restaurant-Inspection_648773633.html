DLX Pre-Flight Pillars

Concept & Learning Objectives

This project will be a virtual public health inspection of a restaurant, with the learner acting in the role of public health inspector.

  1. Flat screen DLX - will be used online and must be accessible outside of class hours, therefore flatscreen web deployment is required.

  2. DLX must have Save & Load feature, so that the studentâ€™s progress is automatically saved as they play the simulation. If the student exits before completing the scenario, they are offered the option to Continue from where they left off or Start from beginning the next time they log in. If a student has never played before, or if they completed their scenario on their previous session, they will not need to make a choice - the scenario will load from the beginning.

  3. The inspection procedure is more of a checklist than a sequence, in that there is no specific order or priority for tasks to be done. The student can navigate freely to any area of the facility, in any order, and complete any number of tasks in each area, in any order. There are two mandatory tasks that the player must complete before they can begin the inspection. If the user does not perform both of these tasks (without being prompted) before they begin the inspection, the game will end and they will need to start over from the beginning:

    1. Identify themselves as a public health inspector, to establish that they have authority to enter

    2. Wash their hands

  4. No task prompts. It is up to the student to perform the inspection as they see fit, just like in real life.

  5. Tools available at all times: The student will be provided with the following tools in the DLX, and can decide to use them at any time, for any purpose, according to the following functionality:

    1. Flashlight - to illuminate dark areas such as behind appliances or under tables.

    2. Camera - to capture photos in game. All photos can be reviewed in game so that the student can determine if the photo is appropriate. Camera tool must be able to zoom and pan. Images captured in the game must be available to the student (e.g. sent by email, downloadable to local drive or Dropbox, etc.) at the conclusion of the user session. All captured photos must be retained as part of Save & Load. After completing the scenario, the photos are no longer retained in the game and the student cannot download/export them again.

    3. Probe Thermometer - to take a temperature reading. The student does not have to operate the thermometer, they just select the probe thermometer and click on the item that they want to assess. A temperature reading will be shown in the format of XXX.X (up to 3 whole numbers followed by one decimal place) in degrees Celcius.

    4. Infrared (IR) Thermometer - to take a temperature reading. The student does not have to operate the thermometer, they just select IR thermometer and click on the item they wish to assess. The temperature reading will be shown in the format of XXX.X (up to 3 whole numbers followed by one decimal place) in degrees Celcius.

  6. This DLX will focus on decision-based procedural learning (e.g. choose WHAT to do, such as take a temperature reading or a photo) as opposed to more tactical learning (e.g. HOW to properly wash your hands or take a temperature). The student will decide all of the following:

    1. Where to start the inspection and how to proceed (there is no prescribed order for tasks, other than the 2 mandatory starting tasks identified above)

    2. Which tools to use (camera, probe thermometer, IR thermometer) for which tasks - all tools are available all the time

    3. How to record the results of the inspection - the student may choose to open a different application (e.g. Hedgehog, Notepad) in another tab or window, or physically write down their findings in a paper notebook, or any method that suits them. The DLX does not provide any way to record inspection findings, this is entirely up to the student.

  7. Scorecard-style feedback at the end of the inspection. The student will decide when the inspection is completed, they will need to click to end the inspection. When they end the inspection (confirm that action) we can show a scorecard of their inspection results. We need to workout exactly how much feedback is appropriate, since the user is generally recording their findings outside of the game and we canâ€™t evaluate if they are correct or incorrect. At the very least, we can know what you clicked (every location you navigated to, every photo, every temperature) but we wonâ€™t be able to tell what you noticed or didnâ€™t notice, or if your findings were correct.
