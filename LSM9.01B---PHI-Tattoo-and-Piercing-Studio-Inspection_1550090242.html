Type of Solution

Procedural - Flatscreen Web

PoV - First person

In the BEPH program, students benefit from the opportunity to practice their inspection skills in a variety of different environments, but it can be difficult to arrange real-world inspection opportunities for all students at local facilities. Students might not get a chance practice as often as they would like, or have exposure to different types of inspection environments, to build their confidence prior to graduation. Furthermore, the facilities that are most likely to offer practice inspections to students are likely to be highly compliant with public health codes, leaving students with little opportunity to find and document significant non-compliances until after they have graduated.

The objective of this DLX is to provide a virtual workspace for students in the BEPH program to practice their public health inspection skills and procedures. This is an unguided DLX that is intended for students in their final stages of the program - the DLX does not provide any instructions or feedback to guide the inspection process or validate the inspection results. The primary objectives for this DLX are to provide an immersive and realistic personal services facility for students to inspect, including realistic health code non-compliances that students can learn to locate and identify visually. The DLX will provide downloadable results (including a comprehensive log of all inspections performed, a record of all locations visited, and all photos taken with the in-game camera tool) so that the student can use those results to generate a Hedgehog report. The course instructor will review the Hedgehog report and DLX inspection results with the student to provide coaching and mentorship. Only the instructor will judge the correctness or errors of the DLX inspections - the DLX does not provide any feedback about any inspection, this is by design.

This DLX must be accessible to students for asynchronous use over the web, so flatscreen web deployment is required. The DLX must be designed for use on the LYOD hardware, as specified in Sept 2024 on this webpage Lease Your Own Device.   • Ken identified a possible scope expansion point that could be considered after charter requirements are met, should time and resources permit: Equipment Orientation. Unlike our Restaurant inspection, where all of the inspectable items are pretty well known to the public, the Tattoo & Piercing Studio has inspectable items that are very specialized, which the PHI is unlikely to be very familiar with. If the DLX included just a few explorable items in an Equipment Orientation module or minigame, the PHI could gain familiarity and confidence with the tattoo and piercing equipment. The goal is that PHI students would know the name and purpose/intended use for each part of specialized equipment such as a tattoo gun or autoclave. There were no requirements related to equipment orientation in the original project charter, so this would be new scope that would require approval from CVRI-LT before we could add it to this project.

Objectives for PHI - Tattoo and Piercing Studio Inspection

The objectives for this DLX is defined in the Project Charter as follows:
The student will:

  1. Practice independent, self-guided inspection of a personal services facility (in this release, it will be a Tattoo and Piercing Studio) to build confidence and familiarity with the inspection process and decision-making framework they have already learned in the program.   

  2. Practice finding and identifying a variety of relevant public health non-compliances

  3. Apply their skillset independently and effectively to scenarios they are likely to encounter in profressional practice

  4. Download comprehensive DLX results on demand for use in other learning activities in the program.

Assumptions / Known Constraints

  • Simulations developed under this plan are not intended to be played on tablets or cell phones.

  • Simulations developed on this charter are not guaranteed to have full functionality on devices that do not meet standard program device requirements.

  • Simulations developed under this charter are not intended to replace the teaching of the learning content, but rather augment it. They are intended to be part of formative learning experiences, not formal assessments. The simulation will not report any grade to the LMS, or provide any lasting record of the student’s performance outside of the current user session.

  • These simulations will not be designed to play with VR headsets or controllers, even if demonstration versions of the DLX are provided that are compatible with VR headsets. Demo builds are never intended for use in the program, and are for promotional or research purposes only.

  • These will not be multiplayer simulations (i.e. students playing in pairs or the professor having the opportunity to be in the simulation with the student at the same time).
