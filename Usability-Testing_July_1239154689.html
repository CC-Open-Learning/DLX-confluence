Created by Sana Javeed, last modified on Feb 06, 2025

Date: July 24, 2024

This report contains details for testing the Trades Electricals DLX with 3 students in-person.

It was a 1: 1 interview and usability test to help us better understand the users. The usability test took 45 mins to 1 hour from set up to wrap up and conclusion. This report aims to summarize and analyze the feedback, highlighting key areas of improvement, positive aspects and issues observed.

Electrical Techniques Program User Testing Summary

Program Details

  • Program: Electrical Techniques, First Semester

  • Average Rating on Video Game Experience (1-10): 4

Observations

  • Device Usage: All three users used the trackpad instead of the provided mouse.

  • Tutorial/Onboarding: Needs a storytelling aspect or title to indicate what is expected to be done. Tutorial needed on how to navigate using floor targets.

  • Task Completion: All three users completed tasks without navigating through nav targets.

  • Starting Point: Users began with the project specs.

  • Task Menu:

    • User 1 completed the task of supplying the cable between the fan box and gang box, but preferred freedom to choose the next task when completing a task. It should show the current task that was completed ( current behavior, the next tab for the light box and gang box appears)

    • Users relied on text descriptions along with visuals. Some visuals were not clear. However, it was not a hindrance and the description supported their decision making.

Preferences and Actions

  • Task changes:

    • 2 out of 3 users made changes through the task menu.

    • All 3 Users preferred clear indications for interactive elements in the scene.

  • Measurement Preferences: Users preferred millimeters as default

  • Camera Control: Users were unaware that click and drag meant panning the camera. One user on a Mac expected double tap for panning.

  • Project Specs: Frequent reference to project specs during box mounting.

  • Supervisor Feedback

    • User 1 expected live feedback, but found the current behavior logical as it mirrors real-life practices , and they are okay with the feedback being old as long as it’s acknowledged.

    • User 2 got it all right in the first pass

    • User 3 clicked on “ask feedback again” for every box / location changed where the options seemed to be reducing.( I guess this is a bug)

Positive

  • The simulation is a helpful learning and study tool.

  • The animations in the scene

  • Zooming camera capability to get a closer look at the installation

  • The simulation encourages independent thinking rather than leading users.

Suggestions/ Improvements

  • More information about switches (to know that they have to install the gang box).

  • Rename "Tasks" to "Tasks & Tools" as Tasks are [actually] Project Specs.[Ours labelled Tasks] is the tools for the job.

  • Users expected project specs dialog to close when clicking on the “Task List” menu.

  • Would prefer clearer marking for entrances in the room layout specs.

  • Would also prefer clearer visual marking on the layout floor that reflects the blueprints for better understanding.

  • Clear rationale needed for task menu selections and options.

  • Suggested renaming “homerun to gang box” task to “feed cable” or “back to panel.”

  • Use millimeters for measurements like service loop instead of cm

Bugs

  • User moved the fan box but it still appeared in the old location.

  • Camera positioning was incorrect during cable routing selection.

  • Project specs dialog was cut off and expand button was not easily accessible for one user. Slow response in target points interaction and scene navigation. Will attach the device specs

  • Outlet box showed incorrect wire routing to the panel.

  • User able to ask for feedback for every box/location change that led to reduced options

Research Goals and Objectives

Research Goals

Research Questions

Answers

Ease of use & Navigation (User Experience)

Find out how do students navigate using the DLX in order to complete tasks. How successful are they and how do they go about completing tasks?

Can the students navigate independently? Is it intuitive to move around and how do you find the speed of movement?

 

With what ease or difficulty are the students able to complete the levels in the DLX? Where do they get stuck?

 

Promotes learning (Instructional Design)

 Discover the aspects of design that support their learning achieved from the DLX

Are the students able to understand what is needed to be done to achieve the learning outcomes?

 

How would they use this resource? How would this help them with their learning?

What would be the frequency at which they would use this DLX? which parts of it are helpful?

 

Engagement Factor (Game Design)

 Understand how users stay engaged to complete an inspection while also determining the pain points encountered.

Do they understand the controls that are being used in the DLX? Do they have any pain points?

 

How engaged are they in the Explore ALL Level before they move to the next level?

 

 

What would keep them motivated to play the levels?

NOTE TO FACILITATOR & OBSERVERS:

WHAT TO DO

WHAT NOT TO DO

 

 

Being friendly and smiling when talking to them

Interrupting them way too often while they are doing the task

OR chatting with them

Observe the reactions and make notes at what point do they feel - frustrated, happy or confused

Helping them click on what they are supposed to do

Asking them follow-up questions when you see a reaction or a struggle OR ask them if everything is what they were expecting

 

When they are stuck, ask them questions like:

  • Could you explain more on how you got here?

  • What do you think should be the next step?

When they are stuck,

  • oh very simple. here it is.

  • Do you think it is a good idea?

If they are still stuck and are not able to proceed, then

  • Do you see anything on the screen that would help with you what you are maybe looking for?

  • Do you think there was a better way to approach or an intuitive way to represent that?

  • I observed that you seemed to be looking for something on the screen, could you tell me more?

    • What do you think about the back button being there? is that the place you expected it to be? if not, then where?

If they are still stuck and are not able to proceed, then

  • this is the button to click

  • is it a bad design to have that look?

  • is that confusing for you?

  • Did you notice that back button on the top left? was that intuitive enough?

When they provide feedback good or bad,

  • That is great feedback. that is very helpful

When they provide feedback good or bad,

  • Well,… we designed it that way because….

(justification for a design)

Having a poker face on any task they do

Expressions like being surprised or happy or congratulating on being able to successfully complete or saying

  • oh wow, nobody else got it but you

  • that is pretty obvious, you should have figured that out by now
