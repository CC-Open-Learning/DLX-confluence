Synopsis of User Feedback - 10 Responses Total

  • Most respondents (8/10) had no trouble accessing the DLS.

    • One respondent had excessive load time, and two respondents indicated the sim did not respond to any input.

  • Most respondents (9/10) felt the introduction video provided the right level of information.

    • On the follow up question about the Intro video, one respondent suggested to add a statement mentioning that the stations need to be completed in order would be useful.

  • ALL respondents had some or a lot of trouble navigating around the auto-shop environment or got stuck somewhere.

    • Few respondents (3/10) indicated the movement was okay once they got the hang of it.

    • Few respondents (2/10) were dissatisfied about being “forced” to use the view menu

  • Few respondents (2/10) were unsure how to initially interact with certain click and drag objects in order to continue in the sim/complete the task.

    • Specifically, one respondent said the hoist arm interaction was confusing, and the other said the hydraulic floor jack wheels were confusing.

      • “I also found the floor jack area a bit tricky...not knowing that you could spin the wheels etc.”

      • “The hoist inspection for the legs were not clear on how to interact with them”

  • Few respondents (3/10) had issues that stopped them from doing what they wanted to do

    • One respondent could not inspect the welds on the floor jack stand and the vertical locks on the hydraulic host.

      • “I couldn't inspect the jack, I got hung up on that last task, it wouldn't let me inspect the welds. I couldn't complete the vertical lock check on the hoist and was permanently stuck, couldn't move forward”

    • One respondent could not select the fire pull in the tool area station behind the cluttered workbench.

      • “In the shop inspection at the tool bench, I was not able to access the fire pull and needed to move to the overview (Overhead door station) as the tools were in the way”

    • One respondent could not complete the floor jack station because the “continue” button does not do anything if all tasks are not complete.

      • “Could not complete the Hydraulic jack / jack stand tasks. All boxes (Checklist) were checked but the '"continue' button would not take me anywhere even though this was the final task. Tried this task 3 times with the same result. I felt it was hung-up on the "weld" on the axle stand. I could only click on one of the welds.”

    • One respondent had trouble with the parts washer label and floor jack weld line tasks and suggested adding hints for learners.

      • “I was not sure where I have to click on the weld lines and where should I exactly click when I'm reading the WHIMS label. For this situation, I think we can add hints”

  • Most respondents (9/10) felt the simulation accurately reflects the real-world experience of an auto shop.

  • Most respondents (9/10) felt the DLS helped them become familiar with safety, and potential hazards in an auto shop.

  • Most respondents (9/10) felt that the DLS is effective in helping them prepare for in-person learning at an auto shop.

  • Some respondents (4/10) indicated they had suggestions to improve the DLS. Details of their suggestions can be found below.

    • One respondent expressed concern about the good tool rest model on the bench grinder looking worn, but us showing it as normal.

      • “The bench grinder steady rest appears worn on the grinding wheel side. A typical steady rest has either a flat edge facing the wheel or a squared off relief where the grinder wheel fits into. I would be concerned with showing a worn items and saying it's normal.”

    • One respondent suggested adding additional magnifying glass options and reversing the hoist arm tooltips.

      • “On the hoist inspection, the front and rear arms are labelled opposite to what they are. The front arms should be facing the wall and the rear arms facing the interior of the room. The one piece that I found difficult to see was the frayed hoist cable, I couldn't get close enough to inspect (possibly a place for a magnifying glass option).”

    • One respondent wanted a button to move to the next station automatically after finishing all tasks at the current safety station.

      • “Once a station has been completed and the prompt reads "let's move to the xxxxx station", could a link to move there be embedded in the prompt rather than the student having to get there through the views menu.”

    • One respondent suggested adding more clarity to correct and incorrect answers in the inspection checklist as they thought the thumbs up and thumbs down symbols was feedback.

      • “I was a bit unclear if I had a right or wrong answer. The green thumbs up and red thumbs down I figured out were not indicative of a right or wrong answer.”

Plan of Action

Due to the difficulties learners had with navigating the auto-shop and completing certain outlier tasks like the weld line inspection, WHIMIS label inspection, and the weight rating label inspection, I am recommending to HOLD RELEASE. Most issues can be solved with fairly low-cost solutions like adding additional information to the intro video, and updating string tokens to offer more guidance to learners. However, there is one critical issue that throws an exception in the Daily Hoist Inspection scenario which is https://varlab-dev.atlassian.net/browse/MP-530.

Below is the a breakdown of work that needs to be done before functional testing can be done and approval for general release given. A micro sprint has been planned for October 28th - November 5th. Development will end on Thursday November 4th which will include all pull requests, builds and deploying to eConestoga for functional testing on Friday November 5th.
