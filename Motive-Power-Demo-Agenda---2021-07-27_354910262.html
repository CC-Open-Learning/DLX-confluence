Questions

As part of the demo meeting, we will need to get clarification on the following questions for the Hazards shown in the auto shop:

  1. Is there a particular chemical that should be shown in the spills throughout the shop (e.g. the parts washer chemical TCI 105)?
    We can show oil spills as hazards in the shop.

  2. Is the student required to clean up hazards?
    Tripping hazards and cluttered workbench should be moved out of the way into their appropriate locations. The important information for learning outcomes is identifying that an object is a hazard and knowing where it should be moved to in order to resolve the hazard.

    1. Tripping hazards: they could be moved out of the way, instead of completely removed

    2. Chemical spills: There’s a spill kit in this 360 image from the auto shop, do students use this? Or put a wet floor sign or other marker on top of them?
      Students would clean up spills using FloorDry or AbsorbAll, which are diatomaceous earth absorbants.

    3. Cluttered on workbench: move the clutter to the side instead of removing it? Or putting it back in its correct location?

  3. Should blocked doors be a hazard, as part of tripping hazards?
    It is a possibility for a tool cart or something with wheels to be left in front of an exit door. This would be a good example of a hazard to show.

  4. Should cords from the bench grinder or parts washer be a tripping hazard?
    Electrical cords from the bench grinder or parts washer would be tripping hazards if they are out in the working area. An air hose left on a table would also be a tripping hazard and would contribute to a cluttered work bench.

Demo Outcomes

A recording of the demo has been saved to Microsoft Stream and is accessible to all VARLab team members.

The action items below are notes that were taken during the demo based on discussions and feedback with Kevin. Items should be checked off when they have either been turned into a user story or been used to update an existing user story:

  • ABC fire extinguisher notepad needs to be updated wrt "nozzle" damage, to reflect the actual damage that is being shown on the extinguisher
  • Make the missing fire extinguisher in the side wall cabinet more obvious e.g. adding an empty bracket or make it possible to open the empty cabinet to see that there is no extinguisher
  • Consider adding an inspection tag on "good" extinguishers. Does not need to be legible, just present
  • “Good” extinguisher should show the needle in the pressure gauge in the green section
  • Notepad for parts washer was shown too soon, then not shown after the learner selected the Stuck state for the parts washer. This was resolved in MP-305
  • Damaged fire extinguisher needs to be missing part of the handle or hose - not just scuffed up or dirty
  • Many extinguishers will look exactly like the "damaged" one from the sim. Good job with the gauge being in the red, but more obvious damage to the handle would make it really visually distinct
  • When a learner encounters a tripping hazard, they can click to put it away.
  • When a learner encounters a spill, they can apply FloorDry or AbsorbAll to clean it up
  • Suggested air line connected to a tool that is left half-on, half-off the workbench is a great idea.
  • Students need to know not to rinse/wash spills down the drains. They need to apply an absorbent cleaning material on top and sweep it up.
  • Good suggestion for the black barrel near the parts washer would be bulk storage of the chemical for the parts washer, or just a simple "USED OIL" sign with the barrel leaking
  • Reminder to make something like a wheeled cabinet blocking the exit door on an oblique angle to the wall to make it more obvious that it doesn't belong
  • Students do not repair the bench grinder or parts washer if broken/malfunctioning, they flag the damage
