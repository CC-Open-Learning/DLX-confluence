VARLab : Facilitator Script 2024-01-29

Created by Germán Gaussmann, last modified by Sana Javeed on Apr 02, 2024

This script is for testing the CSI/Traffic DLXs in class with students of the program that would be using them in class.

Objective

To [simulate their first time encountering the DLX in class and identify any pain points in the experience, as well as successes we want to keep using in this type of DLXs]

Script bullet points

Click here to expand...

  • We introduce ourselves as the VARLab.

    • Explain what the VARLab does

      • Develop Digital Learning Experiences to be used in the classroom as a way to provide a safe space to experience situations that would be dangerous and or expensive to recreate in class

      • We provide every student with a front row seat allowing them to get the full experience first hand allowing them to explore the material at their own pace

      • Example: it would be very expensive to crash two cars/establish a crime scene for every student in the class so they can go and perform an analysis individually. Even doing it once it would mean that all the students would have to go over the same scene, potentially tampering with the evidence for other students or someone could be at the back of the group and not be able to inspect things properly missing crucial information for their report.

    • Who we are in the VARLab

      • While we are conducting the test, we are part of the User Research team. We should not mention that we are designers or involved in the development of the DLXs other than in a User Research capacity.

        • Removes a (sometimes subconscious) barrier that would prevent participants from giving negative feedback about the experience

        • Gives us a reason to not be able to help them when they are stuck: we need to see how students would do without anyone’s help when none of us are around to answer questions

          • We’re not leaving them without help, but our answers should not be about telling them how to get around the issues, but helping them figure it out on their own

            • “What did you expect to happen when you did [action]?”

            • “What do you need to move forward in this situation?”

            • “What have you tried that didn’t work as you expected?”

            • “If this wasn’t a simulation, how would you do this in real life?”

    • What is the objective of today’s testing session

      • What are the participants going to be doing

      • What are the outcomes for them

        • Reinforce that we are not evaluating them, we are evaluating the software they will be using

          • There is no pass/fail for them

          • If they get stuck, they can raise their hands and one of us will come over and see what they are having trouble with, but we won’t be able to tell them how to move forward

            • We should only be providing any help that the students would have in class, if there’s a document that they would have access to, we can ask things like “Have you checked on the [instructional document/manual]?” instead of telling them how to use the DLX

      • What are the outcomes for us (what is the purpose of this exercise)

        • We are facilitating the testing to gather information on

          • how our target audience (students from this program) interact with the DLX

          • any interactions that prevent the students from completing the experience

          • things that do not work in the way students expect them to work or do not reflect real-life interactions accurately to a degree where it’s difficult to understand the way they work in the DLX

    • What’s the timeline for today’s testing session

      • How much time will they spend working on the DLX

      • Filling out the survey form

      • Follow-up interviews/questions?

You’ll need headphones for this, if not mute and use subtitles

Script

Hi, my name is (first name) and I am part of the User Research team at the VARLab.

First things first: what is the VARLab and what do we do? VARLab stads for Virtual and Augmented Reality Lab, we are part of Conestoga College and are currently located at the Waterloo campus. The VARLab’s main purpose is developing these simulations for various programs in the College. These are meant to recreate situations that would be too dangerous, too expensive or counterproductive to set up in real life.

For example today we will be looking at a car crash scene and you will have to investigate the crash. Setting this up in real life where each one of you gets a different car crash would mean crashing about eighty cars against each other in a way that is realistic enough for you. I’m sure you can already figure out how this would be extremely dangerous and not very cost effective.

The other big point for these experiences is that each student gets a front row seat. You won’t have to worry about someone blocking your view if you are at the back of the class, or someone else tampering with your work. You get this experience just for you without anyone else getting in the way.

Moving on to what we will be doing today. If you haven’t done this already, I would ask you to go now to the material on your course and get it to start loading since it can take quite a while to do so.

A very important thing that we need you to have in mind through this whole exercise, is that you are not the ones being evaluated today. We are here to test this particular simulation with our intended audience, who are students like you. The one being evaluated is the simulation, to see if it provides enough information for you to be successful and find where the gaps are so we can fix them as best as we can.

For this we will need your help, if at any moment you get stuck, please raise your hand and one of us will come to you. We won’t be able to tell you exactly what you need to do to move forward, but we want to hear why you got stuck. If there was something you expected to happen but it didn’t, if something didn’t react you would expect it to in real life, if you feel like something is missing for you to be able to move forward, and so on. Like I mentioned at the start, we are part of the User Research team, which means we are not part of the team who designed or developed this simulation, [big smile] so don’t feel bad about bringing up things that don’t work, you won’t be hurting our feelings. This also means we aren’t experts on the specifics of how the software works, so we might not have the answers on how to move forward. But like we mentioned before, our work here is to look precisely at those issues and document them, so please let us know when you encounter them and we’ll come around.

We will be putting on the whiteboard the time at which we will be starting the testing as well as when it’s going to end. On the post-it notes next to you, you will find that there’s a number and some extra space underneath. The number is for us to be able to keep track of questions or comments you share with us while using the simulation in our notes. Anything you tell us will be recorded anonymously, but since you might speak with more than one of us today, we want to make sure we can cross-reference our notes later. In the extra space we would like you to record the time at which you started and ended each scenario you go through. This is just for us to get a rough average of how long it takes to complete them. Once the time for going through the scenarios is done, we want you to take some time to fill in a form with questions regarding your experience today, and that would be the last thing for today.

We will now play a video that will cover what we expect you to do with the simulation and after that we should be good to start. Thanks everyone for your time and please if you have any questions or need help at any point, raise your hand and we will come help you.

[Play video]

Alright everyone, it’s (current time), we have until (ending time) to go through the scenarios. We will let you know when there are 15 and 5 minutes left. You don’t need to finish all of the scenarios, do them at your own pace and don’t feel like you’re being rushed to finish when we give you the time warning!
