Open Q/A

Action Items

  • Ask for clarification on the exact ways that the vertical lock mechanism can malfunction. Currentl implementation of the malfunction keeps them in the “unlocked” position when they are pushed up by the lock ladder.
  • Provide confirmation to Kevin that the list of post-inspection scenarios will be “must complete,” so that students will experience all of the disaster scenarios.

Demo Outcomes

  • Overall very positive response to the appearance and realism of the hoist and all components. 

  • Failure of the height limit bar is very uncommon, Duane Cressman (Unlicensed) indicated that he could address frequency of failure as part of the inspection scenarios

  • Duane Cressman (Unlicensed) asked about potential defects of the vertical locks, and Kevin replied that it wasn't so much a failure of the vertical locks or hydraulic system, but there is the potential for the lock on one side to engage before the other one. It isn't something that needs to be factored into the daily checklist, but should be included (and is already noted) as one of the post-inspection scenarios.

  • Warped/twisted hoist arm is very rare, but a more common problem with hoist arm is failure of the telescoping function (jammed, rusty, salt), so that might be a good inspection point

  • Great response to audio, fits perfectly with inspection scenarios and adds value

  • During inspection of pads we need to zoom in so that it is more obvious if one is out of place

  • "Unhappy" outcomes of using the hoist without proper inspection of all required components will be must-see, not optional for the learner.

  • Decision was to definitely involve the learner in the post-inspection "unhappy" scenarios to ensure learner engagement. Car will be pre-loaded and ready to go, but the learner will be the one to actually operate the controls during the demo.

  • Suggestion to consider tying each unhappy outcome to the specific areas of the inspection that were not completed successfully by the learner. We will play with this idea a bit more and see what we come up with. Emphasis should be on cause and effect in a meaningful way, relevant to the learner's inspection results. [ Stacey Dineen (Unlicensed) comment: However, the 4 mandatory unhappy scenarios will still all need to play out for the learner, so we need to make sure that we show them, even for a learner who did perfectly on the inspection.]

  • Next steps include adding cars, suggestion was to be sure to include lots of different vehicles (tiny convertible, mini van, maxi van, RV, full-size loaded pickup truck, rusted-out "Canada Car", etc.) to let the learner experience different body types and weight distribution on the hoist.
