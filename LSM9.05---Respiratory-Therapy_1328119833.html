Type of Solution

Procedural - Flatscreen Web

PoV - First person

The objective of this DLX is to provide a virtual practice workspace for students to familiarize themselves with the equipment, supplies, and procedures that are essential to their success in bronchoscopy clinical practicum for the Respiratory Therapy program. The college does not own the type of bronchoscopy equipment (aka “bronch tower”) that students will need to use at their clinical placement, so we will provide a realistic digital twin for them to help prepare them for their in-person learning experiences.

This DLX must be accessible to students for asynchronous use over the web, so flatscreen web deployment is required. The DLX must be designed for use on the Respiratory Therapy Program device requirements hardware, as specified in Sept 2024.

  • PI has met with both DNA leads to review the overall project implementation plan. It is acknowledged that the timeline for this project remains very tight, and we must strictly control scope and risk if we are going to meet our delivery targets. At this time, the biggest areas of risk appear to be:

    • On-demand or unscheduled tasks. Depending on how we approach tasks like this, there is a risk of adding complexity through state management (especially if there are multiple state-dependent tasks) and scene continuity. This risk appears to be well controlled at the moment, we will keep an eye on it.

    • Cutscene animations that are intended to act like a live demonstration of the bronchoscopy and biopsy procedures. Cutscene segments would play in between user tasks, to maintain the storyline and enhance engagement, but these are known to be very time-consuming to produce and can introduce risk if they are not kept very linear. Everyone is aware that we need to strictly avoid forking the user path, and we can implement cutscene animations in priority order to ensure the best possible experience for the learner in the project timeframe that we have to work with. This risk appears to be well controlled at this time, we will continue to monitor.

21 Nov 2024

  • Procedural segments design work has begun, the plan is still that we will complete the Orientation segment by the end of F24 term (some placeholder assets will need to be replaced later when 3D models are available) so that we can move on to the 4 procedural segments:

    • Procedure Setup (select and prepare all of the equipment and supplies for the procedure)

    • Patient Prep (get the patient ready for the procedure)

    • Bronchoscopy Procedure (assist with the bronchoscopy and biopsy procedure)

    • Clean-up (disassemble and clean all of the equipment and supplies)

  • CAMERA: We have determined that the player (camera) will be stationary during all 4 procedural segments, so that there will be no user navigation (no need to walk around the space). Everything will be within reach. We are figuring out how to provide task-specific views in all 4 procedural segments so that the learner will have a default camera position that will optimize their chance of success. Pan & zoom will be available, but we will limit these functions to prevent the learner from accidentally looking away or being unable to complete tasks. The goal is to give them just enough camera control that they can customize their experience, without letting them get “lost”

  • TASK PROGRESSION: All tasks will be prompted by the DLX, not selected by the student. The student does not need to decide WHAT to do, they just need to make decisions about how to do the required task successfully. Task prompts could come in different formats, such as:

    • On screen text prompts in the UI or task list

    • Audio prompts (with or without descriptive text) such as verbal interactions with the physician or patient, or a sound effect from a monitor or instrument

    • Prompts may be more detailed for Beginner and less detailed for Advanced play modes

    • Procedure steps and task status will be shown in the Journal/List feature in the main toolbar, accessible at all times during game play.

  • FEEDBACK: Feedback is provided for each task (each decision) immediately. The user cannot proceed to the next task without correctly completing their current task. Feedback may be presented in different ways, including visual indicators or audio/sound effects. Feedback text may be more detailed for Beginner and less detailed for Advanced playmodes.

  • HELP: Help is task-specific or POI-specific and is always accessed on the main toolbar. Help is not shown unless the student clicks on the Help icon. Help could include visual resources such as diagrams or images, or text/audio resources such as guidance from the preceptor or physician. Help is always available, and it is identical for Beginner and Advanced players. The student can view the Help content whenever and as often as they want, and they will click to close the Help content before proceeding with their task.

  • DECISON-MAKING: This is not finalized, but we are making progress. At present, our design is leaning toward a UI-based decision process for many procedural steps, where the learner is offered several task options in a menu or selection UI and they can select the option they want. Final decisions are not yet made - this is the design that was shared as a starting point. Dev emphasized that we need a design that is simple, consistent, and repeatable for all procedure steps. This means that our “template” for decision making needs to include different types of decision scenarios, including:

    • Selecting items from a pre-defined set of objects

    • Selecting task actions such as location (e.g. where to connect an item) or preparation (how much medication to draw up)

    • Responding to input from the physician or patient (conversation style interactions)

  • TIMELINE: A preliminary timeline has been sketched out for the remaining segments of the RT project, and it is very tight indeed. To deliver all 4 segments on time, we will need to design an unprecedented degree of reuse, so that we are essentially replaying the exact same user experience (but with custom content) for each of the 4 segments. The goal is to ensure that all steps are covered, and all decisions get meaningful and accurate feedback. The inevitable tradeoff for this will be that we need to sacrifice some opportunities for richer engagement or immersion in the scene - the tasks will feel somewhat repetitive in terms of their format, but we are hoping that the content will be engaging that the student will not be bothered by the simple task structure. They will be focused on practicing the procedure and engaging with the physician, the patient, and the equipment!

This is just a rough sketch - definitely not a final timeline. Just to give a working copy of how we would need to divide our time across 2 terms to meet our target by the end of Spring 25.

20 Aug 2024

First storyboarding meeting with Corey, Lisa, Nilupul, Sundar, and German

We still have some work to do in terms of marking the differences between Guided and Unguided workflows for the Procedural segments of the DLX, but we were able to identify a familiar workflow for the Equipment Orientation segment. Even though we know we won’t have all of the art assets that we need to finish this segment, we’ve decided to start our development effort with this segment because we are confident about how it should work. The general look and feel of the Equipment Orientation segment for RT will somewhat resemble the “Explore mode” workflow for AMB. Here are some notes about what we know so far:

ALL IN ONE MODULE - We previously thought that we would publish different segments of this DLX as individual WebGL modules, but Corey wants everything in one module with a big menu to allow students to browse through all available segments. Due to increased file size, this could result in slightly longer load times, but this is normal and we will do our best to optimize.

Equipment Orientation is only in Guided mode (there is no advanced version of this segment).

Setting: This can be done in an empty room for now.  We can find out later if the setup should take place in a supply area or at the patient bedside or a procedure room or what.

Camera/Navigation: First person POV, stationary camera.  No navigation, we only need pan & zoom for this segment. 

Character(s): No Patient or Physician NPCs are required for the equipment orientation. Mentor NPC can be voiceover at this stage, we can decide later if/how we want to represent that character in the scene.

  • There are no procedural steps (no tasks, no prompts, no feedback, no playtimer) required for this segment.

  • Progress is just an organized (expandable?) list of all items.  When the learner clicks on an item, that item gets a checkmark in the list. Segment is "complete" when all items have been clicked on at least once.  There is no limit to how many times a learner can explore the same POI or the same item, this is an unlimited segment.

  • Two main POI's for this scene are: Bronch Tower and Supplies.

  • For the Bronch Tower, the equipment will be front and centre in the scene, with hover highlight on the interactable parts and hover tooltips to display the name of the highlighted item.  

  • For the Supplies, we will use an expandable menu of some kind along with the Object Viewer.  Object Viewer is perfect for this interaction because it allows the learner to turn the 3D model over and look at the label as well as looking at the item itself. Every object in the menu will be labelled with its name, and will highlight on hover. When selected, the item will be launched in the Object Viewer to allow the learner to inspect it more closely, as well as displaying detailed information (name + other info, such as what it is used for). From a learning perspective, we chose NOT to put the supplies into the scene during the Orientation segment because we do not want students to accidentally memorize items by location (3rd drawer down on the left) instead of learning to recognize supplies using their label, appearance, and use. There are many options for how the supplies menu might be styled or laid out or organized, and we can decide that later.

  • The learner can click on each explorable item (part, button, port, whatever) to get detailed information (name + other info, such as what it is used for. A "listen" icon of some kind will be available so that the learner can hear the item name read by the voiceover.  We may optionally include another "listen" icon for the other information, in case we want voiceover for that too.  For now, the info can go into a dialog similar to the one used in AMB - we don't know exactly how this info will be styled in the finished scene yet, but we can decide later. The AMB dialog can be a placeholder while we are getting started.

  • Save & Load will save progress checklist only in the Equipment Orientation segment. 

Objectives for Respiratory Therapy

The objectives for this DLX is defined in the Project Charter as follows:
The student will:

  1. Become familiar with proper equipment and procedures for their coursework and be able to review to be more competent and confident for their clinical placement   

  2. Develop some awareness of some of the affective aspects RTs might encounter during patient care 

  3. Apply their skillset independently and effectively to scenarios they are likely to encounter in clinical settings

  • Voiceover - on demand, as determined by design team

  • High-contrast and low contrast closed captions - We will always use optimal contrast for all UI text no need for user-facing controls to adjust contrast.

Assumptions / Known Constraints

  • Simulations developed under this plan are not intended to be played on tablets or cell phones.

  • Simulations developed on this charter are not guaranteed to have full functionality on devices that do not meet standard program device requirements.

  • Simulations developed under this charter are not intended to replace the teaching of the learning content, but rather augment it. They are intended to be part of formative learning experiences, not formal assessments. The simulation will not report any grade to the LMS, or provide any lasting record of the student’s performance outside of the current user session.

  • These simulations will not be designed to play with VR headsets or controllers.

  • These will not be multiplayer simulations (i.e. students playing in pairs or the professor having the opportunity to be in the simulation with the student at the same time).
