VARLab : Trucking: Wheels and Tires - Dev Considerations

Created by Stacey Dineen (Unlicensed), last modified on Apr 29, 2022

As we have developed the exterior inspection procedure for Release I and into Release II, problems and confusion have arisen with regard to the standard naming convention for similar components and standard inspection procedure (order of steps). This document will attempt to document those standards for future reference.

Naming Conventions for Tires

Having SO MANY tires has created confusion with how to identify them, so a standard naming convention has been agreed upon:

Examples:

DS Trailer Rear Brake chamber

PS Steer Rim

DS Tractor Front Inner Tread

PS Trailer Front Brake drum

Notice that we never refer to any of these wheel/tire inspectables with words lie left or right, first or second, etc. Those terms are relative to where you are standing, so we’ve eliminated them.

NOTE: In many issues and documents, you will see the term “Rear Tractor” used in place of “Tractor”. This was causing confusion, since the drive tires that are located at the rear of the tractor (8 tires in total) are also identified according to axle position, such that we would have things like DS Rear Tractor Front Inner Tire. The term “Rear Tractor” is popular, but it unnecessarily confusing for our project, so I’ve stopped using it. Tractor tires are just Tractor now, not Rear Tractor.

This naming convention can easily be extended to other inspectable elements, such as lights. The essential thing is to be consistent and use static reference points that do not change when the camera moves or if the GS is rearranged.

Example: DS Front Top Marker lights, DS Trailer Bottom Marker light, etc.

Standard Procedure for Inspecting Each Wheel

Having a standard approach for inspecting each tire (or each set of double tires) helps the student to remember all of the steps, by turning it into a repeatable pattern. This supports the learning objectives and makes it easier to identify problems.

  1. Tread (for double tires, both treads are inspected together)

  2. Debris (double sets of tires only, skip this step for Steer tires)

  3. Sidewall (outer tire only for double tires)

  4. Rim (outer tire only)

  5. Lug Nuts (outer tire only)

  6. Inflation (both tires together for double tires)

  7. Brake chamber and brake drum

  8. Air lines

  9. Axle seal (inner tire only for double tires)

Always inspect items in the order that they are encountered in the 360 walkaround:

We begin at the DS front and walk toward the DS rear, inspecting the tires in the order when we encounter them:

DS Steer Tire

DS Tractor Front Tires & Brakes

DS Tractor Rear Tires & Brakes

DS Trailer Front Tires & Brakes

DS Trailer Rear Tires & Brakes

Now we stop and inspect the back of the trailer, van doors, etc. Then we proceed, but this time we’re coming from the rear of the vehicle toward the front.

PS Trailer Rear Tires & Brakes

PS Trailer Front Tires & Brakes

PS Tractor Rear Tires & Brakes

PS Tractor Front Tires & Brakes

PS Steer Tire
