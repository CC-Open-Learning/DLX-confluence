Objectives for All Heavy Equipment Inspection DLS

The objective of this module is to allow Heavy Equipment student operators to inspect heavy equipment in a virtual setting, as a supplement for practicing with the real machinery. It’s expected that the students would have an understanding of the necessary steps of the pre-op inspection before beginning the simulation, so no new material needs to be presented by the simulation.

As a learning tool, the virtual inspection will focus on the following KEY learning objectives:

  • Gain familiarity with the scale and overall appearance of the equipment - this is done using a first-person view, at eye level for an average adult, including objects of known size such as traffic cones, chain link fence, barrels, etc. so that students can become familiar with the scale of the equipment. Heavy machinery can be intimidating for new users, so first-person views are essential to helping students gain confidence.

  • Learn best practices for inspecting heavy equipment - where to start, how to proceed in an efficient and logical manner that will be repeatable daily. Working in a familiar pattern helps to ensure that inspections are done correctly every time, maximizing worker and workplace safety benefits.

  • Landmark inspection tasks on the equipment - where to find the checklist items on the machinery. Workers will always have a checklist handy to make sure they know what to inspect, but this won’t help them find those components if they don’t know where to look.

  • Recognize good vs bad states for inspection tasks - learners need to know exactly what the equipment is supposed to look like, so that they can recognize problems. The DLS will show realistic examples of wear, fault, and damage so that the learner can confidently identify problems.

Assumptions / Known Constraints

  • It is expected that the students be familiar with the vehicle in question. We will not be explaining what the equipment is, what it is used for, or covering anything about operation of the equipment. The scope of this DLS is the daily pre-op inspection ONLY.

  • The 3D excavator model will be used mainly for landmarking purposes and show the learner where to go to find various access panels and inspection points on the equipment they will be using in-class. It is understood that there is some normal variation in heavy equipment models, even from one model year to another of the same manufacturer, and so it is not important that our model or mixed-media inspection interactions must be specific to one exact make/model of excavator. In all cases, the equipment shown in the DLS should represent commonly seen equipment in the field, but is not required to be an exact match to any particular equipment.

  • Because of the high degree of similarity between the Bulldozer and Excavator inspection sims, they will be designed ONCE to ensure a high level of continuity and conformity in the user experience. To avoid having to fix bugs twice, we will get the Bulldozer DLS to the point where it is nearly done (functionally complete, does not have to be perfect) and then copy the project and change out the models and visual media for the Excavator. This will ensure that the look & feel and user workflow will be consistent for both sims, and from there we can tailor the inspection tasks in Excavator as needed. Wherever possible, we will reuse the exact same inspection tasks, if they are applicable to both machines.

  • We are NOT PLANNING to use 3D models to visually present all parts/states of the equipment for inspection. This has proven to be exceedingly costly in previous projects, including the HE - Backhoe project. Our focus for the HE Bulldozer and Excavator projects will be on using any visual media formats available, including 2D photos, 360 photos, scanned models incl. photogrammetry, and purchased models, alone or in combination, to provide the learner with a rich visual experience of the heavy equipment under inspection. This list is not intended to be exhaustive, but rather to illustrate that a variety of different visual media options may be used in this sim, as opposed to our previous approach of using 3D models exclusively.

SAFETY

  • May include exterior equipment securement devices such as locks, pins, chocks that must be visually inspected and verified to be secure prior to starting the pre-op inspection

  • ROPS/FOPS chassis is intact and undamaged (cracks, dents)

CAT Inspection Checklist

Exterior

EXTERIOR

  • Bucket

    • Intact and undamaged (dents, cracks)

    • bucket teeth and cutting edges intact

  • Pins and bucket linkage - keeper bolts are in place, everything is lubricated

  • Bucket cylinder and linkage (damage, leakage)

  • Stick and stick cylinder (welds, damage, leakage)

  • Boom and boom cylinders (welds, damage, leakage)

  • Hydraulic lines - intact, no leakage

  • Boom foot (lubricated), top hat, and swing circle (no visible damage)

  • Undercarriage of machine (fluid leaks, damage, debris)

  • Steps and handholds (intact, unobstructed, secure)

  • Mirrors - intact and unobstructed

ENGINE COMPARTMENTS (there are several)

  • Batteries - terminals are tight and no excessive corrosion or leaks

  • DEF filler port - cap tight no leaks

  • Fuel filler port - intact, no leaking

  • Boom lube system - check for leaks in lines connections and piping

  • Swing motor oil level - dipstick

  • Hydraulic control valve - check for leaks or damage

  • Standard pattern change valve (IF PRESENT) which can change the machine configuration from “Standard ISO” to “Backhoe”. The correct position is “Standard ISO” - make sure this valve is intact, in proper position, and secured with the locking nut

This order is based on the level where these items are found in the reference videos, so the specific order of inspection tasks may change based on our equipment. Essentially, the inspections are done in order from the ground up, so that the operator inspects each component as he encounters that access panel or that equipment.

MAIN ENGINE COMPARTMENT

  • Overview of engine compartment (obvious signs of damage, leakage, or debris)

  • Coolant fluid level

  • Main engine oil level

  • Pump oil level (IF PRESENT) - this is on the checklist but is not in any of the videos I’ve seen.

  • Oil filters - primary and secondary (cracks, leakage, check dates)

  • Air filter restriction indicator (green,yellow, red)

  • Hydraulic oil tank (intact, leaks, level)

  • Hydraulic oil filter (intact, leaks, date)

  • Radiator (debris, fin damage, leaks)

  • Belts (tension, intact, no excessive wear)

  • Refer to O&M manual for specific fluids and levels

Operation & Maintenance Manual is specific for each model and model year, and includes specifics such as fluid types, how frequently to change/flush fluids, etc.

TRACKS

NOTE: This is the same inspection as bulldozer

  • DS and PS tracks are intact (no missing track pads, no missing bolts)

  • Tracks are properly tensioned (too loose looks like excessive sagging) - operator has to open a panel to tighten them up

  • Rollers are intact and not damaged or missing

  • Rock guards at the bottom of the track should be free of rocks or debris

  • Final and teeth should be undamaged and teeth need to be squared at the top (not overly “pointy”

Start from the idle (front end of track) and work your way back to the final (back end of track)

CAB INTERIOR - COLD

  • Operator seat intact, secure

  • Seat belt intact and functional

  • Visibility is good

    • Windows are intact and clear

    • Washer/wiper is functional

    • Mirrors or visibility aids (if any) are intact and clear

  • Levers for all controls are all loose, can be moved freely, nothing is jammed

  • Fire extinguisher present and functional

  • Turn key halfway and look for error messages or warning lights that stay lit

CAB INTERIOR - WARM

  • Start engine - make sure it starts

  • Hours meter - record hours

  • Fuel gauge - should be full

  • DEF gauge - should be full

  • Check for warning lights/alarms

  • Backup alarm - must sound

  • Horn - must sound

  • Heating and AC - functional

EXTERIOR - WARM

  • Exterior lights and beacons

Need to confirm exactly how these are checked.
