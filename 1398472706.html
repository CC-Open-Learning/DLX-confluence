User Tasks

Requirements

  1. Orientation

Familiarize with the bronch tower setup and understand the purpose of each component.

  1. Procedure Biopsy

This simulation guides students through essential biopsy procedure steps, focusing on learning and reinforcing both sequential and non-sequential actions.

Key procedures such as the bronch tower setup, patient preparation, biopsy, and post-procedure steps are designed to enhance recall of sequential actions.

Non-sequential tasks, like setting up the procedure table and emergency equipment, allow flexibility, enabling students to complete these actions in any order, while guided prompts and the usage of UI minimize errors in the scene.

Handbook

 

  • Learner can access the handbook anytime.

  • It is optional for the learner whether they want to refer to it or not.

  • The handbook may contain 2 types of information: the current progress (step indicator and list of tasks) and a library of content that a learner can refer to any point of time.

To avoid cognitive overload, the handbook will only show information wrt to the stage at which the learner is in (like setup, patient prep, biopsy and post-procedure).

To accommodate both beginner and advanced learners, the handbook option would provide freedom of choice that impacts adult learning.

MAIN ACTIONS IN THE GAME FOR BIOPSY PROCEDURE

  1.  Physician Requests Equipment:

    • RT retrieves equipment from the procedure table.

    • IMPORTANT: When the instruction is on the screen- they can look at the patient, the monitors, the procedure table, whatever they want.  It is up to them to respond to the prompt, they will look at whatever they need.

    • RT administers the equipment.

    • Cutscene transitions into animation for this action.

    • IMPORTANT: When animations take place, the camera view of the user is locked and they cannot access anything else

  2.  Biopsy Needle Progression: 

    • RT is shown the bronch monitor where RT uses game buttons to advance the needle. 

    • Action triggers motion of needle progression on the monitor.

    • IMPORTANT: Bronch monitor will show static images in response to trigger in-scene or through UI but no videos or dynamically moving.

    • IMPORTANT: When bronch monitor is being shown, the camera view of the user is locked and they cannot access anything else

  3. Providing Patient Vitals:

    • RT explicitly shares patient vitals status during the procedure.

 NOT IN THE GAME

  • Hand Movements: The precise movements of the RT's or physician's hands cannot be visualized.

  • Biopsy Needle Progression: The detailed motion of the biopsy needle into the patient by the RT through the bronchoscope cannot be represented 

  • No Dynamically moving or Video on the bronch monitor

  • Clippy Thing Usage: The RT cannot interact with or use the clippy mechanism at the end of the biopsy needle.

  • Chest Breathing Animation: The visual cue of the chest rising and falling during breathing cannot be depicted.

BRONCH MONITOR IMAGES

Still images on the monitor will not be animated but will need to be replaced/updated as the procedure progresses to support the narrative. Stages at which we will need the images to be updated on the monitor are:

  • Physician states we are in the right upper lobe.

  • When biopsy needle is advanced, forceps clamping the specimen in the lungs, needle being retracted

  • airway being irrigated with saline when administered

  • bleeding visible in the airway

  • bleeding stops when epi is administered

Procedure

Camera FOV

There are 6 POIs

  • Frontal view of the bronch tower+ patient view+ a part of procedure table+ bronchoscope view+ vitals monitor

  • Patient view

  • Bronchoscope view

  • vitals monitor view

  • Bronch tower monitor

  • Procedure table

Learner is oriented in the room such that they can view the POIs from the front view

Hovering and clicking on these assets will zoom the camera with a frontal and closer view of the vitals monitor/ bronch tower monitor/procedure table/ patient view/ bronchoscope view

The Learner can also access the views from the menu located at top of the screen. Clicking on the views will behave the same way as if they clicked on the POIs in the scene

Reason: To provide an immersive experience and orient them to the relative positioning of items in the field of practice

Learner performs sequential procedural steps

ACTION: Physician Requests Equipment:

  • If the learner makes wrong selections of supplies from the procedure table, they will be prompted that it is wrong. Hence they cannot proceed with a wrong decision

  • Learner can view handbook to know more.

Example:

  • Physician’s Prompt: "20cc of saline"

(stays on the screen)

  • Player selects wrong item <feedback sound>

  • wrong item <feedback sound>

  • Player selects correct item.

  • Prompt disappears.

  • Cutscene proceeds with correct action

  • Player clicks to hear their verbal response/prompt on screen to the physician

  • once the RT confirms

  • Journal gets updated

  • Physician’s next prompt

ACTION: Biopsy Needle Progression- With Bronch monitor controls

  • When the physician prompts the RT to get the biopsy needle, the RT picks it up from the procedure table to then place it on the bronchoscope.

  • In order to reduce complications of animations here, there is a cut-scene to the bronch tower monitor where the RT will perform actions through UI buttons reflecting on the Physician 's instructions - and the image on the bronch monitor reflects the action triggered.

The actions performed are

  • Up arrow (to retract the needle out the bronchoscope that is inserted into the patient’s mouth)

  • Open Forceps

  • Close Forceps (to grab the specimen)

  • Down arrow (Advance the needle into the bronchoscope that is inserted into the patient’s mouth)

ACTION: Providing Patient Vitals

Vitals monitor evaluation

  • RT should evaluate the vitals and decide to Proceed with the procedure or Pause the procedure, because that is a real life decision.

  • RT can recommend to either pause or proceed the procedure.

  • But to exit from the monitor, they must either choose to proceed or pause.

Patient’s breathing patterns

  • There are hotspots provided on the Patient’s chest. The RT hovers on the hotspots where the audio cues of the breathing patterns are heard.

  • The decision on whether it looks good or not - is the status provided by the RT by clicking on the buttons on the UI panel attached to the Monitor.

  • At all times the breathing will be good. Whether the RT decides to proceed or pause the procedure, the physician will always proceed

14 year old patient

Normal Vital signs:

Heart rate 60-100 beats per minute, Respiratory rate 12-18 breaths per minute, Blood pressure 100/70-120/80 mm Hg. SpO2 95%-100% end-tidal CO2 35-45 mmHg
