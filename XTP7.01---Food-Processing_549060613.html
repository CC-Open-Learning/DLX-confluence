    Project Scope

    • Students will be able to access and play the play the simulations directly from eConestoga on their laptops that meet program device requirements

    • The learning outcomes as listed above to be achieved through a series of tasks the student will navigate through

    • Students will navigate through the simulation either by using a mouse or by using identified keys on a keyboard.

    • Video instructions will be embedded in simulations demonstrating procedures

    • Interactive knowledge testing activities will be built into the simulation. Students will get feedback on what they completed correctly and where they made errors, and why their choices were correct or incorrect on completion of the simulation

    • Simulations will be SCORM wrapped (if an external build, the VARLab will work with the external vendor to ensure the simulation will work in our LMS)

    • A tutorial will be built into each simulation demonstrating how to navigate through the simulation and describing why the learning is meaningful for students

    • Short videos after milestone meetings (1-1.5 minutes) highlighting the progress and features of the simulations will be provided to Conestoga

    • For randomized scenarios, screenshots of the images and a brief description of the problem/task will be provided in the facilitator‚Äôs guide for the professor/technician to use for teaching purposes. These will be provided as jpgs/pngs as well so they could be used as needed in teaching. Videos used in the simulations will be provided as stand alone videos as well.

    ¬†Out of Scope

    • Simulations developed under this plan are not intended to be played on tablets or cell phones

    • Students who are using a mouse pad on their laptop (instead of the recommended mouse or keys) may not have ease of use moving through the simulation

    • Simulations developed on this charter are not guaranteed to have full functionality on devices that do not meet standard program device requirements

    • Simulations developed under this charter are not intended to replace teaching of the content but augment it. They are intended to be part of formative learning experiences, not formal assessments.

    • These simulations will not be designed to play with VR headsets or controllers

    • These will not be multiplayer simulations (i.e., students playing in pairs or the professor having the opportunity to be in the simulation with the student at the same time)
