Objectives for Uplift of Heavy Equipment - Backhoe

The objective of this uplift is to improve user experience and long-term maintainability of the HE-Backhoe DLX. All previous learning objectives and success metrics remain in place, refer to the original Heavy Equipment project workspace and charter for those details.

Timeline: The ideal GR target for the uplifted Backhoe DLX would be Sept 2023, in time for use in class in October 2023. This is a very tight timeline, and can certainly be renegotiated on a cost-benefit basis as we finalize the scope.

NOTE that none of the learning objectives, user tasks, or success metrics from the original release of HE - Backhoe are planned to be changed in this 1.1 release.

Intended Benefits for this Uplift Release:

  • Save & Load- Students and instructors have complained bitterly about having to start the backhoe inspection process all over again if they are interrupted before completing all objectives. The primary objective of this uplift project will be to implement at least some degree of save & load capability in the HE-Backhoe DLX. Ideally, we will be able to capture the specific inspection (vertex) that a student was working on when they exited the DLX, and restore them to that point in the DLX without losing any of their progress and results up to that point. Given the tight timeline for this project, we might need to compromise and instead define some logical “milestones” where progress will be saved, if it is too complex to track user progress right down the task.

  • Analytics - This release will include basic analytics such as total unique user count (total number of unique students to ever play the DLX, in any course, for all time). Other analytics may be achievable within the scope of this uplift project, such as statistics about instructional hours (total active minutes of all students, divided by the total number of unique students this could provide an average # of instructional minutes per student), but we have not committed to exactly which metrics are required.

  • Separate HE-Backhoe into its own repository - The original release of HE-Backhoe was built on COREv1 in a common repo that includes DLX projects such as Trucking and Hydraulics. For performance and maintenance purposes, it is important for us to separate HE-Backhoe into its own repository and sever any dependencies with other COREv1 projects.

  • General Bug Fixing - Like all DLX that have been in use for more than a year, various minor pain points and complaints have emerged. Through playtesting and review of user feedback, we will identify and resolve as many minor bugs as we can in this uplift release.

  • Performance and Optimization - We have learned a lot about optimizing our DLX for overall package size and load times. In this uplift, we will not miss the opportunity to optimize HE-Backhoe to improve load times and performance.

  • Stretch Goals - Instructors have requested features such as Completion Status (LMS shows Incomplete/In Progress status until the student reaches a defined milestone at the end of the DLX, then status is set to Complete in the LMS) and Exported Results (DLX offers the student the option of downloading their DLX results (or receiving them by email) at the end of the learning experience. This would need to be in the form of a document (probably a PDF) that the student could attach to an assignment in eConestoga to hand in. These features are highly sought after by faculty, but are not presently the focus of this uplift project. We can consider any of these valuable add-ons to scope, if time and resources permit.

Success metrics

Goal

Metric

Save & Load

Students will be able to save their progress, at least at a milestone level, so that they do not lose all of their progress if they need to exit the DLX prior to completing all tasks.

Analytics

VARLab senior leadership will be able to report the total number of unique users that launch the HE - Backhoe 1.1 DLX from eConestoga, regardless of which course or section they launch it from. Each student will be counted only once, instructors or other roles will not be counted at all. Additional metrics may be enabled as value-add

Performance & Optimization

Measurable improvements in load time will be achieved by reducing the overall package size and optimizing art assets and textures.

Assumptions / Known Constraints

  • No changes to the scope, learning objectives, or overall user experience from HE-Backhoe 1.0. There will be no new or deprecated inspections.

  • The 1.1 version will completely replace the previous 1.0 version, such that the 1.0 version will no longer be available or supported after 1.1 has been released.

  • We do not expect any artwork or art revisions in the 1.1 project scope. No art resources are assigned, other than to review assets and textures during the optimization phase of the project. At that time, if any art tickets are added to scope, we will discuss staffing options on an as-needed basis.
