VARLab : Adding Pre-Inspection Videos to Inspections

Created by Patrick Cho (Unlicensed), last modified on Dec 12, 2022

Introduction

This brief Confluence page will explain how you can add a video before an inspection. This could be useful in a variety of situations, such as showing a brief demonstration on how to do something (i.e. Oil Dipstick Check) before the inspection.

Preparation

First, you will need to get your video clip downloaded. If you don’t have a video clip, and only have a gif available, just use a free online tool such as https://ezgif.com/gif-to-mp4 to convert it. In most cases, the video size will be larger than we want it, so you want to encode the video using https://handbrake.fr/. This will significantly reduce the size of the video, which is important not only for reducing the size of our sim, but improving loading speed as well. Additionally, videos over 30mb are not able to be included in WebGL builds under our build configuration. Once your video is downloaded, name it descriptively and accurately. Next, add it to the Assets/StreamingAssets folder in the Heavy Equipment project. This is the required location that the code backend is looking for the videos when passed a string filename.

Implementation

The way you can add one of these videos is quite simple, you just have to fill in two fields in the Inspection script added to our objects that can be inspected. This example below is for the Oil Dipstick, which happens to be a 2D inspection, but these fields will exist on any inspection type. The pre inspection video title field is for the text that will show at the top of the screen.

IMPORTANT: Always remember to add the file extension to the URL field. Otherwise, it won’t be successful in finding the video. Additionally, the term “URL” is a bit confusing, as you shouldn’t put an actual URL link in here, but rather the video name with extension. This is just what Unity’s video player refers to that field as.

As long as your video is properly placed in the appropriate location, and the URL matches correctly, the video should work without issues.

The “Proceed to Inspection” button will take you to the regular inspection window, and allow you to select the correct answer for the inspection question.
