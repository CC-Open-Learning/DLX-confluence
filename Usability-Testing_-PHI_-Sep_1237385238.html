VARLab : Usability Testing_ PHI_ Sep

Created by Sana Javeed, last modified on Oct 21, 2024

Testing Date: September 27, 2024

This report contains details for testing the Public Health Inspection DLX with 23 students in-person.

A survey was also shared providing valuable insights into their experiences and perceptions of the simulation. This report aims to summarize and analyze the feedback, highlighting key areas of improvement and positive aspects.

Pre-conditions:

With Sept 18 being the milestone release date and the possibility of the students having introduced to the DLX on sept 20, the research goals and testing objectives would be towards understanding the usability of the DLX & application of the pre-brief and de-brief activities in class.

Positive

The PHI DLX got a 44 as the Net Promotor score, where we asked them if they would recommend this DLX to other students in the program. The 3 users who voted a 6/10 were the ones not comfortable with video games

Usage Frequency

Ranges from Sometimes to regularly with a higher probability of using it often.

  • Easy to navigate & user-friendly interface. [ONLY ONE USER SUGGESTED ASWD KEYS]

  • Loved the navigation perspective (yellow target points)- clear markers made it easy to navigate

  • Fun and engaging experience while identifying compliance/non-compliance issues.

  • Allows for replay to practice identifying infractions, especially for students.

  • Provides a realistic introduction to real-life public health inspection scenarios

  • Encourages critical thinking by requiring inspection of every detail.

  • Helps utilize student knowledge in a more applied setting.

  • High level of detail and interaction with the objects

  • Offers tools like probes and cameras for accurate inspections.

“I liked how I could replay it as many times as I wanted because its possible not to catch the infractions at once because I am a student and not a perfect licensed PHI yet. This allowed me to go back and forth to check if I have mentioned everything in my reports.”

“This game allowed me to get a broad feel of what I would actually be doing in the field, which is so far the closest I've gotten to the real thing.”

Research Goals and Objectives

Research Goals

Research Questions

Answers

Ease of use & Navigation (User Experience)

Find out how do students navigate using the DLX in order to complete tasks. How successful are they and how do they go about completing tasks?

Can the students navigate independently? Is it intuitive to move around and how do you find the speed of movement?

With what ease or difficulty are the students able to complete the levels in the DLX? Where do they get stuck?

Promotes learning (Instructional Design)

 Discover the aspects of design that support their learning achieved from the DLX

Are the students able to understand what is needed to be done to achieve the learning outcomes?

How would they use this resource? How would this help them with their learning?

What would be the frequency at which they would use this DLX? which parts of it are helpful?

Engagement Factor (Game Design)

 Understand how users stay engaged to complete an inspection while also determining the pain points encountered.

Do they understand on how to know when the inspections are complete?

Do they know how to review their inspections from the inspection log?

Instructional Design Support external to the DLX for instructors and students

How effective were the supporting materials external to the DLX in setting up students for a successful learning experience in the DLX ( so that the learning outcomes are achieved)
