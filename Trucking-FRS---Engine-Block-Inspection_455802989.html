TI-24 - Getting issue details... STATUS

Engine oil

As a student I want to inspect the engine oil level indicator and dipstick to determine the engine oil level.

User clicks to trigger the animated cut-scene that demonstrates the evaluation activity required to check the engine oil.

When triggered, the animation will show the engine oil dipstick being removed, wiped down with a rag, replaced in original position, and removed again. Camera view will zoom in to show the engine oil dipstick so that the learner can observe the level and characteristics of the engine oil.

If everything looks good, the learner will click “Next Inspection” to continue to the next inspectable or next POI.

User clicks on the model to open the Inspection UI and report a bad state.

On click: Object outline is highlighted

When the user clicks on the model, the Inspection UI opens to Engine Oil so that the user can record appropriate “bad” state in the inspection checklist.

Options: low

User is not required to record results if the Engine Oil is full. If the user does not click on the model to report damage, they may click the Next Inspection option to move on to the next inspectable or POI. The dipstick will be automatically returned to original position when the learner indicates that they have completed their inspection.

Engine oil dipstick is in original position.

Good: oil is honey-coloured to light brown in colour, level shows full.

Bad: oil is dark brown to black coloured, level shows low.

In checking the dipstick, student will need to:

  • wipe dirt off around the dipstick to avoid contaminants

  • pull out dipstick

  • wipe dipstick

  • reinsert dipstick

  • pull out dipstick and check oil level

Note that full oil will always look newer in this sim and low oil will always appear darker/older. It isn’t the colour that makes it a bad state, but we want to represent how oil colour changes over time.

Coolant

As a student I want to inspect the level and colour of the engine coolant so that I can learn how to maintain my vehicle in a safe condition.

User must locate and visually inspect the coolant reservoir.

If the student observes a problem that needs to be recorded on the checklist, they will click on the coolant tank.

On click, the coolant tank is highlighted.

When the user clicks on the model, the Inspection UI opens to Coolant so that the user can record appropriate “bad” state in the inspection checklist.

Options: low, discoloured, leaking

User is not required to record results if the Coolant is in good condition. If the user does not click on the model to report damage, they may click the Next Inspection option to move on to the next inspectable or POI.

Coolant tank is semi-transparent, such that the level and character of the fluid can be observed right through the plastic tank.

Leaking coolant could be caused by a poorly secured fill cap on the coolant tank or a cracked/damaged tank. Coolant would be visible on the outside of the tank and would be dripping.

Good: Full to indicator line, brightly coloured yellow, orange, or pink. Looks clean.

Bad: Level is low, coolant is dark and dirty looking, colour is very muted and dingy.

Leaking coolant would be on the outside of the tank and dripping.

Washer fluid

As a student I want to inspect the level of windshield washer fluid so that I can ensure safe visibility through the windshield on long trips.

User must locate and visually inspect the washer fluid reservoir.

If the student observes a problem that needs to be recorded on the checklist, they will click on the washer fluid tank.

On click, the washer fluid tank is highlighted.

When the user clicks on the model, the Inspection UI opens to Washer Fluid so that the user can record appropriate “bad” state in the inspection checklist.

Options: low, damaged

User is not required to record results if the Washer Fluid is in good condition. If the user does not click on the model to report damage, they may click the Next Inspection option to move on to the next inspectable or POI.

Only the ‘neck’ of the washer fluid reservoir is visible, it is semi-transparent, such that the level of the fluid can be observed right through the white plastic filler neck. Washer fluid is bright blue and transparent. Leaking fluid is evident by dripping.

Good: Full to indicator line.

Low: Level is low or empty.

Leaking: Washer fluid is dripping.

Damaged: Filler neck or cap is visibly cracked.

NOTE: The location of the washer fluid filler neck on our model is so low to the ground that I was concerned that a dripping leak would still not be seen by the user. After discussing with the artist, we will not model a dripping leak but will instead focus on inspecting the fluid level and identifying if there is visible damage.

Engine fan and fan belt

As a student I want to inspect the general condition of the engine fan and fan belt so that I can learn to maintain my vehicle in a safe operating condition.

User must locate and visually inspect the visible portions of of the engine fan - specifically the blades and belt.

If the student observes a problem that needs to be recorded on the checklist, they will click on the damaged components.

On click, the object is highlighted.

When the user clicks on the model, the Inspection UI opens to Fan & Fan Belt so that the user can record appropriate “bad” state in the inspection checklist.

Options: fan blades damaged, fan belt damaged

User is not required to record results if the belts are in good condition. If the user does not click on the model to report damage, they may click the Next Inspection option to move on to the next inspectable or POI.

Good: Fan blades and belt are intact, with no visible cracks or damage

Fan damaged: One or more blades on the engine fan are severely cracked, broken, or missing.

Fan belt damaged: Any portion of the visible belt appears cracked, frayed, or broken.

The fan can only be inspected while the engine is not running, both for safety reasons (it is dangerous to inspect a moving belt) but also for practical reasons (it is impossible to inspect the individual blades of the fan when it is running - it just looks like a blur)

0-1 problems with the wiring

Good: Wiring is intact, neatly bundled, with no visible cracks or fraying.

Damaged: Any portion of any wiring shows obvious cracks or fraying.

Loose: Any bunch of wiring appears loose and unbundled, such that it could become caught or tangled up.

Wiring can be located anywhere under the hood, and usually only small sections of it are visible. Wiring is most likely to fray or become damaged at flex or pinch points where the wiring has to bend (e.g. something opens and closes)

For air lines, one of the biggest clues to a problem is the hissing sound of air escaping after the truck has been turned off. This might be too difficult to include in this sim, but would be cool.
