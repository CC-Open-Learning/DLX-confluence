Synopsis of User Feedback - 8 Responses Total

  • MOST respondents (9/10) indicated that they had no problem accessing or launching the DLS.

    • One respondent indicated that they could not launch the sim on a Mac, but were successful when they moved to a Windows computer.

    • One respondent indicated that it took an excessively long time to load and windows were freezing.

  • MOST respondents (6/8) indicated that they felt confident using the simulation after completing the tutorial.

    • One respondent indicated that they could not open the tutorial at all.

    • One respondent indicated that they would like the opportunity to repeat the most recent direction given, rather than having to repeat the entire tutorial from the start.

    • One respondent pointed out that the correct response for the ERG number is a 3-digit number, but the user interface has space for up to 4 digits.

  • MOST respondents (7/8) indicated general satisfaction that the learning simulation was adequate to meet the learning objectives:

    • SOME respondents (3/8) indicated that they definitely provided a good opportunity to practice skills and gain confidence.

    • SOME respondents (4/8) indicated that they thought the DLS was okay for practicing skills and gaining confidence, but that it would need some changes to be really valuable. One suggestion was to have the tutorial provide hints or correct answer guidance if the learner could not get a correct answer (so that they could progress)

    • ONE respondent indicated that they could not successfully complete the tutorial (could not enter digits)

  • ALL respondents indicated that the clues given to identify hazardous materials in the simulation were realistic and appropriate.

  • Most respondents (6/8) indicated they had no problems moving around and seeing what they needed to see in the simulation

    • One respondent suggested that some objects were difficult to see clearly (dark label on a black barrel)

    • One respondent reported that they were unable to move and had to stand in one spot. This was likely confusion about using the mouse to move the camera but being required to use the keyboard to move around.

    • Two respondents had trouble using binoculars effectively - mouse sensitivity was very high

  • Overall, respondents indicated that they felt this sim would be very valuable to students, was very realistic, and would definitely help support learning about HazMat situational response.

    • “I think this is a fantastic idea and it will help students gain confidence doing Hazardous Materials incidents.”

    • “I thought it was very realistic and a fun, engaging way to test knowledge and see if we have a good understanding of the material”
