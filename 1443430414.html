Overview 

The Ambulance Orientation DLX was developed by CVRI (formerly VARLab) at Conestoga College from July 2023 until November 2024.  The purpose of the DLX is to help students orient themselves to the location of all materials in the ambulance including the various bags, compartments and cabinets. It is meant to be a 1:1 representation of the ideal stocking/locations for everything compared to the College’s real training ambulance.

The students are given two main play modes: Explore and “Seek & Find”. In Explore mode students can explore every nook and cranny of the ambulance to find 100% of the items. There is no time limit or optimal path. In Seek & Find mode students are challenged to complete a scenario where they locate all of the required equipment for a given simulated emergency. In Seek & Find there are multiple scenarios for each of the three semesters of the program as well as various difficulties including time limits.

This DLX does not provide any assessment capabilities, nor return any grades to the LMS. At most the students may compete against themselves for personal best scores in Intermediate and Advanced Seek & Find modes. 

DLX Objectives 

The primary objective of the Ambulance Orientation DLX is to provide students with unlimited access to the ambulance, the various bags that are used in class, and a majority of the equipment for the whole program. Students can use this in a number of ways when access to the real equipment is either heavily scheduled or unavailable (after hours).

The Content is divided into two types: 

Explore Mode: The objective is to find 100% of the items presented on the checklist. Hints are allowed and the students can locate the items in any order they wish with no time limits.

Seek & Find Scenarios:

  • Beginner: For the given scenario, students have unlimited time and unlimited hints to locate everything they need. Additionally a list is provided. There is no personal best, but students do collect “completed” status for each scenario they finish.

  • Intermediate: In intermediate mode the students still have unlimited hints and a checklist of the required items, but a count-down timer is also added. If the students complete the scenario before the timer expires they will earn both a “completed” status and a personal best time. If they complete the scenario faster in a subsequent time they will improve their personal best. If they fail to complete the scenario in a subsequent play they will NOT lose their “completed” status for that element.

  • Advanced: In this hardest mode there is a timer, hints are removed, and the list is converted into a shopping-cart style list where the students submit their proposed set of equipment and it is evaluated against the master list of items. They may play this mode as frequently as they want and they can memorize the lists from Beginner or Intermediate mode if they wish. In our play testing and focus groups this mode was mostly used for “speed runs” where players competed for bragging rights. In Advanced mode there are also alternative allowable items for some requirements. For example, Larger gauze can substitute in for smaller (but no vice versa), or the long spine board can be used instead of the power stretcher.

Known Issues 

At the time of General Release (November 25, 2024) for Ambulance Orientation 1.0, the following low-impact issues were known to exist.  For each issue, a workaround is provided, to assist users who may encounter these issues when using the DLX.

Description 

Workaround or Discussion

AMB-928

AMB-1023 

Some smaller items (such as the micron filters or zip ties) overlap other components when exploding from bags, pockets or compartments. This breaks the normal pattern for most items and can be confusing or unexpected to the player.

This is deliberate in all instances we are aware of.

In conjunction with our content experts it was determined that this compromise is in fact desirable to enhance contrast when seen against the environment in the background.

No change is planned or needed.

In user testing this was determined to be the “lesser of two evils”.

AMB-1026

Medication kit does not appear in the list for the ALS bag.

The original software design for the sim did not allow for bags within bags.

By the time we decided to put the med kit inside another bag it was technically impossible to rework the hierarchy.

The student can still complete all aspects of gameplay, and explore all items but the medication kit will never appear in the ALS bag checklist.

AMB-1038

Status popups can occasionally block interactable components.

If the user triggers a pop-up status message (such as “oops! You’ve already found [X]”) the pop-up will dismiss itself after (n) seconds. During that time it can occasionally block interactable items in areas such as the Med Kit.

To work around this the user has two options. They can wait for the message to disappear, or they can manipulate the camera angle to continue immediately.

At no point is the student blocked from proceeding permanently. It might be irritating if they are trying to achieve a new personal best.

UNIVERSAL

Playing in multiple windows or on different computers (desktop and laptop) at the same time will cause the process that saves progress to conflict and not act as intended.

DO NOT have the DLX open in two different tabs or computers at the same time.

LMS/Brightspace Issue

Brightspace reports an error “An error occurred running the Unity content on this page…”

This error is inconsistent and intermittent with no established pattern.

Users who experience this should try reopening the simulation, or clearing the cache in their browser. Alternatively, if the issue persists perhaps also consider a different browser entirely?

AMB-1020

When playing an advanced mode scenario where multiple alternative equipment options exist it’s possible to select multiple valid options in the “shopping cart” to fill a single need in the scenario. For example, the student can submit a list that includes both the scoop stretcher AND the long spine board.

Only the first valid version of an item added to the list will be marked “correct” after submission and any additional alternative items will be shown in the “incorrect” column.

This is the expected and designed behaviour.

Students who submit multiple overlapping items will have missed some other requirement that is needed to successfully complete the scenario.

Students are encouraged to think more critically about the item lists they submit in advanced mode, or go back and review the scenario in Beginner or Intermediate mode where the optimal list of equipment is provided.

Expected and designed.

POI Targets not clickable when inside a compartment.

If there is a back button in the top left of the screen, then the navigation targets to move around the scene are disabled.

This is the intended behaviour even though it requires the player to learn to use the back button.

AMB-1006

There are a small number of locations in the simulation that are abnormally dark or bright.

None of these minor issues impact the learner’s ability to complete the tasks and therefore the changes required to remove these artifacts were deemed not worthwhile.

Students are encouraged not to play this simulation in direct sunlight or with the screen brightness set to zero.

AMB-2024

The Hawiian shirt is 2D.

It’s just a sticker on the back wall, but commemorates Neil Freckleton’s vibrant contributions.

AMB-1025

AMB-1034

Objects may appear outside of the default viewable area.

When playing on a small screen or in a small window, some objects may appear either partially or completely outside of the default camera view. The player can still pan/tilt their camera to the left and right to successfully complete the work flow. This happens primarily in compartments with a lot of objects. Every effort has been made to minimize this drawback, but doing more would “clutter” the center of the screen for the majority of users.

AMB-1022

(Deliberately) missing “back” button to exit the patient bay.

Very early design decision. Changing it at this stage is effectively impossible.

This is the only instance where this was necessary.

AMB-4242

There are no snacks in the glove box

They got stale so we threw them out. Grocery run required.

UNIVERSAL

DLX cannot be played on mobile devices such as tablets or phones 

Use program-recommended Windows PC to play this DLX 

UNIVERSAL

If playing on a MAC computer, the Safari browser is not supported.

Please use Chrome or Firefox.

The Safari browser does not enable WebGL by default. WebGL is required for the 3D visualizations that this simulation is built on. The steps required to enable WebGL in Safari are beyond most student’s skills. Therefore Safari is explicitly not supported and any users who try it are doing so at their own risk.

UNIVERSAL

Saving progress while playing offline is not possible. 

Assuming the DLX is loaded and begun while connected to the internet, the user may continue to play and explore offline. However if the user does not re-establish network connectivity before closing the browser tab (and waiting at least 1 minute) then neither explore progress or seek & find completion will be saved.

UNIVERSAL

DLX is not available without login to eConestoga 

There is no workaround for this problem. User controls for eConestoga are managed by Central IT. Please open a ticket to gain access to eConestoga. 

LMS/Brightspace Issue

Intermittent pop-up error when attempting to go to full screen mode.

If the DLX is not configured to load in a new tab/window then pressing the back button (either on the mouse or in browser) can cause an error when returning to the DLX and going into full-screen.

This is extremely hard to reproduce and we do not believe this has anything to do with the DLX. It appears to be caused by a variance in how the SCORM package is configured in the course.

Workaround: Instructors should ensure that the DLX opens in a dedicated window. If the error still appears all progress appears to be saved and a simple reload corrects the issue for the student.

AMB-9324

Front left tire is under pressure.

Our virtual air compressor is broken, so we just need to accept it.

DLX CORE Analytics

Definition of generic “Complete” is ambiguous for this sim.

The DLX is considered “complete” when the student finds 100% of the items in explore mode, even if they do none of the scenarios. The SIM-specific analytics Power BI dashboard shows completion rates, playtime etc. for each individual scenario if needed.
