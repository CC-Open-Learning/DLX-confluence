VARLab : Guided Tutorial - User Experience Definition

Created by Stacey Dineen (Unlicensed), last modified on Mar 16, 2023

This page will define the user experience for the Tutorial inside Guided Inspection mode.

Launch

After selecting Guided Inspection from the main menu, the learner will be welcomed to the Guided Inspection. To access the Tutorial for Guided Inspection, the learner will click on the Tutorial button in the main toolbar. Only the Guided Tutorial can be accessed from inside the Guided Inspection (and only the Advanced Tutorial can be accessed from inside Advanced Inspection) - it would be too confusing to offer both tutorials in both play modes.

Initial View and Welcome

On initial load, the Tutorial Menu will be shown. The voiceover will welcome the student and prompt them to select a topic that they want to explore.:

How to Play - This will include short tutorial segments about how to navigate, how to report a problem, how to adjust the camera view using move, pan, and zoom, and how to understand & respond to feedback. These will be interactive tutorial segments similar to the old version of the tutorial, where the learner will be prompted to perform the action and then told “Good job” when they complete it successfully. This section could probably also include info about grading (not graded) and Save & Load, if we decide that is important.

Truck Overview - This will include basic information about the major parts of the truck (tractor/cab/truck vs trailer, coupling, etc.) This will include orientation vocabulary such as “driver’s side” and “passenger side” to help a brand new learner understand the terminology they need to orient themselves to the truck.

Inspection Walkthrough - This will offer a big pictorial menu of different inspection steps around the truck so that the student can see how to successfully perform each type of inspection.

The exact number of and length of walkthrough videos have not yet been determined, but an example is here for Wheel inspection.

Modular Design - Lots of very short pieces

The most important part of this Tutorial is that learners can drop in anytime for specific help with the specific thing they are working on. Our test data to date (using Release III in the F22 term) indicates that most students attempt the simulation without accessing the tutorial at all. Feedback was very clear in terms of requiring the tutorial to be concise and modular, allowing students to go straight to the help they need without having to play through lots of other content that is not of interest at that moment.

Walkthrough videos are extremely popular on YouTube for other video games, so we thought we would try to apply the concept here. Each walkthrough video will demonstrate a particular inspection (or type of inspection) from beginning to end, including voiceover narration that will direct the learner about what to look for, and how to report a problem, and how to understand feedback.

How To Play - Segmented Help for How to Complete In-Game Tasks

This segment closely resembles the older version of the tutorial, with step-by-step interactive learning about how to perform inspection tasks in the simulation. The main difference in the redesigned version is that users can select one specific task or topic that they need help with, instead of having to play through the whole thing every time.

The trick is breaking down the individual tasks in a way that they will each still make sense, without having to play any of the other clips. This is a risk of choosing a segmented approach and will require careful review.

Get a Better Look

This interactive tutorial segment should use a complex view where the user might need to pan & zoom in the inspection workflow, such as the Engine Compartment (either DS or PS) or the Trailer Connections. There should be no visible damage or problems in this tutorial segment - nothing to report.

  • Zoom the camera (Try it, good job!)

  • Pan the camera (Try it, good job!)

Looks good! Move to Next Inspection

  • Next Inspection button on the toolbar (highlight)

  • Tap the space bar on your keyboard (Try it, good job!)

Report a Problem

This interactive tutorial segment should use at least 2 different examples of POI’s where the user will report damage, with the second example being a POI with multiple components to inspect in a single inspection task.

Example 1 - Battery with electrical fault.

  • Click on the battery to report a problem. (Try it, good job!)

  • Read the options in the inspection panel. Click on the wrong answer - Corrosion (turns red, oh no!)

  • Click again on the wrong answer again to fix it (turns back to black, whew)

  • Click on the correct answer - Electrical fault (turns green, woohoo!)

  • Move to Next Inspection using keyboard, main toolbar, or tiny shortcut in top right corner of inspection panel

Example 2 - Cab Front License Plate and Fog Lights (both must be in bad state)

  • Click on the license plate to report a problem (Try it, good job!)

  • Select Flickering (turns green, good job!)

  • Try to move to Next Inspection using any of keyboard, toolbar, or shortcut button (Try Again dialog, oh no!)

  • Click Back on the Try Again dialog. Look around for another problem.

  • Select the Fog Light that is burned out. (Well done!)

  • Select Burned Out (turns green, good job!)

  • Move to Next Inspection

Learning Objectives and Grades

  • Voiceover only - no actions for the learner to do

  • Can include text (main points only) in text dialogs

  • Use a generic DSF view of the truck as a backdrop while the voiceover explains briefly:

    • The main objective of this learning experience is to practice and gain confidence with the pre-trip inspection procedure. Through repetition and practice, you will learn how to perform daily inspections, and learn to recognize common problems that need to be reported.

    • This learning experience is not graded. Your instructor will not see your inspection results, but they will be able to tell if you have completed the learning experience.
