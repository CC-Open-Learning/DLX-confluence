VARLab : Canadian Experience Interview Notes

Created by Hannah Schmitting (Unlicensed), last modified on Sept 09, 2021

Weather:

  • Dressing appropriately for winter weather.

o   Heavy duty coat

o   Gloves

o   Hat

o   Winter boots

  • Waiting until you reach Canada to purchase winter equipment or purchasing from Canadian vendors is worth it to ensure your equipment is designed for Canadian winters.

  • Freezing temperatures can easily lead to frostbite and/or hypothermia very quickly if you’re not prepared.

  • Black ice is dangerous and difficult to spot even if you know what you’re looking for, especially if it’s hidden under light snowfall.

Academic Integrity:

  • Academic integrity is not as strictly enforced in some countries as it is in Canada. Interviewed students reported that being reprimanded for plagiarism is extremely rare.

  • As a result, some students are surprised by the strict academic integrity policies or were unaware of the consequences of not maintaining academic integrity.

  • Some students felt they did not properly understand how Turnitin works or how strict it would be.

Currency:

  • Digitally managing currency is very prominent in Canada, but that isn’t always the case in other countries.

  • Loading money onto a card may be a new experience to some students (ONE Card).

Transportation Etiquette:

  • Unspoken bus rules include:

    • The turn-down seats at the front of the bus must be vacated if someone with a wheelchair or someone with a stroller boards so they can access that space. Folks with disabilities also have priority access to these seats.

    • Folks with wheelchairs board first and disembark first.

    • Jaywalking is extremely dangerous in Canada, especially in the winter months.
