Students have the opportunity to apply procedures for investigating a car crash and conducting an analysis on the crash scene to determine what charges (if any) should be laid against the driver(s) involved according to their knowledge and understanding of the Ontario Highway Traffic Act. This version adds environments, weather scenarios, avatar characters to interact with and take statements from, new models of cars , additonal possible traffic violations to consider and enhanced measurement capacities.

The "Traffic Investigation" simulation provides an immersive platform for students to delve into the procedural aspects of vehicular accident investigations. Set within a dynamic, interactive learning environment, this tool simulates various crash scenes, complete with diverse environmental conditions, vehicle models, and avatar characters for witness statements. As learners navigate through these scenarios, they are tasked with applying their understanding of the Ontario Highway Traffic Act to examine evidence, analyze crash dynamics, and determine appropriate legal actions. The simulation's design mirrors the intricacies of real-life traffic investigations, offering a comprehensive duration that ensures a deep understanding of investigative protocols and legal frameworks. Through engaging with this virtual practice, students not only refine their analytical abilities but also acquire valuable procedural knowledge crucial for their future roles in law enforcement.
