Tutorial

The format of the Hydraulics simulation is that there will be a comprehensive interactive tutorial where the user will learn how to use the Virtual Lab. The student will perform the lab work in what is traditionally called “Explore Mode” - with no graph scenario (no tasks, no feedback). This gives the instructor and students total freedom to execute labs from their lab booklets, just as they would in the “real world” hydraulics lab on campus, but it places a much higher demand on the Tutorial. The Tutorial will be (almost) the only opportunity that the student has to get feedback regarding how to place, connect, and adjust the components they will use in their circuit. In co-operation with Content Expert Dr. Mirza Ahmed, the following steps have been agreed upon for the Tutorial:

  1. Open the drawer - Have the user open the drawer.

  2. Place the Pressure Gauge on the board - Click on the pressure gauge to place it on the board.

  3. Place a Flow Control Valve on the board - Click on the FCV to place it on the board.

  4. Close the Drawer - Close the drawer by clicking on it. This changes the camera view to the board.

  5. Move the Pressure Gauge - Click and drag the object around the board.

  6. Rotate the Pressure Gauge - While the Pressure Gauge is selected, the rotation buttons will show. Click the rotation buttons to rotate the object clockwise or counterclockwise.

  7. Add the Pressure Gauge to the Magnifier Panel - While the Pressure Gauge is selected, click on the Magnifier button in the toolbar. This will add the Pressure Gauge to the Magnifier view panel. (see Confluence page for notes A to help with implementation). By default, the Pressure Gauge is not locked in the Magnifier panel.

  8. Clear the Magnifier - While the Pressure Gauge is visible in the Magnifier panel, but is not locked, click on the Flow Control Valve. The Pressure Gauge will disappear from the Magnifier panel because it was not locked and the learner clicked on the board.

  9. Re-Add the Pressure Gauge to the Magnifier - While the Magnifier panel is still open, click on the Pressure Gauge. It will be re-added to the Magnifier panel.

  10. Lock the Magnifier View - Click on the lock icon in the Magnifier panel beside the Pressure Gauge to keep the Pressure Gauge in the Magnifier panel for future viewing. Click on the Flow Control Valve to demonstrate that the Pressure Gauge is not removed from the Magnifier panel when it is locked.

  11. Place More Components on the Board - Open the drawer using the View menu “Drawer” option and place the following components on the board by clicking each one with the mouse pointer: (another) Flow Control Valve, Directional Control Valve, Flow Meter. This will demonstrate that the drawer appears to contain only one of each component, but the learner can place that component multiple times on the board. There should be 5 components on the board, it is not important where they are placed or how they are arranged. Close the drawer to return to the Board view.

  12. Add the Flow Meter to the Magnifier panel and Lock it - Select the Flow Meter to add it to the Magnifier panel so that both the Pressure Gauge and the Flow Meter are displayed and locked in the Magnifier panel. This will teach the learner that they can magnify multiple things at once and that each magnifier view is locked or unlocked individually.

  13. Remove one of the Flow Control Valves - While the Flow Control Valve is selected, click on the X icon between the two rotation icons to remove the FCV from the board.

  14. Connect the Ports - Make a connection between all of the objects by clicking and holding on the port of one object and dragging the mouse to the port of another object. Release the mouse button on the second port to complete the connection.

  15. Remove the Connection - Click on one of the ports to remove the connection. We must remind the learner that the circuit needs to be complete, with no breaks, and must include all components on the board without skipping any. (notes B) We do not need to teach how to complete a circuit, we just need to remind the learner that the circuit needs to be complete and they must include all of the components.

  16. Connect a component to the HPU - Create connections to the Hydraulic power unit to complete the circuit. (notes C)

  17. Move the camera to the HPU - Click on the HPU to move to the camera to the HPU

  18. Adjust the Pressure Relief Valve on the HPU - Click and drag to turn the relief valve to an appropriate value

  19. Turn the HPU on - Click on the power switch once to turn on the HPU. (notes D) At this point, if the circuit is not complete, the HPU will not power up and the learner will see a toast message telling them that one of their components is not connected properly. The learner may need to modify the hydraulic connections or rearrange the components to get a complete circuit before they are able to complete this task. We need to supply supporting tips and general info that will help them succeed if they get stuck in this spot.

  20. Take a Pressure Reading - Observe the Magnifier panel to see how the Pressure Gauge and Flow Meter have changed. It does not matter what the actual readings are, only that the learner understands how to see them.

  21. Adjust one of the FCV to change the rate of flow - Click on any one of the FCV to select it. While selected, click and drag on the knob to twist it, this will open or close the valve. This will increase or decrease the rate of flow. Observe the Pressure Gauge and Flow Meter in the Magnifier panel to see if the readings change as the FCV is adjusted. This will depend on the relative position of the FCV to the other components, but the prompt will remind the learner to always observe the real-time readings in the Magnifier panel as they make changes in the circuit.

  22. Adjust the DCV to change the direction of flow- While the DCV is selected, click and drag the handle to move it back and forth. This changes the direction of flow. Observe the Pressure Gauge and Flow Meter in the Magnifier to see if the readings change when the DCV is adjusted.

  23. Try to Remove a Connection while the HPU is Running - Click to disconnect a component while the circuit is active and the HPU is running. This cannot be done. To make changes to the circuit, the PRV must be released and the HPU must be powered off. Toast message must inform the learner that the circuit cannot be changed while it is active.

  24. Turn off the HPU - Click on the HPU power switch to turn the HPU off. Observe the Magnifier panel to see that the Pressure Gauge and Flow Meter are at zero.

Please note that, for each of these tasks, the task management system will be used to track the student’s activity. They can only move on to the next task when the previous task is completed successfully.

Real World Application: Dock Leveller

Manually controlled by pushing a lever, thus extending a hydraulic single acting cylinder. When lever is moved in the opposite direction, the cylinder retracts. If lever is released, the cylinder will stop.

Supplemental Details

Functional Requirements & Qualities

Flow Control Valve (FCV)

  • Interactive knob

    • Clockwise and counterclockwise interactive rotation

    • Visual stopping point after certain amount rotation (Upper/Lower Bounds)

Pressure Relief Valve (PRV)

  • Same controls as Flow Control Valve, but different appearance - see reference materials.

  • PRV is used to set the maximum pressure for the circuit, as measured at the HPU

  • Unlike other valves, this is not used to control the rate of flow. This valve will do nothing unless the maximum pressure is exceeded, when it will automatically open and relieve the circuit pressure.

  • The valve must be adjusted to an appropriate value, based on the pressure limits of the lines and components used in a given circuit. This does not need to be taught in the sim, it will be covered in class.

Hydraulic Power Unit (HPU)

  • Interactive Pressure Relief Valve (PRV)

  • Pressure Gauge (for Lab #2)

  • Interactive power button

  • Audible feedback from system interaction

4/3 Directional Control Valve (DCV)

  • Handle that operates between three directions for user interaction

  • User can view the changing directions (movement of schematic block)

Pressure Gauge

  • True-to-life range of pressure gauge measurements

  • Animated measurement dial to display bar values

Single Acting Cylinder

  • Animation of cylinder movement for student observation

  • Audible feedback (extension/retraction) for student observation

Flow Rate Meter

  • True-to-life range of flow rate measurements

  • Animation of flow rate changes
